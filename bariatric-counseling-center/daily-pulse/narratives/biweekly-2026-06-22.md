# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-22

Over the 14 days ending June 20, the site averaged 22 sessions per day across 311 total sessions -- a functionally steady baseline with one meaningful signal: the second half of the window is running 10% lighter than the first, suggesting early momentum from the June 18 content batch has not yet offset a gradual soft patch. ### Volume + composition

The 14-day window produced 311 sessions, 251 users, and 1,244 pageviews -- averaging 22.2 sessions and 17.9 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks returned zero across the entire window, which is a data pipeline issue to flag rather than an actual organic collapse; the efficacy rollup for the last 30 days shows 24,891 attributed impressions and 37 clicks, confirming search visibility exists and is just not flowing into this window's aggregate correctly.

Channel composition landed at approximately 44% Direct, 42% Organic Search, 12% Referral, and a small slice of Organic Social and AI Assistant traffic. That is a notable departure from BCC's expected baseline of 60% Direct / 25% Organic Search. Organic Search is running nearly double its baseline share, which is a positive signal -- it suggests content is pulling search visitors that weren't arriving before. Direct is running well below baseline, which could mean email and social distribution have been lighter in this period, or that untagged campaigns that typically inflate Direct are simply absent.

Referral at 12% (36 sessions) is slightly elevated relative to the 10% baseline and is worth watching. With 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links including 5 edu links in the backlinks total, there may be a few specific referral sources driving consistent traffic. Knowing which domains are sending those 36 sessions would help prioritize outreach.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions dropped 10.4% from the first half (164 sessions, June 1-11) to the second half (147 sessions, June 14-20). Pageviews tell a sharper story: 736 in the first half versus 508 in the second, a 31% drop. The first half included a notable spike on June 3 (42 sessions, 230 pageviews) and another on June 11 (30 sessions, 178 pageviews) -- both flagged as anomalies above the 28-day median. Without those two days, the two halves would look closer to even. So this is a window where individual high-traffic days are doing a lot of work, and the underlying daily floor is relatively flat at around 18-22 sessions.

The second half shows a more consistent mid-range pattern (June 14-17 running 22-28 sessions) with a hard drop on June 20 (8 sessions, down 62% vs. median). June 20 is a Saturday, which likely explains most of that drop. The honest read here is that the window is steady-to-slightly-softening, not accelerating -- but the June 21 keyword position data showing "bariatric counseling" jumping from ~17 to ~10 suggests the June 18 content updates may not have had enough time to register in session data yet.

The self-esteem and hydration posts don't appear in the top launches data with impression counts, suggesting they may still be in early indexing.

That last number is the most important one to change. The "bariatric counseling" keyword moving from position 16.92 to 9.62 (per June 21 commit message) represents real progress toward a top-10 ranking and is the clearest win in this window. No new backlinks are recorded within the window itself; the 4 referring domains and 5 edu links appear to be existing links rather than new acquisitions.

### What this means + what to do next

1. Rewrite the title tag and meta description for the medications page (/understanding-weight-loss-medications-wegovy-ozempic-zepbound-and-mounjaro/) before next week is out. 2,178 impressions at 0 clicks in four days tells you the page is indexed and visible but the snippet is not earning the click. Test a title that names a specific question a person searching for Wegovy or Zepbound would actually type, and write a meta description that surfaces the page's perspective rather than just its topic.

2. Investigate the GSC data pipeline. The window aggregate shows zero impressions and zero clicks across all 14 days, while the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 24,891 attributed impressions. That discrepancy suggests a GSC property connection or date-range query issue in the aggregate pull. Resolving this is worth an hour of time because you cannot track content efficacy accurately without impression data at the daily level.

3. The "bariatric counseling" position improvement from ~17 to ~10 (June 20-21 commits) likely needs an internal linking push to carry it into the top 5. Pages ranking in positions 9-11 frequently stall there because they lack internal link equity from other pages on the site. A targeted internal linking pass -- identifying 5-8 posts that could naturally link to the main bariatric counseling service page -- is a realistic week-long project with a specific upside.

4. Audit the hydration and self-esteem posts from the June 18 batch. Neither appears in the top-launch impression data, which suggests they may not be indexed or are too new to register. Submitting them for indexing via GSC URL inspection is a quick action, but this trend briefing is the moment to flag that if they're still showing zero impressions by June 25, something beyond newness is likely the issue.

5. Consider whether Direct traffic's below-baseline share (44% vs. expected 60%) reflects reduced email or social distribution. If BCC has an email list or active social channels that haven't been activated recently, that is a low-cost session floor the site is currently missing. Organic Search picking up share is a good sign, but Direct traffic typically represents your highest-intent returning visitors -- and that pool appears quieter than usual.
