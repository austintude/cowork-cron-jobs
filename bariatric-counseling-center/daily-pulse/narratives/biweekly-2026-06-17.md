# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-17

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days from May 18 to June 15, the site held steady at roughly 24 sessions per day with a near-flat session trend (first half 166, second half 169, +1.8%), but pageviews accelerated meaningfully in the second half -- driven by two standout days (June 3 and June 11) that suggest content is generating deeper engagement when it does land. GSC data is entirely absent for this window, which is the most urgent infrastructure issue facing the site right now.

---

### Volume + composition

The 14-day window produced 335 sessions from 273 users, generating 1,269 pageviews. Per-day averages were 23.9 sessions and 19.5 users -- numbers that are modest but consistent with a site in early organic growth. Pageviews per session ran approximately 3.8, which is above a typical informational-content baseline and suggests people who do arrive are reading multiple pages.

Channel composition ran roughly 49% Direct (164 sessions), 37% Organic Search (123 sessions), 11% Referral (37 sessions), and 3% split between Organic Social and Unassigned. That Direct proportion is lower than BCC's expected ~60% baseline, and Organic Search is running higher than the ~25% baseline. That is directionally good news -- it suggests organic content is incrementally pulling its weight -- but the gap warrants watching. A Direct proportion that drops too far can signal that returning, loyal visitors are declining, since Direct traffic at a counseling center tends to reflect people who already know BCC and are actively considering or in treatment.

Referral at 37 sessions (11%) is roughly in line with the ~10% baseline. With only 4 referring domains and 12 total inbound links (5 of which are .edu), the referral ceiling is low. There is real headroom here if link-building accelerates.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions were essentially flat across the two halves (166 vs. 169, +1.8%), so raw volume is not growing in this window. That is an honest read: the site is not in a momentum surge. However, pageviews tell a more interesting story -- 527 in the first half versus 742 in the second half, a 40.8% jump. That divergence means roughly the same number of people arrived, but they read significantly more in the second half. The most likely explanation is the June 11 batch of sitemap-touched pages, which appears to have deepened the site's content density enough that visitors found more to engage with once they landed.

Session duration anomalies reinforce this. Multiple days in the second half showed average session durations 100-327% above the 28-day median, peaking at 555 seconds on June 10. Combined with the pageview lift, this suggests the binge-eating and bariatric content cluster is resonating with people who arrive -- they are staying and exploring. That is a quality signal even if volume is flat.

---

### Launches + efficacy

The most significant launch activity in this window was a batch of sitemap touches on June 11, covering ten pages: the binge-eating screening pre-surgery page, binge-eating in men, the BCC binge-eating IOP day-in-the-life, binge-eating disorder and Ozempic, binge-eating vs. emotional eating (clinical), the hydration and weight management piece, the bariatric therapist San Antonio landing page, therapy for depression and weight loss, food addiction treatment San Antonio, and the eating disorder IOP San Antonio page, plus the GLP-1 medication overview page. These are substantive pages -- several are direct service landing pages with clear local intent, and several target high-demand informational queries around eating disorders and GLP-1 medications.

The problem: every single one of these pages shows zero GSC impressions and zero GSC clicks. The pre-bariatric surgery binge-eating screening page has one GA4 session to its name. This is partly a timing issue -- Google typically takes 2-4 weeks to index and surface new or refreshed pages -- but the complete absence of GSC data across the entire 14-day window is a red flag that goes beyond normal crawl lag. Either GSC data is not flowing into the pipeline correctly, or these pages have not been crawled yet, or both. This needs to be resolved before any efficacy judgment on these launches is meaningful.

The 30-day rollup attributes 258 GA4 sessions to launched content and shows 24 pages launched and 76 pages modified, with 31 git commits. That volume of modification activity is solid. However, with zero ranked positions in the top 3 or top 10 per the rollup, the organic channel is running entirely on unranked long-tail impressions -- or the GSC connection is broken. Either scenario requires immediate attention. No new backlinks were recorded in this window specifically.

---

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC pipeline before anything else.** Zero impressions across 14 days for an active site with 123 organic search sessions is not credible data -- it points to a broken or disconnected GSC integration, not to actual search invisibility. Confirm the GSC property is verified, the data connector is pulling correctly, and that the date range is aligned. Until this is resolved, you cannot evaluate whether any content is ranking, and every efficacy report is incomplete.

2. **Submit the June 11 page batch for manual indexing via GSC's URL Inspection tool.** The ten sitemap-touched pages -- especially the service landing pages like `/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and `/food-addiction-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/` -- are the highest commercial-intent pages in the recent launch set. Requesting indexing now compresses the crawl timeline and gives you real impression data faster.

3. **Audit internal linking into the June 11 binge-eating cluster.** The session duration spikes and pageview lift in the second half suggest people who reach these pages are engaged. The gap is likely that not enough entry points exist to funnel visitors into the cluster. Spend next week mapping which high-traffic pages currently lack links to `/eating-disorders/binge-eating-vs-emotional-eating-clinical-differences/` and the IOP page, and add them.

4. **Investigate the Direct traffic drop relative to baseline.** BCC's baseline is ~60% Direct; this window ran at ~49%. That 11-point gap is worth understanding -- it could reflect a shift in how returning patients find the site, a lapse in newsletter or email sends, or simply normal variance. Pull a 90-day channel trend to see if this is a new pattern or noise.

5. **Build a backlink outreach target list around the .edu connection model.** Five of BCC's 12 inbound links are .edu, which is an unusually strong ratio for a local counseling practice. That suggests an existing relationship or content quality that academic sources find credible. Identify which institution(s) those links come from, then research whether UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or similar local institutions have resource pages or student health content where BCC could earn a second or third .edu link through a targeted pitch.
