# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-17

### The window in one sentence

The seven days of July 9--15 were a structurally mixed week: volume held near baseline (144 sessions, 20.6/day average) with a strong open on July 9--10 followed by a sharp collapse on July 12 that pulled the second half down. The big story is not this window's traffic -- it is the 19 page modifications that shipped July 16, all sitting at one day old, with early GSC signals on a handful of them worth watching closely.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 144 sessions, 129 users, and 387 pageviews across seven days. The per-day averages (20.6 sessions, 18.4 users, 55.3 pageviews) are roughly in line with BCC's recent baseline, so there is no dramatic story in the totals. GSC data returned zero impressions and zero clicks for the entire window -- this is almost certainly a GSC reporting delay or a data pipeline gap rather than a real signal, because individual page-level impressions in the launches data confirm Google is actively crawling and surfacing pages.

Channel composition this week was unusually balanced: Organic Search (54 sessions, 37.5%) and Direct (55 sessions, 38.2%) ran nearly even, with Referral contributing 17 sessions (11.8%), Organic Social 10 (6.9%), Email 5 (3.5%), and AI Assistant 3 (2.1%). BCC's expected baseline is roughly 60% Direct / 25% Organic Search -- so this window shows Direct running well below baseline and Organic Search running above it. That inversion is a mild positive signal: it suggests organic discovery is picking up relative to return visits or untracked internal traffic. The Referral number (11.8% vs. a ~10% baseline) is also slightly elevated and worth watching -- if a specific referring domain drove that, it may be worth identifying and cultivating.

The AI Assistant channel (3 sessions) is small but worth noting as a category to track going forward. That traffic comes from tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity surfacing BCC in responses, and it tends to grow as a site's authority and content depth increases.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is technically declining: sessions fell from 76 in the first half (July 9--11) to 68 in the second half (July 12--15), a drop of about 10.5%. That sounds concerning until you account for what happened on July 12 specifically -- sessions cratered to 6, a drop of roughly 69% versus the 28-day median. July 12 was a Sunday, and BCC's traffic patterns suggest weekends, particularly Sundays, run soft. The surrounding days (July 13--15) recovered immediately to 20--22 sessions each, which is squarely normal.

The more meaningful pattern is that July 9--10 were genuinely elevated days -- new users ran 58% and 125% above the 28-day median respectively on those dates. Session duration on July 10--11, however, dropped more than 72--80% below median, which suggests the spike in new users may have been low-intent visits (possibly from a social post or untagged link that brought in people who didn't stay). July 15 flipped that dynamic: session duration ran 124% above median with a normal session count, suggesting a smaller group of people who engaged deeply. That July 15 quality signal is the more encouraging pattern to watch.

### Launches + efficacy

All 19 page modifications in this window shipped on July 16, so they are one day old as of this briefing. That is too early for meaningful traffic attribution, but GSC impression data from the launches ledger gives an early read on which pages Google has already begun surfacing. Three pages stand out immediately.

The bariatric-surgery-counseling page (/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/) has 1,056 GSC impressions with zero clicks after one day. That is a strong crawl signal -- Google is showing it -- but the 0% CTR says the title or snippet is not compelling people to choose it over competing results. The bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation page (173 impressions, 0 clicks) has the same pattern. These two pages need title tag and meta description attention now, while they are fresh and Google is actively testing them.

The night-eating-syndrome page (/night-eating-syndrome-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/) is showing 537 impressions and a 0.9% CTR -- low but nonzero, which is a different situation. The ADHD-and-emotional-eating page (/adhd-and-emotional-eating-in-san-antonio-tx/) is showing 211 impressions at 2.8% CTR, and the GLP-1 weight regain page (/glp-1-weight-regain-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/) is at 204 impressions and 2.5% CTR. Those CTRs are respectable at day one and suggest the titles are doing their job. The remaining pages -- Mounjaro/Zepbound, Ozempic, Behavioral Support for GLP-1, Counseling for Anxiety and Weight -- are showing zero impressions at day one, which is normal and not a concern yet.

On the 30-day efficacy rollup: 375,143 GSC impressions and 1,459 clicks attributed across that window with 779 GA4 sessions is a reasonable foundation, but the absence of any top-3 or top-10 rankings in the data is a gap that the July 16 modifications are specifically trying to address. No new backlinks were earned in this window -- the current totals (4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, 5 edu links) are stable but thin for a site with this content volume.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the title tags and meta descriptions on the two zero-CTR bariatric pages this week.** /bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ and /bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation-in-san-antonio-tx/ are already getting impressions -- that means Google has crawled and surfaced them. A 0% CTR at this impression volume is an actionable signal, not a waiting game. Rewrite the meta title to be more specific and patient-facing, and write a meta description that names the outcome or addresses a real concern.

2. **Use the ADHD-eating and GLP-1-regain pages as your CTR benchmark for the cluster.** Those two pages are already converting impressions at 2.5--2.8% after one day. Look at what their titles do differently -- specificity, emotional language, clear value -- and apply the same logic to the underperforming pages in the same July 16 batch.

3. **Investigate the July 9--10 new-user spike and the referral channel elevation.** Something drove a 125% surge in new users on July 10 with very low session duration, and Referral ran above baseline all week. Identifying the source (a social share, a directory listing, an untagged email link) tells you whether it is repeatable or noise. Pull the Referral sessions in GA4 and check the source/medium breakdown.

4. **Build a backlink outreach target list around the edu-link base.** Five edu links from four referring domains is a meaningful foundation, but organic search authority at scale requires more. Identify which edu domains are already linking, confirm whether any of the July 16 pages are relevant to their audiences, and explore whether a second link from any of those institutions is realistic.

5. **Give the remaining zero-impression July 16 pages until July 23 before intervening.** Mounjaro/Zepbound, Ozempic, and the anxiety-and-weight pages are at zero impressions at day one -- that is not unusual. Check them again in the next 7-day rollup. If they are still at zero by day 10, submit them directly via GSC URL inspection to confirm indexing status.
