# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-09

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending July 7, sessions trended up 19.6% (first week vs second week), closing the period with a 32-session surge on July 7 that coincided with a large batch of page modifications pushing content across the eating disorder, bariatric, and GLP-1 support clusters. GSC data is completely absent for this window, which is the most pressing data integrity issue on the board right now.

---

### Volume + composition

The site logged 224 sessions, 200 users, and 968 pageviews over the 14-day window -- roughly 16 sessions per day and 69 pageviews per day on average. Those averages are dragged down by the holiday cluster (July 3-4 together produced only 17 sessions and 36 pageviews, which is expected) and a quiet June 28 that registered just 4 sessions. The June 29 spike to 30 sessions and 339 pageviews stands out as the single largest day in the window and deserves closer attention -- that volume of pageviews relative to sessions (11+ pages per session average for that day) suggests either a crawl event, an internal staff browse, or a very small number of users clicking through a large portion of the site.

Channel composition came in notably inverted from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led at 109 sessions (49%), nearly double its expected 25% share. Direct landed at 85 sessions (38%), well below the typical 60% baseline. Referral accounted for 19 sessions (8%) and Organic Social contributed 9 (4%). The Organic Search dominance is a real signal -- it suggests content is gaining search traction, not just repeat visitors coming back directly. The suppressed Direct number could mean the referral base is quieter than usual, or that some direct-intent traffic is now finding the site through search instead, which would actually be a healthy shift. There is no evidence in the channel data of a major untagged email or social blast, so the Direct dip looks structural rather than a tagging gap.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The first half (June 24-30) totaled 102 sessions and 557 pageviews. The second half (July 1-7) totaled 122 sessions and 411 pageviews -- a 19.6% session increase, but a 26% pageviews decrease. That combination is worth naming clearly: more people came in the second half, but they viewed fewer pages per visit. One explanation is that the holiday days (July 3-4) brought in casual or low-intent traffic that bounced quickly, while the July 7 surge (32 sessions, 130 pageviews, avg session duration up 42.6% vs the 28-day median) shows higher engagement on that specific day. The window is accelerating in raw session terms, which is good news, but the pageviews-per-session compression in the second half suggests engagement quality is mixed. July 7 is the most encouraging single day in the entire 14-day stretch -- 27 users, 23 of them new, session duration well above baseline -- and it landed the same day as a significant batch of page modifications, which is worth tracking forward.

### Launches + efficacy

July 7 was a high-output publication day. Nineteen page modifications went live, spanning three clear content clusters: eating disorders (/binge-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/, /emotional-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/, /eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/, /eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/, /eating-disorder-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/, /binge-eating-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/), bariatric services (/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/, /bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation-in-san-antonio-tx/, /bariatric-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/, /bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/), and GLP-1/medication support (/ozempic-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/, /foundayo-behavioral-support-san-antonio/). That is a substantial content push delivered in a single day.

It is only two days since those pages went live, so performance data is predictably thin. That said, two pages already have GSC impressions: /bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ is showing 698 impressions with zero clicks, and /emotional-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/ has 16 impressions with zero clicks. The bariatric counseling page getting 698 impressions in its first 48 hours is genuinely notable -- Google has clearly indexed it and it is appearing in results, but nothing is converting to a click yet. That is almost certainly a title tag or meta description issue, not a relevance problem. The Foundayo (orforglipron) page has already pulled 1 GA4 session, which makes sense given how little content exists anywhere for that specific medication name.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 154,471 GSC impressions attributed to recent launches, 611 clicks, and 633 GA4 sessions, with 1 page ranking in the top 3 and 2 in the top 10. That top-3 ranking is BCC's most valuable organic asset right now and should be identified and protected. No new backlinks were earned in this specific window, but the site currently holds 4 referring domains generating 12 inbound links, including 5 from .edu sources -- that is a disproportionately strong link profile for a local counseling center and suggests the existing outreach model is working.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the /bariatric-surgery-counseling/ snippet this week.** 698 impressions and zero clicks in 48 hours is the clearest optimization signal in this entire dataset. Rewrite the title tag and meta description for that page to lead with the most search-intent-aligned language you have. A 1-2% CTR improvement on that impression volume translates directly to sessions.

2. **Audit the July 7 page batch for internal linking.** Nineteen pages went live in a single day with almost no internal links pointing to them yet. Google can find them via sitemap, but users cannot navigate to them organically from existing high-traffic pages. Spend time this week mapping which existing pages should link into the eating disorder cluster and which should funnel into the bariatric services cluster.

3. **Investigate the June 29 pageview spike (339 pageviews / 30 sessions).** That ratio suggests something unusual -- a crawl, a staff session, or a referral source that drove one or two people to explore the site extensively. Identifying the source will either uncover a content win to replicate or a data quality issue to filter.

4. **Push for a second .edu backlink while the model is working.** Five .edu links from four referring domains is strong for this market. Whoever generated those links -- whether through a partnership, a resource listing, or a directory -- is a template worth repeating. One targeted outreach to a San Antonio-area university health or counseling program this week could compound a link profile that is already punching above its weight.

5. **Resolve the GSC data gap before next rollup.** Zero GSC impressions and clicks for the entire 14-day window is not a content performance issue -- it is a tracking or property configuration issue. If the Search Console property is disconnected, misconfigured, or pulling from the wrong domain variant, you are flying blind on organic search trends at the exact moment the site is publishing its largest content batches. Getting this fixed is a prerequisite for knowing whether the July 7 cluster is actually working.
