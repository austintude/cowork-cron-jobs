# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-16

### The window in one sentence

### Volume + composition

Total volume for the window: 323 sessions, 269 users, 1,268 pageviews, and 23.1 sessions per day on average. GSC impressions and clicks are reported as zero across every single day -- that is not a traffic result, it is a data pipeline problem and needs to be resolved before any meaningful organic search conclusions can be drawn.

That is a meaningful shift from BCC's expected baseline of 60% Direct / 25% Organic. The lower-than-expected Direct share (50% vs. 60% baseline) likely reflects the same data gap rather than a true behavioral change. Referral is tracking at baseline. No anomalous channel spikes suggest untagged email or social campaigns during this window.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions grew 4.4% from the first half (158 sessions across May 17--June 1) to the second half (165 sessions across June 2--June 14). That is technically positive momentum but barely above flat -- this is a slow drift up, not an acceleration. The more interesting signal is in pageviews: first half logged 541 pageviews while the second half logged 727, a 34% jump. That pageview-to-session ratio increase suggests people arriving in the second half are reading more pages per visit, which is consistent with the session duration anomalies that kept firing throughout June (May 31 at +207%, June 1 at +86%, June 3 at +133%, June 8 at +134%, June 9 at +143%, June 10 at +327%, June 11 at +101%). Visitors are spending significantly more time on site in the second half of this window. That pattern lines up with the June 11 content launch and suggests the new binge-eating pages are pulling people deeper into the site, not just generating single-page bounces.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the GSC data connection this week.** Zero impressions across 14 consecutive days is not a traffic result -- it is a broken pipeline. Until this is resolved, it is impossible to evaluate whether new pages are being indexed, whether rankings are improving, or whether the Organic Search lift (36% of sessions vs. a 25% baseline) is coming from existing pages ranking better or new pages entering the index. This is the prerequisite for everything else.

2. **Audit the June 3 traffic spike.** 42 sessions and 230 pageviews on June 3 -- more than double the baseline -- are not attributed to any launch in the ledger. Look at the Referral and Direct breakdowns for that day specifically. If it was a social share, an email send, or a mention somewhere, identifying the source tells you what's actually moving the needle for BCC beyond content production.

3. **Build internal links into the June 11 binge-eating cluster.** These pages are new enough that Google likely hasn't fully crawled them. Internal links from established, higher-authority pages on the site (the bariatric surgery prep section, the eating disorders hub if one exists) will help crawlers find them and reinforce topical relevance. The session duration data suggests visitors who do find these pages are staying -- the issue is discovery, not content quality.

4. **Identify and pursue a second .edu backlink.** BCC has 5 .edu links out of 12 total, which is an unusually strong ratio for a private clinical practice. That means at least one institution already sees BCC as a credible enough resource to link to. Research which school(s) those links come from, identify their relevant departments or resource pages, and make a direct outreach case for a second link -- whether to the binge-eating cluster, the GLP-1 medications page, or the bariatric therapist service page.

5. **Investigate the `/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/` modification from June 11.** This is a high-commercial-intent local page (someone searching that term is close to booking), and it was modified the same day as the cluster launch. Once GSC is restored, this page's impression and click trajectory should be one of the first things checked. If it's not ranking in the local 3-pack or top 10 organically within the next two to three weeks, it likely needs a structured data review and a title/meta-description pass.
