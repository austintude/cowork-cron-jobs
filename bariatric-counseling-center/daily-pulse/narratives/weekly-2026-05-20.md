# Weekly briefing -- 2026-05-20

### The window in one sentence

Over the three days tracked (May 17--19), sessions climbed steeply from 12 to 37 -- a near-tripling across the window -- with May 19 registering as a CRITICAL positive anomaly on every core GA4 metric. The data is too short and too volatile to call this a trend yet, but the directional signal on May 19 is worth watching closely.

### Volume + composition

Across the three-day window, the site recorded 73 sessions, 60 users, and 272 pageviews at a daily average of 24.3 sessions and 20.0 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks show zero across all three days, which is a data pipeline issue to flag -- Google Search Console typically carries a 2-4 day lag, but a flat zero across every day suggests the GSC integration may not be pulling correctly for this window.

Channel composition came in at 59% Direct (43 sessions), 34% Organic Search (25 sessions), 5% Referral (4 sessions), and 1% Unassigned. That Direct share is essentially in line with BCC's baseline of ~60%, which is unremarkable. The Organic Search share, however, is running about 9 points above the ~25% baseline -- 34% organic on a low-volume window could mean noise, but it could also reflect early traction from recently indexed pages. Worth watching if that share holds as volume recovers.

The Referral channel is quiet at 4 sessions total. Given BCC's 12 total inbound links across 4 referring domains (5 of which are .edu), there should be a steadier referral floor than this suggests -- low referral volume may indicate that linked pages aren't easily navigable from those sources, or that the .edu links are on low-traffic interior pages.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves comparison field returned empty, which is expected given the window is only three days. Reading the daily series directly: sessions went 12 -- 24 -- 37, and pageviews went 74 -- 56 -- 142. The session line is a clean acceleration. The pageview line dips on May 18 then more than doubles on May 19, which suggests May 18 traffic was shallower (lower pages-per-session) while May 19 brought more engaged visitors.

May 19 hit CRITICAL thresholds on sessions (+85% vs 28-day median), pageviews (+90.6%), users (+66.7%), and new users (+100%). Year-over-year comparisons for May 19 also read positive: sessions up 60.9%, pageviews up 35.2%, users up 42.9%. That YoY confirmation is meaningful -- it rules out the spike being a fluke of a weak comparison period. The concern is that May 17 was a legitimately soft day (-40% sessions vs 28-day median) and May 18 showed a CRITICAL drop in average session duration (44 seconds vs a 115-second median). The window opens weak and closes strong, which is the right shape, but three days is not enough to call this momentum rather than noise.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything shipped in this window is infrastructure and operational -- daily pulse commits, a GA4 data merge, a GSC data pull, and the internal operational dashboard (commit 591d051). None of these are public-facing content pages, so no direct SEO or traffic attribution is expected or appropriate to measure here. The two author page sitemap touches (/author/tmccord845/ and /author/austintude/) and the homepage modification (wp_id 2091, modified May 13) are also in the window, but all three show zero GSC impressions and zero attributed sessions. At 7 days post-touch, the author pages are too new to expect ranking signal -- Googlebot typically needs 10--21 days to re-crawl and reflect sitemap updates in Search Console.

Looking at the 30-day efficacy rollup: 11 pages launched, 2 modified, 8 pages ranking in the top 3, and 8 in the top 10 -- but GSC impressions attributed to launches are zero and GA4 sessions attributed are 49. That zero-impression figure is the most important number in this section. Eight pages at top-3 positions should be generating impressions. This strongly suggests a GSC data pipeline problem rather than a ranking problem -- the rankings exist (as confirmed by position data) but the impression counts aren't flowing into the attribution system. This needs a direct check in Search Console's own interface, not just the API pull.

No new backlinks were earned in this specific window. The existing backlink profile (4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, 5 .edu) is a meaningful foundation for a local medical practice -- the .edu links in particular carry authority weight -- but the count is small enough that a single new domain-level link would move the needle noticeably.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap before anything else.** Eight pages reportedly ranking top-3 with zero attributed impressions is a contradiction. Log into Search Console directly, pull the Performance report for the last 28 days filtered to the URLs of those 8 top-ranking pages, and compare what you see there to what the API is returning. If the console shows impressions that the API doesn't, the pipeline needs a fix -- all efficacy scoring downstream of this is unreliable until it's resolved.

2. **Treat May 19 as a hypothesis, not a conclusion.** The spike was real and the YoY lift confirms it wasn't a weak-baseline artifact -- but one day doesn't establish a trend. Pull the May 19 traffic by channel to see whether the lift was Organic (suggesting a ranking or CTR improvement) or Direct (suggesting an email send or social post that wasn't tracked). That source identification will tell you whether to expect it to repeat.

3. **Investigate the homepage modification (May 13) for measurable impact.** The homepage is typically BCC's highest-traffic single URL. A content or structural change on May 13 -- a week before the window -- could be contributing to the May 19 session behavior. Compare homepage sessions and bounce rate for the week before vs. after May 13 to see whether the edit is helping or hurting engagement.

4. **Plan a backlink outreach play for next week.** With 4 referring domains total and 5 .edu links already in place, there's proof that institutional and academic sources will link to BCC content. Identify 2--3 additional San Antonio--area .edu or health system domains where BCC content is relevant (UTHSCSA, CHRISTUS Health, UT Health San Antonio patient education pages), and map which existing BCC pages would be a natural fit for a link request. This is a week of research and relationship prep, not a cold pitch.

At the 21-day mark (around June 3), check both in Search Console to see if Googlebot has crawled and indexed them.
