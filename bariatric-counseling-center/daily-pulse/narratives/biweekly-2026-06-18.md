# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-18

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14-day period (May 19 -- June 16), the site averaged 24 sessions per day across 339 total sessions, with traffic momentum declining roughly 16% from the first half to the second -- though session depth (time on site) is running unusually high across the back half, suggesting content quality is landing even as raw reach has softened.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 339 sessions, 279 users, and 1,307 pageviews, averaging 24.2 sessions and 19.9 users per day. Those are modest absolute numbers, but they sit within a normal operating range for a local behavioral health practice in growth mode. GSC impressions and clicks are zeroed out across the entire window -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline or integration issue, not a real-world drop to zero organic search visibility, and it needs to be resolved before the next rollup or it will distort any trend comparison.

Channel composition was roughly 49% Direct, 37% Organic Search, 11% Referral, and 2% Organic Social. That is a meaningful shift from BCC's baseline of ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic Search. Organic Search punching up to 37% is the noteworthy signal here -- it suggests the content work is starting to pull in search-referred visitors at a higher rate than historical norms. Referral at 11% is also above the ~10% baseline, which is a reasonable sign given the backlink profile described below. Direct is lower than baseline, which may simply reflect that the practice has not run an email or social campaign that drives direct/untagged traffic in this window.

The 36 Referral sessions are worth watching. With only 4 referring domains in the backlink ledger, referral traffic is concentrated -- a small number of sources are doing meaningful work. That concentration is both an opportunity (double down on those relationships) and a fragility (losing one domain could move the referral line noticeably).

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions dropped 15.8% from the first half (184 sessions across May 19 -- June 3) to the second half (155 sessions across June 8 -- June 16). That is a real decline, not noise, and it should be named plainly: traffic volume contracted over the course of this window. The June 3 spike of 42 sessions heavily inflates the first half -- strip that day out and the halves are much closer. Still, the direction is slightly concerning heading into summer, when local service traffic often softens.

The countervailing signal is session duration. Average session duration fired CRITICAL anomalies on June 8, 9, 10, 11, 15, and 16 -- readings of 267, 277, 555, 261, 206, and 208 seconds respectively, against a 28-day median around 114-132 seconds. People who are reaching the site in the second half are spending roughly double the time per session. That pattern strongly suggests the new binge-eating and eating-disorder content (launched June 11) is resonating with the visitors it does reach -- they are reading, not bouncing. Fewer people are arriving, but the ones who do are genuinely engaging. That is a quality-over-quantity dynamic worth paying attention to.

### Launches + efficacy

The most substantive launches inside this window were seven sitemap-touched pages that appeared on June 11, alongside the daily pulse and SEO pulse git commits running from June 12 through June 17. The June 11 content cluster includes pages that are directly relevant to BCC's clinical positioning: `/eating-disorders/binge-eating-vs-emotional-eating-clinical-differences/`, `/self-improvement/mental-health/binge-eating-disorder-ozempic-what-nobody-tells-you/`, `/self-improvement/mental-health/day-inside-bcc-binge-eating-iop/`, `/self-improvement/mental-health/binge-eating-in-men-why-40-percent-of-cases-missed/`, and `/self-improvement/weight-loss-journey-self-improvement/pre-bariatric-surgery-binge-eating-screening/`. Two high-value local pages also touched the sitemap that day: `/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/` and `/therapy-for-depression-and-weight-loss/`.

These pages are 7 days old as of this briefing. At 7 days, GSC ranking data and impression volume should not be expected yet -- Google's index cycle for new content typically runs 2-6 weeks before position stabilizes. However, June 11 also happens to be the day sessions spiked to 30 (a MAJOR anomaly, +39.5% vs median) and pageviews hit 178 (a CRITICAL anomaly, +154.3% vs median). That correlation is not causal proof, but it is a reasonable hypothesis that the pages went live and were immediately crawled or internally linked in a way that generated real visits. Critically, the session duration anomalies starting June 8-9 and running through June 16 suggest the content already in place before June 11 was pulling in engaged visitors -- the binge-eating cluster likely accelerated a trend already forming.

The git commits captured in this window are all pulse/SEO report files and represent operational cadence, not net-new public content. They will not generate search impressions on their own. The 30-day rollup shows 24 pages launched and 73 pages modified across the broader period, with 258 GA4 sessions attributed -- that attribution number is meaningful context. Zero rankings in the top 3 or top 10 is consistent with a content program that is still in the indexing-and-aging phase. On backlinks: 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 .edu links is a genuinely strong signal for a local practice. No new backlinks are explicitly logged in this window's ledger, but the .edu presence is worth protecting and expanding.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose and fix the GSC data gap first.** Zero impressions and clicks across 14 consecutive days is not organic reality -- it is a tracking or authentication issue. Every strategic decision about content efficacy, ranking progress, and keyword targeting depends on GSC data being reliable. This is the highest-priority infrastructure task for the coming week.

2. **Run an internal-linking pass on the June 11 cluster before it ages further.** The binge-eating and eating-disorder pages are live and already appear to be pulling engaged traffic, but without GSC data it is impossible to confirm whether they are indexed and accumulating impressions. In the meantime, making sure those seven pages link to each other and to BCC's service pages (`/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/`, etc.) will help Google understand the cluster's structure and may lift CTR once rankings appear.

3. **Investigate the June 3 and June 11 traffic spikes for source.** Both days produced CRITICAL anomalies in sessions and pageviews. Neither spike has an obvious attributed cause in the launch ledger. Pulling the GA4 source/medium breakdown for those two days specifically would confirm whether a social post, an email, a referral link, or something else drove the volume -- knowing the source would let you replicate it intentionally.

4. **Develop a second page on the Ozempic + eating-disorder topic.** The `/binge-eating-disorder-ozempic-what-nobody-tells-you/` slug targets a high-intent, high-search-volume topic that directly intersects BCC's clinical work. Once GSC data is restored, watch that page's impression curve closely. If it indexes well, a companion page covering GLP-1 medications and binge eating recurrence in people who stop the medication would likely cluster well and capture adjacent queries.

5. **Explore a referral-building outreach to a second .edu domain.** BCC has 5 .edu links from what appears to be a small number of sources. Those are high-authority links that likely contribute to the Organic Search share running above baseline. Identifying one additional San Antonio-area university health program, nursing school, or dietetics department as a relationship target -- and pitching a resource link or guest contribution -- is a low-cost, high-leverage play that fits the content strategy already in motion.
