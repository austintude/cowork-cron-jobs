# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-09

### The window in one sentence

The week of July 1--7 was heavily shaped by the July 4th holiday trough, but the site recovered sharply by end of week -- July 7 posted 32 sessions and 130 pageviews, both well above baseline, suggesting the holiday drag masked an underlying upward move. The same day saw a major page-modification push (15+ pages updated July 7), which likely contributed to the late-week spike and sets up a meaningful indexing and ranking story for the coming two weeks.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the 7-day window, the site recorded 122 sessions, 111 users, and 411 pageviews. The per-day averages -- 17.4 sessions and 15.9 users -- land close to the 28-day median, but those averages are misleading given how lopsided the distribution was. July 3 and July 4 came in at 8 and 9 sessions respectively, both flagged as critical anomalies against the median. Without those two holiday-depressed days, the rest of the week averaged closer to 21 sessions per day.

GSC impressions and clicks both show zero across the entire window, which is a data gap to flag. This almost certainly reflects a reporting lag or a disconnected property rather than zero organic search activity -- the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 154,471 attributed impressions and 611 clicks, so the pipeline is producing GSC data. Worth confirming the GA4/GSC integration is pulling correctly for this window.

Channel composition is notably inverted from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led at 66 sessions (54%), while Direct came in at 49 sessions (40%). Normally Direct runs ~60% and Organic Search ~25%. This suggests either a genuine organic search lift -- possibly from earlier content work now maturing -- or that Direct traffic was suppressed during the holiday, and people who did visit came via search rather than direct navigation. Referral (5 sessions) and all other channels were minimal. The Organic Search dominance this week is worth watching: if it holds into next week when Direct normalizes, it would signal a real ranking improvement.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The first half (July 1--3) totaled 48 sessions and 161 pageviews. The second half (July 4--7) totaled 74 sessions and 250 pageviews -- a 54.2% session increase. On the surface that looks like strong acceleration, but the math is heavily influenced by July 3 (holiday eve) dragging down the first half and July 7 (32 sessions, the week's peak) lifting the second half. Strip out the holiday distortion and the trend looks more like a recovery curve than a true acceleration.

That said, July 7 is genuinely notable on its own terms. It posted 130 pageviews (up 120% vs the 28-day median), 23 new users (up 92%), and an average session duration of 203 seconds (up 43% vs median). That combination -- more people, more pages viewed, longer time on site -- is a quality signal, not just a volume bump. It coincides exactly with the July 7 page-modification batch, which suggests at least some visitors were finding and engaging with the updated content almost immediately.

That is a significant volume of content touching BCC's most commercially relevant keyword clusters in a single day.

At 2 days since launch, most of these pages show zero GSC impressions and zero GA4 sessions -- which is expected and not a concern yet. Two pages are already showing early indexing signals. /bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ has 698 GSC impressions in 2 days with 0 clicks -- that is a strong crawl signal and means Google is surfacing it for queries, but the title or snippet is not compelling enough to earn the click. /emotional-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/ has 16 impressions in the same window. The 698-impression showing on the bariatric counseling page this quickly is the most actionable data point in this entire briefing -- it is ranking and being shown, but the click-through rate is essentially zero.

The 30-day efficacy rollup adds important context: 154,471 impressions and 611 clicks are attributed to content work from the last 30 days, with 1 page ranking top-3 and 2 ranking top-10. That is real traction. The backlink picture is modest but credible -- 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and notably 5 .edu links. Edu links carry authority weight disproportionate to their count, so that number is worth protecting and building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit and rewrite the title tag and meta description for /bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ this week.** 698 impressions in 2 days with 0 clicks is not a visibility problem -- it is a CTR problem. The page is being shown; something about the snippet is not earning the click. Test a title that leads with the specific need ("Preparing for Bariatric Surgery? Counseling That Addresses the Whole Person") rather than a generic location identifier. This is the highest-leverage single edit available right now.

2. **Submit the full July 7 page batch for GSC indexing via the URL Inspection tool.** Fifteen pages modified on the same day creates an indexing queue. Do not wait for Googlebot to find them on its own schedule -- request indexing for each URL so the clock starts now on ranking data accumulation.

3. **Build an internal linking pass across the eating disorder and bariatric clusters.** The July 7 modifications touched pages that logically reference each other (e.g., /eating-disorder-treatment/ should link to /eating-disorder-iop/, /bariatric-therapy/ should link to /bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation/). With 15 pages in the same clusters now freshly updated, a systematic internal link audit this week would consolidate page authority and improve crawl depth before these pages are fully indexed.

4. **Investigate the GSC data gap for this window.** Seven straight days of zero impressions in the aggregate while the 30-day rollup shows 154,000+ impressions suggests a property connection issue or a date-range filter problem. Confirm the GSC property is correctly linked and pulling into the analytics stack before the July 7 pages start generating impression data -- otherwise you will be flying blind on their early performance.

5. **Pursue a second .edu backlink, using the existing 5 as proof of concept.** Five .edu links from 4 referring domains is a credible start. Identify which institution(s) those links came from and map out one adjacent outreach target -- a UTSA health sciences page, a UTHSCSA student resource list, or a local community college wellness directory. The model is working; the move is to replicate it deliberately rather than wait for the next link to arrive organically.
