# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-24

### The window in one sentence

The June 3--22 window delivered 315 sessions at a 22.5 sessions-per-day average, but the second half trended down 13.6% from the first -- pulled lower by a near-complete traffic collapse June 20--21, then rescued by an unusually strong June 22 that likely reflects same-day content activity rather than organic momentum. GSC data is absent for the full window, which limits conclusions about search performance and is the most pressing data gap heading into the coming week.

---

### Volume + composition

Over 14 days (June 3--22), the site recorded 315 sessions, 249 users, and 1,347 pageviews. The per-day average of 22.5 sessions sits close to the 28-day median (roughly 21 sessions), so the site is operating at baseline, not growing. There are no GSC impressions or clicks recorded for any day in this window -- either a data pipeline issue or a property-level problem that needs to be diagnosed before any SEO conclusions can be drawn.

Channel composition is worth noting. Organic Search (130 sessions, 41%) is running well above BCC's expected 25% baseline, while Direct (127 sessions, 40%) is below the expected 60%. Referral contributed 42 sessions (13%), also above baseline. The Organic Search overperformance is a positive signal -- it suggests content is pulling search traffic -- but the suppressed Direct share may indicate that email, social, or returning-user traffic is softer than usual. The 16 Unassigned sessions are worth tagging: that figure often points to improperly tagged campaigns or UTM gaps.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The first half (June 3--15) produced 169 sessions across seven measured days; the second half (June 16--22) produced 146 -- a 13.6% decline. That direction is concerning on its face, but the story is more specific than a simple drift. June 16--17 were genuinely strong days (28 and 26 sessions, with session duration and pageviews both running well above the 28-day median). Then June 20--21 collapsed hard -- 8 and 5 sessions respectively, with pageviews down 78--87% vs. median and average session duration dropping to under 9 seconds on both days. Those two days look like a technical or access event, not an audience event. June 22 then surged to 40 sessions and 223 pageviews (+230% vs. median), almost certainly related to the page-modify activity logged that same day for the Bariatric Therapist in San Antonio page. The underlying trend, stripped of those three anomalous days, is essentially flat at a low baseline.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything that shipped in this window launched on June 23 -- the final day -- so none of it falls within the June 3--22 measurement period. That means this window is evaluating pre-existing content, not fresh launches. The six post-modifies from June 23 (the binge eating cluster: "Binge Eating in Men," "Binge Eating Disorder and Ozempic," "A Day Inside BCC's Binge Eating IOP," "Pre-Bariatric Surgery Binge Eating Screening," "Emotional Eating vs. Disordered Eating," and the hydration page) plus the new Ozempic behavioral support post are all in their first 24 hours. It is too early to evaluate them.

The one exception worth flagging: the hydration page (/healthy-living-2/why-hydration-is-essential-for-weight-loss-and-management/) already shows 1,060 GSC impressions and 24 GA4 sessions at one day old. That is a meaningful early signal -- it suggests the page is being crawled quickly and may already be appearing in SERPs, though a 0.2% CTR on those impressions indicates the snippet is not compelling enough to drive clicks yet. That page deserves a title tag and meta description review this week.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 47,048 GSC impressions and 67 clicks attributed to tracked launches, with zero pages ranking in the top 3 or top 10. That gap -- high impressions, near-zero click share, no top-10 rankings -- is a consistent pattern that points to a CTR and ranking-depth problem across the content portfolio, not a volume problem. Also worth noting: the backlink profile sits at 4 referring domains and 12 total links, but 5 of those are from .edu sources, which is a meaningful quality signal. No new backlinks were earned in this specific window per the ledger.

The Bariatric Therapist in San Antonio page (/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/) was modified June 22 and likely drove the day's traffic spike -- that page is a high-intent local service page and warrants close tracking over the next 7--10 days to see whether the modification holds or improves its position.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap first.** Zero impressions across 14 consecutive days is not a performance result -- it is a reporting failure. Before drawing any SEO conclusions about whether content is working, confirm that the GSC property is correctly connected, that the verified domain matches production, and that date ranges are not offset. This is the single highest-leverage clarification task of the week.

2. **Run a CTR audit on pages with high impressions and low clicks.** The hydration page hit 1,060 impressions in one day at 0.2% CTR. The 30-day rollup shows 47,048 impressions yielding only 67 clicks across all tracked launches. The content is appearing in search -- it is just not being chosen. Pull the top 10 impression-generating pages in GSC, review their title tags and meta descriptions, and prioritize rewrites for any page generating more than 500 impressions at under 2% CTR.

3. **Investigate June 20--21 technically.** Session duration under 9 seconds on both days combined with session counts at roughly one-quarter of median is not a normal audience dip -- it suggests a possible site availability issue, a mis-firing GA4 tag, or a redirect loop that cleared up on its own. Check server logs or Cloudflare/hosting uptime records for that 48-hour window to rule out a recurring technical vulnerability.

4. **Build internal links into the binge eating cluster now, before indexing settles.** The six pages modified June 23 are in the indexing window. Internal links added now will be crawled with the first substantive re-crawl of those pages. Connect them to each other and to relevant service pages (particularly the Bariatric Therapist and any BED-adjacent program pages) so Google sees topical depth rather than isolated posts.

5. **Develop a second .edu outreach play.** Five of BCC's 12 inbound links are from .edu domains -- that is a disproportionately strong ratio and suggests an existing relationship or natural citation pattern worth building on. Identify which institution(s) are linking, look at what they cited, and pitch one additional .edu contact (university health centers, nutrition programs, counseling departments in San Antonio or South Texas) with content that complements what they already linked to.
