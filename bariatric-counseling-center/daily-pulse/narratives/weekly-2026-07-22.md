That elevated Referral share is worth watching: it suggests a partner page, directory listing, or external mention drove meaningful traffic this week. The AI Assistant channel contributed 2 sessions, which is a small but real signal worth tracking over time. As more people use AI tools to research bariatric and eating-disorder topics, that channel has potential to grow -- but two sessions is noise for now, not a trend.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining, and sharply. The first half (July 14-16) produced 69 sessions and 213 pageviews; the second half (July 17-20) produced 48 sessions and 166 pageviews -- a 30.4% drop in sessions. The second half also included Saturday July 19, which was nearly a dead day (2 sessions, 4 pageviews, both reading as CRITICAL anomalies against the 28-day median). That kind of Saturday floor is not unusual for a healthcare practice site, but the Friday-into-weekend falloff was steeper than normal.

What complicates the interpretation is that the first-half spike was itself anomalous -- July 16 ran 75% above the user median and 106% above the pageview median. So the second half isn't necessarily a decline from a healthy baseline; it may simply be a return to normal after an unusual mid-week bump. The more honest read is that the underlying daily average (~17 sessions) is roughly consistent with the 28-day median of ~19-20, and the shape of the week was "spike then drop" rather than a sustained build.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap first.** Seven consecutive days of zero impressions in the pipeline is not a content problem -- it is a measurement problem. Before drawing any conclusions about organic performance, confirm that the Search Console property is properly connected and that the data pull is hitting the correct date range. Every other strategic decision this week depends on having reliable GSC data.

2. **Set a 14-day checkpoint for the Binge Eating Disorder page.** It is the highest-priority organic asset launched this week. Around July 31, pull its first impression data and initial ranking position. If it shows impressions but low clicks, the next action is a title-tag and meta-description revision. If it shows no impressions at all, submit it for manual indexing via GSC.

3. **Investigate the elevated Referral traffic.** At 21% of sessions (25 total), Referral ran roughly double BCC's expected baseline this week. Identify which referring domain(s) drove those sessions -- if it is a health directory, a partner organization, or a media mention, that is a relationship worth nurturing explicitly. If it is an untagged internal tool or a shared link, it should be cleaned up so the attribution is accurate.

4. **Audit the `/eating-disorders/` cluster for internal linking.** Now that the Binge Eating Disorder page is live, it should be cross-linked from related pages in that cluster (the emotional eating page, any BED-adjacent content, the treatment and counseling pages). Internal linking at launch -- not weeks later -- is what helps Google understand the cluster structure and helps readers move deeper into the site.

5. **Build a content calendar plan around the ADHD-eating connection.** The ADHD page targets a keyword that sits at the intersection of mental health and eating behavior -- a space that is growing in search interest and directly relevant to BCC's trauma-informed positioning. If that page gains traction, a supporting cluster (ADHD and emotional eating, ADHD and binge eating, ADHD medication and appetite) would be a logical next investment. This is a hypothesis to validate once first-impression data is available, not a commitment yet.
