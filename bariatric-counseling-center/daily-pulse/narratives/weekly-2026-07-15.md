# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-15

### The window in one sentence

The week of July 7--13 delivered solid session volume above the 28-day median on most days, with Organic Search and Direct running nearly neck-and-neck -- an encouraging channel mix shift -- but session duration collapsed in the second half of the week, and zero GSC data is coming through, which makes it impossible to evaluate search performance with any confidence.

---

### Volume + composition

The 7-day window produced 167 sessions, 152 users, and 502 pageviews. That works out to roughly 23.9 sessions per day and 71.7 pageviews per day. On its face, those numbers are above the site's recent 28-day median (which sits around 19 sessions/day), so the week was genuinely busier than normal by that measure. However, GSC shows zero impressions and zero clicks across every single day in the window -- that is almost certainly a data pipeline issue, not a real signal. Do not read anything into "search performance" until GSC is confirmed to be reporting again.

Channel composition is worth paying attention to. Direct came in at 68 sessions (40.7%) and Organic Search at 67 sessions (40.1%) -- those two are essentially tied, which is a notable departure from BCC's baseline of ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic. Organic running that high relative to Direct suggests either a real ranking lift or -- more likely given the GSC blackout -- some sessions that would normally be attributed to search are leaking into Direct due to tracking gaps. Referral contributed 13 sessions (7.8%), Email added 7 (4.2%), and Organic Social brought 10 (6.0%). That social number is modestly above baseline and worth watching. The 2 sessions tagged as AI Assistant is a new channel worth noting -- not actionable yet, but worth tracking as AI-driven referral traffic develops.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is gently declining. The first half (July 7--9) produced 87 sessions across 3 days, averaging 29 sessions/day. The second half (July 10--13) produced 80 sessions across 4 days, averaging 20 sessions/day -- a measured drop of about 8% in total volume. That alone would be mildly concerning but not alarming. What is more concerning is the session duration data. July 7--8 showed session duration well above median (July 7 was up 42.6%). Starting July 10, session duration crashed -- down 72.8% on July 10, down 80.5% on July 11, and down 76.9% on July 12. July 12 itself was a full drop-off day (6 sessions, down 69.2% vs median), which was a Saturday. The duration collapse starting July 10 suggests something changed -- either a new traffic source sending low-engagement visitors, a page experience issue, or a tracking anomaly. This needs investigation before concluding the trend is simply a mid-week weekend pattern.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything that shipped in this window is operational infrastructure -- daily pulse commits, weekly SEO snapshots, marketing pulse logs, and one LinkedIn content log (Todd psych-eval turnaround, Sara food noise). None of these are public-facing pages that would generate search impressions or direct sessions. The ledger does capture one substantive SEO commit from July 9: internal links added, a near-me meta description applied on staging12, and a login blocker cleared. That last item -- the login blocker -- is worth flagging specifically. If any pages were accidentally gated or inaccessible to Googlebot prior to July 9, clearing it may explain why new user counts spiked (July 8: +116.7%, July 9: +58.3%, July 10: +125.0%) in the first half of the window and then the session quality metrics deteriorated as Google crawled freshly accessible pages at scale.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 207,947 GSC impressions and 880 clicks attributed across the broader content library, with 2 pages ranking in the top 10 and zero in the top 3. That's a real foundation -- the site is getting seen -- but click volume relative to impressions (880 clicks on nearly 208,000 impressions) is a click-through rate of roughly 0.4%, which is low. That gap between visibility and clicks is the single most actionable number in this rollup. Backlinks stand at 4 referring domains and 12 total inbound links, with 5 of those being .edu links, which are high-authority. No new backlinks appear to have been added in this specific window.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap before anything else.** Seven consecutive days of zero impressions is not a search performance story -- it's a reporting failure. Confirm whether the GSC property is verified, whether the date range in the API query is correct, and whether a site-level issue (like the login blocker cleared July 9) may have triggered a temporary indexing disruption. Until GSC is healthy, ranking and search trend analysis is guesswork.

2. **Investigate the session duration collapse that started July 10.** Duration dropped from well above median to 70--80% below median in a single day. Compare what changed on the site July 9--10: the internal linking work and meta description updates are candidates. It's also worth checking whether a specific page or landing destination is now sending people through quickly -- possibly a thin page that wasn't linked before but now is. High new-user counts plus low duration suggests new visitors arriving and leaving fast, not a returning audience deepening engagement.

3. **Address the click-through rate problem in the existing content library.** With 208,000 impressions and only 880 clicks over 30 days, visibility is not the constraint -- title tags and meta descriptions are likely underperforming. Audit the top 20 pages by impression count in GSC and rewrite their title tags and meta descriptions to be more specific, benefit-forward, and aligned with how people living in larger bodies actually phrase their questions.

4. **Build a formal tracking tag for the LinkedIn content being logged.** The July 13 commit documents LinkedIn content going out (Todd psych-eval turnaround, Sara food noise), but Organic Social only contributed 10 sessions this week. If LinkedIn is being used as a distribution channel, UTM parameters should be attached to any links shared so that social sessions are attributed correctly and not leaking into Direct or Unassigned.

5. **Prioritize getting at least one piece of content to a top-3 ranking.** The 30-day rollup shows 0 pages in the top 3 and only 2 in the top 10. The .edu backlinks already in place give BCC meaningful domain authority to work with. Identify which of those top-10 pages is closest to position 3--5, audit its content depth and internal linking, and treat it as the priority page for the coming week. A move from position 6 to position 3 on even one high-impression term would have more measurable impact than shipping several new pages right now.
