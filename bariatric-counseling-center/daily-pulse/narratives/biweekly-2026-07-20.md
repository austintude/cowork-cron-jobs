# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-20

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14-day window (July 5--18), the site averaged 21.5 sessions per day across 301 total sessions, but the second half of the window ran 25% below the first half -- a soft decline driven largely by a Saturday crash on July 12 and a Sunday exit on July 18. The bright spot is July 16, which spiked hard on every metric and coincides with a batch of page updates that suggests the GLP-1 and bariatric cluster refresh is starting to pull attention.

---

### Volume + composition

Total volume for the window: 301 sessions, 270 users, 925 pageviews, and 0 GSC impressions or clicks. That last number -- zero across all 14 days -- is a data pipeline issue, not a rankings collapse. GSC data almost certainly exists but is not flowing into this report. That needs to be resolved before the next rollup, because the 30-day efficacy data shows 385,200 attributed impressions and 1,529 attributed clicks, so the underlying organic presence is real. The averages (21.5 sessions per day, 19.3 users per day) are modest but consistent with a site that leans heavily on Direct and returning visitors.

Channel composition this window: Direct led at 122 sessions (40.5%), Organic Search followed at 116 (38.5%), Referral at 36 (12%), Email at 12 (4%), Organic Social at 10 (3.3%), Unassigned at 7 (2.3%), and AI Assistant at 4 (1.3%). Compared to BCC's baseline of roughly 60% Direct / 25% Organic Search / 10% Referral, Direct is running low and Organic Search is running notably high -- nearly 14 points above baseline. That is a good sign, not a bad one: it suggests content published in the prior weeks is generating search-driven discovery, not just repeat visits from known patients. Referral at 12% is also above baseline, which is worth watching. The AI Assistant channel showing 4 sessions is worth noting as a long-term trend to track -- that number will likely grow.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. The first half (July 5--11) logged 172 sessions; the second half (July 12--18) logged 129 -- a 25% drop. However, this decline is heavily distorted by two weekend-end days. July 12 (Saturday) was a critical anomaly at 6 sessions, roughly 70% below the 28-day median. July 18 (Friday, likely end-of-day data) hit only 8 sessions against a median of 20. Strip those two days and the second half looks much more competitive with the first.

What the second half actually shows, on its stronger days, is meaningful engagement. July 16 triggered five separate critical-level anomalies, all upward -- sessions up 52.6%, pageviews up 105.7%, new users up 60%, and session duration up 339.5% vs. the 28-day median. July 15 also flagged a 123.7% spike in session duration. Elevated session duration on those days suggests visitors were reading deeply, not bouncing. The pattern of flat low-weekend days plus strong mid-week spikes is probably structural to the audience (people researching care options during the workweek), not a sign of content problems.

---

### Launches + efficacy

The headline launch of the window is the Binge Eating Disorder post published July 17 at `/eating-disorders/binge-eating-disorder-the-symptoms-causes-and-effective-treatment-options/`. At 3 days old it has 2 GA4 sessions and zero GSC impressions -- both of those numbers are completely normal for this stage. Indexing warmup typically takes 1--2 weeks, so no action needed there yet. What does matter is that this page landed the same week as the July 16 site-wide engagement spike, and the `/category/eating-disorders/` category page (a sitemap-touch that refreshed the same day) picked up 71 GSC impressions in 4 days with 0 clicks. Zero clicks on 71 impressions is a CTR signal worth watching -- it likely means Google is surfacing the category page but the title or meta description is not compelling enough to earn the click.

The bigger story from July 16 is the batch of page modifications -- 10 service pages updated in a single day, spanning the GLP-1 cluster (Ozempic, Mounjaro/Zepbound, GLP-1 behavioral support, GLP-1 weight regain), the bariatric surgery cluster (counseling, psych eval, bariatric therapist), and the eating-related conditions cluster (anxiety and weight, ADHD and emotional eating, night eating syndrome). That is a significant content investment. The `/author/shamilton/` page, which surfaced as a sitemap-touch the same day, is already pulling 2,412 GSC impressions and 8 clicks -- a 0.3% CTR that is low but not alarming for an author archive page. The volume of impressions there is notable and suggests Google is associating that author with topical authority in this space.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 385,200 GSC impressions attributed to recent launches -- but zero pages currently ranking in the top 10. That gap between impressions and top-10 rankings is the central efficacy story right now. The content is visible; it is just not yet competitive enough in the SERPs to generate meaningful click volume. No new backlinks were earned in this specific window. The site currently has 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links total, with 5 edu links -- the edu links are disproportionately valuable and represent an outreach model worth repeating.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose and fix the GSC data pipeline before the next rollup.** Zero impressions across 14 days is a reporting gap, not a reality. The 30-day efficacy data proves GSC is tracking -- the daily pipeline is failing to pull it. Resolving this is the highest-priority infrastructure task of the coming week, because without that data the trend briefing cannot tell you whether Organic Search momentum is accelerating or stalling.

2. **Audit the `/category/eating-disorders/` title and meta description.** Seventy-one impressions in 4 days with zero clicks is a clear signal that something in the snippet is not earning the visit. Rewrite the title and description to speak to what someone actually types when they are trying to understand their relationship with food -- not a category label, but a useful destination.

3. **Build internal links from the 10 July 16 service pages into the new Binge Eating Disorder post.** The GLP-1 and bariatric pages just got refreshed and are likely seeing crawl attention. If you place contextually relevant links from those pages into the BED post now, you pass authority to it during indexing warmup rather than waiting for it to earn rankings on its own.

4. **Pursue a second edu-domain backlink using the same outreach model that earned the existing 5.** Five edu links out of 12 total inbound links is an unusually strong ratio for a clinical counseling practice. That signal matters to Google's quality assessment. Identify which institution or program produced those links and replicate the pitch -- a UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or UTHSCSA-adjacent contact is worth a direct outreach this week.

5. **Investigate the structural weekend dip to confirm it is audience behavior, not a technical issue.** July 12 and July 18 both triggered critical downward anomalies. Before treating the 25% week-over-week decline as a content problem, pull a channel breakdown for those two specific days. If Direct and Organic both drop proportionally, it is likely the audience (people researching care on workdays). If only one channel drops, it may point to a tracking or delivery issue worth addressing.
