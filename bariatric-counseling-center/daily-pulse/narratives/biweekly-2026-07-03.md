# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-03

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending July 1, BCC logged 232 sessions at an average of 16.6 per day, with Organic Search leading channel mix for the first time -- a meaningful shift from the usual Direct-heavy baseline. The window was bookended by two sharp traffic spikes (June 22 and June 29) separated by a multi-day trough that dragged overall momentum into a 20% decline, second half versus first.

---

### Volume + composition

The 14-day window produced 232 sessions, 196 users, and 1,107 pageviews. Per-day averages sit at 16.6 sessions and 14.0 users -- modest numbers, but within the range expected for a site of this size and stage. GSC impressions and clicks came back at zero for every day in the window, which is a data-pipeline issue worth confirming, not a sign that organic visibility has collapsed. The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 85,722 GSC impressions and 235 clicks attributed to launched content, so the underlying organic presence is real -- the daily GSC feed just isn't populating here.

Channel composition is the more interesting story. Organic Search led the window at 100 sessions (43% of total), which is well above BCC's baseline of roughly 25%. Direct came in at 83 sessions (36%), short of the expected 60%. Referral added 31 sessions (13%), Unassigned 24 (10%), and Organic Social 17 (7%). The inversion of Organic and Direct is notable: it likely reflects indexed content starting to earn consistent search-driven visits, which is the right direction. The Unassigned bucket (10%) warrants a closer look -- that volume often represents untagged email sends or social posts where UTM parameters were skipped.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. First-half sessions totaled 129; second-half sessions totaled 103 -- a 20.2% drop. Pageviews followed the same direction: 569 first half, 538 second half. That said, the decline is not a smooth slide. The second half contained the window's worst three-day stretch (June 26-28, where sessions fell 50-80% below the 28-day median) immediately followed by the window's single best day (June 29, +50% sessions and +406% pageviews versus median). The June 29 spike rescued what could have been a sharper drop.

The session-duration data from June 26-27 (62 and 6 seconds respectively, against a median of roughly 198 seconds) suggests a bot or crawler event rather than real human decline -- those numbers are too low to reflect genuine browsing behavior. Strip those two days out mentally and the picture is a flat-to-slightly-declining window with one strong outlier day at the end. That is not alarming, but it is not momentum either.

---

### Launches + efficacy

All launches captured in the window ledger are automated git commits -- daily pulse reports, SEO pulse reports, and marketing pulse reports authored by Daniel Bisett. None carry a public-facing URL in the ledger, and none show GSC impressions or GA4 sessions at this stage. These are operationally necessary commits, but they do not represent net-new indexable content. At the 30-day rollup level, the picture is more substantive: 12 pages launched, 61 pages modified, and 75 commits across 30 days, yielding 85,722 GSC impressions attributed to launched content, 235 clicks, and 320 GA4 sessions. One piece is ranking in the top 3; two are in the top 10. That is a thin conversion funnel from impressions to clicks (0.27% CTR across the attributed set), which suggests titles and meta descriptions may need sharpening more than the content itself does.

The June 29 traffic spike (339 pageviews in a single day, 993% above year-over-year for that date) is the most significant event in the window and has no launch tied to it in the ledger. That strongly suggests a social share, an email send, or a referral event that either wasn't tagged or isn't reflected in the launches ledger. Identifying the source of that spike should be a priority -- if it was a specific piece of content, that content is a proven performer and deserves more promotion.

On backlinks: BCC currently holds 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links total, with 5 of those links coming from .edu domains. No new backlinks were logged in this window. The .edu count is disproportionately strong relative to the overall link profile -- that is an asset worth building on.

---

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Track down the June 29 spike source.** Pull the GA4 traffic breakdown for that specific day and identify which pages drove the 339 pageviews and which channel delivered the 30 sessions. If it was a specific piece of content, that slug is your clearest evidence of what resonates -- it should be promoted again and cross-linked internally.

2. **Audit the GSC data pipeline.** Every single day in this window shows zero impressions and zero clicks in the daily feed, while the 30-day rollup shows 85,722 impressions. That gap means the daily trend data is blind to organic performance. Resolve the connection or query method before the next rollup so you can actually see which pieces are ranking day to day.

3. **Address the 0.27% CTR problem before launching more content.** With 85,722 impressions producing only 235 clicks, the content is getting seen in search results and being passed over. Spend next week auditing the title tags and meta descriptions on the two top-10 ranking pages and the one top-3 ranking page. Small copy changes at that level tend to move CTR faster than publishing new pages.

4. **Investigate and tag the Unassigned channel (24 sessions, 10% of total).** Sessions in this bucket are often untagged email or social campaigns. If BCC sent any emails or posted on social in this window without UTM parameters, add them retroactively where possible and establish a tagging convention going forward. Unassigned traffic is invisible traffic.

5. **Develop a second .edu backlink target.** BCC's link profile is small (4 referring domains) but the .edu concentration is legitimately strong. Research which institution is behind the 5 .edu links and identify a second university or health program in the San Antonio area to pitch -- that approach is already working and worth replicating deliberately.
