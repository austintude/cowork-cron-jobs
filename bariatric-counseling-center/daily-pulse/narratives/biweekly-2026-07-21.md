# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-21

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14-day window (July 6--19), BCC averaged 20.6 sessions per day across 289 total sessions, with the window closing on a significant downward slide -- second-half sessions were 24% lower than the first half, driven largely by near-zero traffic on July 18--19. The one meaningful content launch of the window, the binge eating disorder post published July 17, is too new to evaluate fairly but represents the most substantive SEO asset shipped in this period.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 289 sessions, 259 users, and 898 pageviews over 14 days. Per-day averages were 20.6 sessions and 18.5 users -- modest but consistent with BCC's current baseline on weekdays. GSC impressions and clicks both read zero across the entire window, which is almost certainly a data pipeline issue rather than a true signal; the 30-day efficacy rollup separately attributes 386,000 impressions and 1,546 clicks, confirming GSC data exists but isn't flowing into this window's aggregation. That gap needs to be confirmed before drawing any conclusions about search visibility.

Channel composition came in at roughly 41% Direct (119 sessions), 37% Organic Search (106), 13% Referral (37), 4% Email (12), 3% Organic Social (10), 2% AI Assistant (4), and 2% Unassigned (7). That's a notable deviation from BCC's expected baseline of ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic. Organic Search running at 37% -- well above the 25% baseline -- is the most interesting signal here. It suggests either a quiet ranking improvement on existing pages, residual lift from earlier content launches, or both. Direct running below its typical share likely means fewer email or social touches went out during this period. The Email channel at 12 sessions also reinforces that -- a fuller email push would likely pull Direct back toward 60% and lift overall session volume.

Referral at 13% (37 sessions) is above the expected ~10%, which is worth noting given the backlink profile (4 referring domains, 12 total inbound links, 5 of which are .edu). Those .edu links are high-authority and likely contributing to both Referral sessions and Organic Search lift over time.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. First-half sessions totaled 164 (July 6--12); second-half sessions totaled 125 (July 13--19) -- a 23.8% drop. Pageviews followed the same arc: 513 in the first half, 385 in the second. The anomaly data makes clear this isn't a gradual softening -- July 16--17 actually produced the strongest individual days of the window (29 and 24 sessions, respectively, with session duration on July 16 running 339% above the 28-day median), followed by a sharp drop on July 18 (8 sessions, -59% vs. median) and near-total collapse on July 19 (2 sessions, -90% vs. median).

The July 16--17 spike is interesting. It coincides directly with the publication and refinement of the binge eating disorder post and the batch of page modifications to the eating-disorder and GLP-1 cluster pages. Session duration spiking to 490 seconds on July 16 -- more than four times the median -- suggests that visitors who arrived were reading deeply, not bouncing. What followed on July 18--19 is almost certainly a weekend effect rather than a structural decline, but the pattern warrants watching into this coming week to confirm recovery.

No ranking data is available yet, and none should be expected. ### What this means + what to do next

1. **Confirm the GSC data gap before next rollup.** This window shows zero impressions across all 14 days while the 30-day rollup shows 386,000. That discrepancy suggests a pipeline configuration issue -- date ranges may be misaligned or the GSC connector is pulling differently than GA4. Resolving this is the highest-priority infrastructure item because it's currently making it impossible to evaluate search performance at the window level.

2. **Set up rank tracking for the binge eating disorder post now, not after it indexes.** The page is live and the clock is running. Identify 3--5 target keywords (starting with "binge eating disorder treatment San Antonio" and variations) and get them into whatever rank tracker BCC uses so you have a baseline position the moment the page enters SERPs. Waiting until impressions appear in GSC to start tracking means losing early-ranking data.

3. **Audit internal linking from the eating-disorder cluster to the new binge eating disorder post.** Five eating-disorder and behavioral support pages were modified on July 16. If those edits didn't include adding a link to the July 17 binge eating disorder post, that's a quick win -- internal links from topically related pages accelerate indexing and signal relevance to Google.

4. **Investigate what drove the July 16--17 engagement spike.** Session duration of 490 seconds on July 16 and 109 pageviews are anomalously strong. It's worth pulling the GA4 page-level data for those two days to identify which pages received that traffic. If it's the GLP-1 or eating-disorder service pages, that's evidence those July 16 modifications are working and should inform the next round of updates.

5. **Plan an email touch for this week.** Direct traffic is running 19 percentage points below baseline, and Email sent only 12 sessions over 14 days. A single well-timed email (the binge eating disorder post is a natural send) would likely lift both Direct and total sessions back toward baseline and give the new post its first meaningful traffic test.
