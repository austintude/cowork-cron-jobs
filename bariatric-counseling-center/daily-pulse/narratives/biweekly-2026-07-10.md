# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-10

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14-day window (June 25 -- July 8), sessions accelerated 30% from the first week to the second, driven by a cluster of page modifications on July 7 that appears to have triggered the two strongest traffic days of the entire period. GSC data is showing zero across the board, which is the most urgent unresolved issue this window exposed.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 237 total sessions, 213 users, and 991 pageviews across 14 days -- averaging 16.9 sessions and 15.2 users per day. Those averages are dragged down by the July 4th holiday weekend dip (July 3-5 each logged 8-14 sessions with session durations 60-87% below the 28-day median, consistent with a holiday pattern and not a content or technical problem). The underlying daily baseline outside that holiday stretch looks closer to 19-32 sessions, which is meaningfully above recent norms.

Channel composition this window is notably inverted from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led at 113 sessions (48% of total), versus the expected ~25%. Direct came in at 94 sessions (40%), close to but below the expected ~60% share. Referral contributed 20 sessions (8%), above baseline, and Email and Organic Social combined for just 9 sessions. The most important signal here: Organic Search nearly doubling its expected share while Direct stayed relatively contained suggests this is a genuine ranking or indexation gain pulling new visitors -- not a campaign or internal link blast inflating Direct numbers. That is worth paying attention to.

There is also a single AI Assistant session in the data. That channel is worth tracking going forward as AI-driven referrals become more common for healthcare and counseling queries.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is accelerating clearly. The first half (June 25 -- July 1) totaled 103 sessions; the second half (July 2 -- July 8) totaled 134 sessions -- a 30% increase half over half. That is a meaningful directional signal, not noise. Pageviews tell a slightly more complicated story: the first half produced 538 pageviews versus 453 in the second half, but that first-half number is heavily inflated by June 29 alone (339 pageviews in a single day, likely a crawl, a bot sweep, or a heavily internal-linking session -- worth investigating separately). Strip that outlier and the second half's pageview depth looks proportionally stronger.

The session duration anomalies on July 3-5 are almost certainly holiday-related and should not be read as engagement decay. The July 7-8 recovery -- 32 and 31 sessions respectively, with July 7 also logging a 42% above-median session duration -- suggests the page modifications shipped July 7 landed with real visitors who stayed and read. That is the kind of signal that matters.

### Launches + efficacy

The headline action in this window was a batch of page modifications on July 7 covering nine pages across BCC's eating-disorder and overeating cluster: `/binge-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/emotional-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/foundayo-behavioral-support-san-antonio/`, `/overeaters-anonymous-support-san-antonio-tx/`, `/overeating-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/binge-eating-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and `/compulsive-overeating-counseling-san-antonio-tx/`. July 7 and July 8 were immediately the two strongest days of the entire 14-day window (32 and 31 sessions, 130 and 89 pageviews). That timing is not proof of causation, but it is a strong correlation worth building on.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 140,821 GSC impressions attributed, 514 clicks, and 577 GA4 sessions -- with 1 ranking in the top 3 and 2 in the top 10. That is real traction across the content that has had time to mature. The Foundayo (orforglipron) page is worth watching specifically: it targets a branded drug term with low competition and is the kind of content that can pull organic traffic quickly if it gets indexed and crawled.

The backlink picture is small but meaningful: 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 of those are .edu links. Five .edu backlinks for a counseling center of this size is a genuine asset. The concern is that it has not grown in this window -- there is no new backlink activity in the ledger. That gap is worth addressing strategically.

One hard flag: GSC impressions and clicks are reporting zero for every day in this 14-day window. That is almost certainly a data pipeline or property authentication issue, not a real traffic collapse -- the GA4 numbers confirm real visitors are arriving. But it means the team is flying partially blind on ranking and query data right now, and that needs to be resolved before any meaningful SEO efficacy analysis can happen.

---

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Resolve the GSC data gap first.** Zero impressions and zero clicks across 14 consecutive days is a tooling problem, not a search problem. Re-authenticate the GSC property, confirm the correct site is selected (www vs. non-www, http vs. https), and verify the API connection. Until this is resolved, any ranking or content efficacy analysis is guesswork.

2. **Treat July 7's page-modification batch as a case study.** Nine pages modified on one day, immediately followed by the two best traffic days in the window -- document what changed on those pages (copy, schema, internal links, meta descriptions) so the same approach can be replicated deliberately across other cluster pages rather than ad hoc.

3. **Investigate June 29's 339-pageview spike.** That single day accounts for 34% of all first-half pageviews and skews the half-over-half comparison. Pull the GA4 session source and landing page data for that day to determine if it was a bot, a crawl, a specific referral, or a real content moment. If it was a real content event, identify what triggered it and whether it is repeatable.

4. **Plan a .edu backlink outreach push.** BCC already holds 5 .edu links, which is above average for a local counseling practice. No new backlinks appeared in this window. The existing .edu relationships suggest a warm audience -- identify which institutions those links come from and whether a second link request, a resource contribution, or a guest post is viable for the coming month.

5. **Build internal links from high-traffic pages into the July 7 cluster.** The binge-eating and emotional-eating pages now have updated content, but they likely need internal link support from whatever pages are currently pulling the most Organic Search sessions to accelerate their own ranking movement. This is a one-to-two hour audit task, not a content production task -- identify BCC's top 5 organic landing pages and confirm each links to at least one July 7 cluster page with relevant anchor text.
