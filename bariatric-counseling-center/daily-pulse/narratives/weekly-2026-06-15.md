# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-15

### The window in one sentence

The June 1--11 window held steady in session volume (164 total, -2.4% first half vs second half), but the story underneath is more interesting: session duration is running 85--327% above the 30-day median on most days, and a dense June 11 content push -- 13 pages and posts modified in a single day -- is too new to score but worth watching closely.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the 7-day window (June 1--11, with a gap in logged data for June 4--7), BCC recorded 164 sessions, 137 users, and 736 pageviews. Per-day averages were 23.4 sessions and 19.6 users. GSC impressions and clicks report as zero across the entire window -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline issue rather than a true organic search blackout, and it should be confirmed before drawing any conclusions about search visibility.

Channel mix came in at: Direct 44%, Organic Search 36%, Referral 17%, Organic Social 2%, Unassigned less than 1%. That is a meaningful shift from BCC's typical baseline of 60% Direct / 25% Organic / 10% Referral. Organic Search is running about 11 points higher than baseline, which is a positive signal -- it suggests either a quiet ranking improvement or that some existing pages are pulling more impressions than usual. Referral is also elevated at 17% vs the expected 10%, worth identifying the source. Direct is running low at 44% vs the expected 60%, which could mean email and social pushes were lighter than normal during this stretch, or the attribution model shifted slightly.

The June 3 spike is the most notable volume event: 42 sessions and 230 pageviews in a single day, with 38 users -- all roughly double the 28-day median. June 11 also spiked to 30 sessions and 178 pageviews. Both of those days appear correlated with content activity rather than external promotion, though the specific pages driving them are not confirmed in this data.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

First half (June 1--3): 83 sessions, 359 pageviews. Second half (June 8--11): 81 sessions, 377 pageviews. The headline trend is flat -- sessions down 2.4%, pageviews slightly up. That -2.4% is well within noise range and should not be read as decline.

What is more telling is the session duration pattern across both halves. Duration is running 85% to 327% above the 28-day median on nearly every day in the window, with the June 10 reading (555 seconds average, +327%) being the most extreme. Elevated duration consistently across both halves suggests people who are arriving are genuinely reading -- not bouncing. That is a quality signal even when volume is flat. It also suggests the content itself is holding attention, which matters more than raw session counts at BCC's current traffic scale.

### Launches + efficacy

The heaviest lift of the window happened on June 11, when 13 items shipped: four service pages updated (Bariatric Therapist, Therapy for Depression and Weight Loss, Food Addiction Treatment, Eating Disorder IOP), one evergreen page updated (the GLP-1 medications page), and eight posts modified across the binge eating and eating disorder clusters. These are all 4 days old as of this briefing, which is not enough time for GSC to register impressions or for rankings to move. They should not be judged yet.

Of the June 11 content, the GLP-1 medications page (/understanding-weight-loss-medications-wegovy-ozempic-zepbound-and-mounjaro/) leads with 4 GA4 sessions already -- the most of any single page in the launch set. The Bariatric Therapist page and the "Binge Eating in Men" post each show 2 sessions. The others show zero or one. Again, these are 4-day-old numbers on modified pages, not new builds from zero, so some of that early traffic likely reflects returning or referred visitors rather than new organic discovery. The real signal will come in 10--21 days when Googlebot has had time to re-crawl and re-rank the updated content.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 218 GA4 sessions attributed across 31 pages launched and 97 pages modified, with zero GSC data (same pipeline question as above) and zero top-3 or top-10 rankings confirmed. That zero-ranking figure is likely a data gap rather than reality given the Organic Search channel is running above baseline this window. One item worth noting: the backlink profile shows 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 .edu links. Five .edu links is a meaningful number for a behavioral health practice in San Antonio -- if those are genuine editorial citations from university health programs, that is a real authority signal worth protecting and building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Confirm the GSC data pipeline before anything else.** Zero impressions across every day in the window is almost certainly a feed error, not a true organic collapse -- especially given that Organic Search is delivering 36% of sessions. Until GSC data is flowing cleanly, you cannot evaluate whether the June 11 content push is getting indexed, and you cannot track ranking movement on the GLP-1 medications page or the binge eating cluster. This is the highest-priority diagnostic for the coming week.

2. **Identify the source of elevated Referral traffic (17% vs the 10% baseline).** Something is sending referral sessions above normal. If it is one of those 5 .edu links newly active, that is worth documenting and potentially pitching a follow-up link placement. If it is an untagged social post or an untracked email, that needs to get tagged properly so future campaigns are measurable.

3. **Set a 14-day GSC check on the June 11 binge eating cluster.** The posts -- "Binge Eating vs. Emotional Eating," "Binge Eating Disorder and Ozempic," "A Day Inside BCC's Binge Eating IOP," and the "40% of Cases Get Missed" men's piece -- are targeting high-intent, lower-competition queries. Once GSC is flowing again, pull impressions on those four URLs specifically and see which ones Google is choosing to surface. The ones with impressions but low clicks need title and meta description work; the ones with zero impressions need indexing requests submitted manually.

4. **Build internal links from the GLP-1 medications page to the binge eating and IOP service pages.** That page is already pulling early sessions (4 in 4 days) and likely has existing organic authority. Connecting it to the Eating Disorder IOP page and the Food Addiction Treatment page creates a path from a high-traffic informational asset to conversion-oriented service pages -- a structural gap worth closing this week.

5. **Protect and expand the .edu backlink footprint.** Five .edu links from 4 referring domains is above average for a private behavioral health practice. Identify which institutions those are, confirm the pages linking to BCC, and draft a short outreach note for one or two additional university health or nutrition programs in the San Antonio area. The model is clearly working -- replicate it deliberately rather than waiting for organic citation.
