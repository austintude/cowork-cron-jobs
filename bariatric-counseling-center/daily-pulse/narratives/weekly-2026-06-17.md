# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-17

### The window in one sentence

Over the 7-day window (June 3--15), sessions trended up 31.5% from first half to second half, suggesting the site is building modest momentum after a volatile open. The standout story is not volume -- it is engagement: average session duration ran 100--327% above the 28-day median on nearly every day recorded, which points to something genuinely different about who is finding the site right now.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 169 total sessions, 132 users, and 742 pageviews across 7 recorded days, averaging 24.1 sessions and 18.9 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks are reporting zero across the entire window -- that is almost certainly a data pipeline gap rather than a true ranking collapse, and it needs to be resolved before organic search performance can be properly evaluated.

Channel mix came in notably different from BCC's baseline. Direct led at 75 sessions (44%), followed by Organic Search at 64 sessions (38%), Referral at 26 sessions (15%), and Organic Social at 3 (2%). Organic Search is running well above the expected ~25% baseline share, which is a positive signal -- it suggests the content investments are generating some search-driven discovery, even if GSC data cannot yet confirm which queries are driving it. Referral at 15% is also elevated versus the ~10% baseline, worth tracking to see whether that holds.

Direct's share being lower than baseline (44% vs. ~60%) is notable. That shift is not alarming on its own -- it may simply reflect that organic and referral channels are growing faster right now rather than Direct declining. If an untagged email or social campaign ran during this window, that would also suppress Direct's apparent share by routing traffic through other channels.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is accelerating. First-half sessions (June 3, 8, 9) totaled 73; second-half sessions (June 10, 11, 14, 15) totaled 96 -- a 31.5% increase. Pageviews followed the same direction: 349 in the first half versus 393 in the second. The acceleration is real but modest in absolute terms, and June 3 was an outlier day (42 sessions, 230 pageviews, flagged CRITICAL across every volume metric) that inflated the first-half baseline. Removing that spike, the underlying week-over-week trajectory looks even healthier.

The more durable signal here is engagement quality. Average session duration was flagged as a CRITICAL upward anomaly on five of seven recorded days -- ranging from +58% to +327% above the 28-day median. That pattern across multiple days is not noise. It suggests visitors are reading, exploring, or interacting with content in a meaningful way. Whether that engagement is from a specific page cluster, a referral source sending highly motivated visitors, or something else is worth investigating, but it is the most encouraging pattern in this window.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Resolve the GSC data gap before publishing more content.** Zero impressions across every day of a 7-day window is a pipeline problem, not an organic search problem. Without GSC data, there is no way to confirm whether the June 11 page batch is being crawled, indexed, or ranking at any position. Submit the affected URLs via Google Search Console's URL Inspection tool and confirm the integration is pulling data correctly. This should happen before new pages are prioritized.

2. **Investigate what is driving the session duration anomaly.** Five out of seven days showing 100--327% above-median session duration is unusual enough to deserve a specific GA4 exploration -- which pages are anchoring those long sessions, and where did those users come from. If a particular content cluster or referral source is sending highly engaged visitors, that is the pattern to replicate.

3. **Audit internal linking across the June 11 binge eating cluster.** Seven thematically related pages went live in the same batch (`binge-eating-disorder-ozempic`, `binge-eating-in-men`, `binge-eating-iop`, `binge-eating-vs-emotional-eating`, `pre-bariatric-surgery-binge-eating-screening`, plus the service pages). Pages in the same cluster that link to each other pass authority and help Google understand topical relationships. If these pages are not yet cross-linked, that is a low-effort, high-leverage structural fix.

4. **Build on the .edu backlink base.** Five .edu links from 4 referring domains is a real asset for a specialty practice. Identify which institutions those links come from, then research whether there are adjacent .edu programs (social work, nutrition, public health, nursing) at UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or Texas A&M San Antonio that might link to the binge eating or bariatric counseling content. One warm outreach email to a relevant department contact is a better use of time than producing another new page this week.

5. **Hold off on net-new page production this week and let the June 11 batch mature.** Eleven pages launched in a single day is a substantial batch. Rather than adding more pages before the indexing and ranking picture clarifies, the better move is to let Google process what is already in the queue, fix the GSC integration, and do the internal linking audit. Compounding launches without feedback data makes it harder to understand what is working.
