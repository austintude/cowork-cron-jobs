The elevated Organic Search share is the most interesting channel story in this window. ### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions declined 21.2% from the first half (165 sessions, June 2--14) to the second half (130 sessions, June 15--21), and pageviews dropped from 727 to 457 across the same split. On the surface that reads as a declining window. It is not that simple.

The June 20 and June 21 numbers are almost certainly distorted -- 8 and 5 sessions respectively, with session duration collapsing to under 9 seconds and pageviews at 15 and 9. Those are not traffic drops; those are tracking failures or a site access issue. Remove those two days and the second half is performing comparably to the first. Mid-second-half days (June 15--17) were actually strong, with sessions at 24, 28, and 26, and pageviews at 75, 94, and 102. Engagement depth was also elevated on June 15--17 and June 19, with average session durations running 58--517% above the 28-day median on multiple days. That kind of time-on-site signal suggests people arriving and reading, not bouncing -- which matters more than raw session counts at this stage.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Investigate and resolve the June 20--21 data collapse before drawing any trend conclusions.** Session durations of 7--8 seconds with near-zero pageviews on back-to-back days is not a traffic problem -- it suggests a tracking script failure, a server interruption, or a GA4 configuration change. Identify what happened so future windows aren't skewed by unexplained floor-level days.

2. **Verify the "bariatric counseling" position improvement in GSC once the data connection is restored.** A jump from position 17 to position 10 on a core term is the most significant organic signal of the window. Confirm it held, check what page is ranking for it, and consider whether that page's title tag and meta description are optimized for click-through at that position.

3. **Prioritize the sa-bariatric-center page that was approved for publishing.** It is already in the queue -- get it live this week so it has time to be indexed before the next rollup window. A local competitor page with a clear service area signal is exactly what the Organic Search share gain in this window suggests searchers are looking for.

4. **Audit the 16 Unassigned sessions.** That bucket almost certainly contains untagged email or social links. If BCC is sending newsletters or staff are sharing links without UTM parameters, those sessions are invisible to channel analysis. Adding UTM tags to any recurring email or social links costs 30 minutes and immediately improves data quality.

5. **Develop a targeted outreach pitch to one .edu contact for a second referral link.** With 5 .edu links already in the backlink profile, BCC has demonstrated it can earn that type of link. One additional .edu referral from a San Antonio university health program, community health department, or counseling program would meaningfully strengthen domain authority -- and the groundwork is already laid by the existing relationships.
