### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves comparison shows modest positive momentum: 65 sessions in the first half (June 17--19) vs. 72 in the second half (June 20--23), a +10.8% lift. Pageviews show the same direction more strongly -- 264 in the first half vs. 341 in the second half. On the surface, that reads as a gentle acceleration.

The underlying shape, however, is not smooth growth -- it's a valley and a spike. June 20 and June 21 were genuinely bad days (5--8 sessions each, 75--87% below the 28-day median), and June 22 was an outlier high (40 sessions, 90% above median, with pageviews at 223 -- a 230% spike vs. median). The +10.8% trend number is technically accurate but masks the fact that June 22 alone is doing most of the work. What drove June 22 is not identifiable from this data alone, but the combination of 26 new users (117% above median) and strong session duration (198 seconds, 49% above median) suggests it was real engaged traffic, not a bot or redirect artifact. Worth investigating whether any external mention, social share, or partner communication landed on that date.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit the June 22 traffic source before next week's production cycle.** Forty sessions, 26 new users, and strong session duration on a Sunday is unusual. If a partner, social post, or email drove that, the source should be tagged and repeatable. If it's unidentified, it will show up again as Unassigned and you'll keep losing attribution data. Pull the referral paths and landing pages for June 22 in GA4.

2. **Prioritize snippet and title review for the three pages already showing impressions.** /weight-management-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ at 189 impressions with zero clicks in 24 hours is the highest-leverage optimization target right now. A stronger title tag and meta description that speaks directly to what someone searching that phrase actually needs -- not just a service label -- is likely the difference between a 0% and 2--4% CTR. Do this before position gains solidify the current snippet.

3. **Build an internal-linking pass for the June 24 cluster into next week's workflow.** Twenty pages modified in one day is a significant content push, but if they aren't linked to each other and to higher-authority pages on the site, Google has less reason to treat them as a coherent topical cluster. Specifically, the eating disorder pages and the bariatric/GLP-1 pages should cross-link where clinically appropriate, and both clusters should link from any high-traffic entry pages.

4. **Investigate the Unassigned channel (22 sessions, 16% of the window).** This is consistently above what a clean tracking setup should produce. It likely means UTM parameters are missing from some outbound links -- email newsletters, social bios, or partner referrals. Identifying and tagging even one or two of those sources would meaningfully improve attribution quality over the coming months.

5. **Plan a .edu link outreach play for Q3.** BCC already has 5 .edu inbound links, which is a real asset for a local counseling practice. Identifying which institutions those come from and whether a second link or a resource collaboration is possible -- a student health center, a university counseling program, a nutrition department -- is a strategic move worth scoping. This is not a this-week task but a this-quarter hypothesis to test.
