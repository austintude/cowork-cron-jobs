# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-15

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14-day period ending July 13, sessions grew 61% from the first week to the second (104 vs. 167), driven by a sustained run of above-median traffic July 7--10 that pushed the window average to 19.4 sessions per day. GSC data is showing zero across the board, which is a data pipeline problem that needs resolving before organic search can be evaluated with any confidence.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 271 sessions, 249 users, and 820 pageviews across 14 days -- roughly 19.4 sessions and 58.6 pageviews per day. Those are modest numbers in absolute terms, but the shape of the window matters more than the total: the back half outperformed the front half by a significant margin, which is the direction you want to see.

Channel composition is where this window gets interesting. Organic Search led the window at 122 sessions (45%), followed by Direct at 113 (42%). That is a meaningful reversal from BCC's expected baseline of ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic Search. Organic Search running nearly double its expected share suggests either a ranking improvement, a newly indexed page beginning to pull traffic, or some combination of both. Referral came in at 15 sessions (6%), Organic Social at 11 (4%), Email at 8 (3%), and AI Assistant at 2. The AI Assistant channel appearing at all -- small as it is -- is worth noting as a signal that BCC content is being surfaced in tools like ChatGPT or Perplexity.

The elevated Organic Search share is the most strategically important signal in this window. Without functioning GSC data it is impossible to confirm which queries or pages are driving it, which makes fixing the GSC pipeline the single highest-priority infrastructure task right now.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is clearly accelerating. The first half (June 30 -- July 6) totaled 104 sessions and 318 pageviews, heavily depressed by the July 3--4 holiday weekend (8 and 9 sessions respectively, which is expected). The second half (July 7--13) totaled 167 sessions and 502 pageviews -- a 60.6% lift in sessions and a 57.9% lift in pageviews. That is a meaningful acceleration, not a rounding error.

The anomaly data reinforces this: July 7, 8, 9, and 10 each triggered CRITICAL or MAJOR upward alerts for sessions, users, and new users, with July 8 showing new users up 116.7% over the 28-day median and July 10 showing new users up 125%. The session duration crash on July 10--12 (dropping 72--80% below median) is a separate concern -- that pattern, combined with the July 12 traffic collapse (6 sessions, all metrics down 60--70%), suggests a possible bot flush, a crawl spike, or sessions being recorded without meaningful engagement. That dip should not erase the genuine upward momentum that preceded it; July 13 recovered to 22 sessions with new users and users both registering MAJOR upward alerts, which suggests the floor held.

---

### Launches + efficacy

The launches captured in this window are almost entirely operational git commits -- daily pulse logs, weekly SEO snapshots, and marketing pulse files. None of these are public-facing pages with URLs, and none are generating GSC impressions or GA4 sessions by themselves. The one commit with substantive SEO intent is the July 9 implementation log noting "3 internal links + near-me meta desc on staging12; login blocker cleared." That is a real SEO action, and the timing aligns with the traffic uptick that started July 7--10, though the window is too short and GSC is too broken to draw a clean causal line.

The 30-day efficacy rollup tells the more useful story. Across the past 30 days, the site has accumulated 207,947 GSC impressions and 880 clicks attributed to launched content, with 633 GA4 sessions and 2 pages ranking in the top 10 -- and zero in the top 3. Five new pages launched in the 30-day window, 102 pages were modified, and 79 git commits were logged. The top-10 rankings with no top-3 rankings is the critical gap: those two pages are visible enough to attract 207K impressions but not positioned strongly enough to capture meaningful click volume. At a rough implied CTR of 0.4% (880 clicks / 207,947 impressions), there is substantial headroom if either page can move from positions 4--10 into the top 3.

On backlinks: the site currently shows 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 edu links. No new backlinks were logged within this specific window, and no competitor links exist. The edu link count (5 of 12 total inbound links) is a strong signal -- those are high-authority placements that carry real weight in Google's evaluation of a healthcare site. The question is whether there are more edu or institution-adjacent sites that could be approached.

---

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix GSC data collection before anything else.** Fourteen consecutive days of zero impressions and zero clicks is not a content problem -- it is a tracking or property configuration problem. Every strategic SEO decision for the next sprint depends on being able to see what is ranking and at what position. This is not a next-week task; it is a prerequisite for the next week's work to have any measurable outcome.

2. **Identify which pages are holding the two top-10 rankings and investigate the CTR gap.** With 207,947 impressions generating only 880 clicks, the titles and meta descriptions on those pages likely need work. Pull the specific queries driving impressions in GSC (once it is restored), compare the snippet to the top-3 results for the same query, and rewrite accordingly. A move from position 6 to position 2 on even one high-volume query would likely double click volume.

3. **Investigate the Organic Search overperformance.** Organic Search running at 45% of sessions versus an expected 25% is too large a gap to ignore. Once GSC is live, cross-reference the July 7--13 traffic spike dates against newly indexed pages or ranking movements. If a specific page caused the lift, that page becomes the template for the next content push.

4. **Develop a second edu or institution backlink outreach.** Five edu links out of 12 total is an unusually healthy ratio for a San Antonio bariatric practice. Identify which institutions those links came from, determine whether there are adjacent programs (dietetics departments, social work schools, psychology programs) at UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or Trinity University that could be pitched for a resource link or a provider directory listing.

5. **Investigate the July 10--12 session duration collapse separately.** Sessions running 23--33 seconds average (versus a 123-second median) for three consecutive days, followed by a near-total traffic drop on July 12, suggests a non-human traffic pattern or a misconfigured event. Rule out a bot spike or a crawl from a known scraper before attributing any strategic meaning to those days' numbers. If it is a bot issue, filtering it out will make the underlying trend data cleaner going forward.
