# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-22

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending July 20, BCC averaged 20 sessions per day across 284 total sessions -- a number that looked healthy in the first week and then dropped sharply in the second, producing a -30% half-over-half decline that warrants a closer look. The one substantive content launch of the window (the binge eating disorder page, July 17) is too new to evaluate, and GSC data is entirely absent, which is the biggest data gap to resolve.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 284 sessions, 256 users, and 881 pageviews across 14 days, with a per-day average of 20.3 sessions and 18.3 users. Pageviews-per-session averaged just over 3.1, which is within a normal range for informational health content and suggests visitors are at least browsing beyond the entry page.

Channel composition deviated notably from BCC's baseline. Direct led at 116 sessions (41%), followed by Organic Search at 100 (35%), Referral at 38 (13%), Email at 13 (5%), Organic Social at 12 (4%), Unassigned at 7 (2%), and AI Assistant at 4 (1%). Organic Search running 10 percentage points above its typical ~25% share is the most interesting signal here -- it suggests either a ranking improvement is delivering real traffic or a piece of existing content picked up fresh impressions mid-window. That's worth isolating in GSC by page before drawing conclusions. Direct running below baseline (~41% vs ~60%) likely reflects a quieter period for internal communications or email sends rather than audience loss.

The AI Assistant channel showing 4 sessions is worth noting. It's a small number but confirms BCC content is appearing in AI-generated answers and being clicked through. That channel is early and worth monitoring as a separate line item each week going forward.

GSC impressions and clicks are both zero for every day in this window. This is almost certainly a data pipeline or property connection issue, not a true absence of search visibility -- the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 389,868 attributed impressions and 1,591 attributed clicks, so the data exists somewhere. Restoring GSC connectivity is the top operational priority before the next rollup.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. The first half (July 7--13) produced 167 sessions and 502 pageviews. The second half (July 14--20) produced 117 sessions and 379 pageviews -- a -29.9% drop in sessions and a roughly -24.5% drop in pageviews. That's not noise; that's a pattern.

July 15--17 briefly reversed the slide, with the July 16 spike standing out as genuinely anomalous -- 29 sessions, 109 pageviews, and session durations running 339% above the 28-day median. Something specific drove engaged traffic that day, possibly the pre-publish edits to the ADHD piece or a social or referral push that didn't get tagged cleanly. Then July 18 and July 19 collapsed hard (8 sessions and 2 sessions respectively), pulling the second-half average down significantly. July 18--19 fell on a Friday--Saturday, which may explain part of the drop given that healthcare content typically sees weekend softness -- but a -90% single-day reading on July 19 goes beyond normal weekend variance and suggests something else was happening (site issue, crawl problem, or near-zero organic amplification on that specific day). The recovery to 14 sessions on July 20 is partial at best. Overall: the window ends weaker than it started, and that warrants attention.

### Launches + efficacy

The one substantive content launch inside this window is "Binge Eating Disorder: The Symptoms, Causes, and Effective Treatment Options" (slug: `binge-eating-disorder-the-symptoms-causes-and-effective-treatment-options`), published July 17. At 5 days old as of the window close, it is too early to evaluate against any ranking or traffic benchmark -- Google typically takes 1--3 weeks to stabilize positions on new content in competitive health verticals. However, the July 17 traffic spike (82 pageviews, 54.7% above median, +34.4% year-over-year) occurred on the same day this page went live, which is at least consistent with some immediate crawl or internal-link activity. Monitor this page specifically in the week of July 21--27 for its first GSC impressions.

It has zero impressions and zero sessions so far, which is expected at 1 day post-publish. The July 16 modification date is notable because it coincides with the window's biggest single-day spike (29 sessions, session duration +339%). That spike likely reflects editorial or internal review traffic rather than organic discovery, but it's worth cross-referencing against Referral and Direct sub-channels to confirm.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 8 pages launched and 79 pages modified across the broader period, with 389,868 GSC impressions and 1,591 clicks attributed -- but zero pages ranking in the top 3 or top 10 for tracked keywords. That last number is the one that matters most strategically. BCC is generating real search visibility but not yet converting that visibility into top-of-page positions. The content pipeline is active; the ranking ceiling is the constraint. ### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose and restore GSC data before publishing anything new.** Zero GSC impressions for 14 consecutive days is a broken instrument, not a content problem. Every strategic decision about rankings, CTR, and keyword gaps is blind without it. Verify the GA4--GSC property linkage and confirm the correct Search Console property is connected.

2. **Investigate the July 16 spike and identify the traffic source.** A session duration 339% above median on a day with pre-publish edits and a big pageview number suggests either a specific referral source or an untagged email/social push. Pull the July 16 session-level source/medium breakdown and identify whether any Referral or Unassigned traffic came from a specific domain. If a source is reliably driving high-engagement traffic, build a relationship with it intentionally.

3. **Build internal links from existing high-traffic pages into the binge eating disorder page.** The page is in its indexing window (July 17 launch) and internal linking now will accelerate crawl priority and give Google context for the page's topical position. Candidate linking pages include the emotional eating post (`/blog/what-is-emotional-eating/`), the holistic approach piece, and any eating disorders hub or category page on the site.

4. **Develop a deliberate edu-link outreach strategy around the ADHD piece.** BCC already has 5 edu backlinks -- that's a stronger starting position than most small practice sites. The ADHD-and-eating piece targets a topic that is well-documented in academic literature and frequently cited in university health and counseling resource pages. Identify 3--5 university counseling centers or campus health portals that currently link to similar content and pitch a resource addition.

5. **Investigate why BCC has no tracked keywords in the top 10 despite 390K monthly impressions.** That volume-to-position gap -- significant impressions, no top-10 rankings -- suggests BCC is consistently showing up on pages 2--5 across a wide range of queries rather than dominating a narrower set. The strategic question is whether to consolidate content around fewer, stronger topics to build ranking authority, or to continue broad coverage. That's a decision worth making explicitly rather than by default.
