# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-25

### The window in one sentence

The June 8--23 window delivered 292 sessions at a 20.9 daily average, with channel composition flipped from baseline -- Organic Search led at 42% instead of its usual 25%, suggesting real ranking movement is happening. The window ended on a volatile note: a two-day collapse on June 20--21 followed immediately by the single strongest day of the period on June 22, which makes the overall read more complicated than the flat session trend (-11.6% first half to second half) suggests.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the 14-day window (June 8--23), the site recorded 292 sessions, 228 users, and 1,211 pageviews. Per-day averages were 20.9 sessions and 16.3 users. GSC impressions and clicks show zero across all 14 days in the aggregate feed -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline issue rather than a true signal, since several individual pages in the top_launches data are already registering impressions as of June 24. That GSC gap needs to be confirmed and resolved before it can be used for trend analysis.

Channel composition is the most interesting story in this window. Organic Search drove 124 sessions (42%), nearly double its expected 25% baseline share. Direct came in at 110 sessions (38%), well below its usual 60%. Referral contributed 44 sessions (15%), above its baseline 10%, which is a positive signal -- that backlink profile of 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links (including 5 .edu links) is likely part of what's pulling Organic up. The Unassigned bucket at 23 sessions (8%) is worth watching -- that often means untagged email or social traffic that should be attributed somewhere else.

The Direct underperformance relative to baseline is not necessarily a problem. It likely reflects that this window lacked a major email send or social push from the practice itself. The Organic overperformance, by contrast, is something to pay attention to -- it suggests the site is pulling in more search-driven traffic than it historically has, even before the June 24 page modifications had any time to mature.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions dropped 11.6% from the first half (155 sessions, June 8--16) to the second half (137 sessions, June 17--23), and pageviews were essentially flat at 606 vs. 605. On the surface that reads as a mild decline. But the second half contains a severe outlier pair that distorts the picture: June 20 and June 21 together produced only 13 sessions and 24 pageviews -- both days flagged CRITICAL, down 62--76% versus the 28-day median. Strip those two days out and the second half was tracking at or above first-half pace.

What caused the June 20--21 collapse is not clear from the data. Both days hit simultaneously on session duration (near-zero on June 20: 7.6 seconds average; June 21: 8.8 seconds), which suggests bot traffic, a tracking script issue, or a brief site access problem -- not a genuine audience drop-off. The recovery on June 22 was sharp and real: 40 sessions, 223 pageviews, and average session duration of 198 seconds, all flagged CRITICAL upward. June 23 continued that elevated session duration at 507 seconds. The underlying trend, absent the anomalous dip, is more likely steady-to-slightly-improving than declining.

### Launches + efficacy

All 20 page modifications in the ledger landed on June 24 -- the final day of (or just after) this window. That means none of them had meaningful time to accumulate traffic data within the June 8--23 window itself. Their early GSC numbers from day one are genuinely interesting, though. The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 40,567 GSC impressions and 82 clicks attributed across recent launches, with 278 GA4 sessions -- solid impression volume, but the click-through rate is very low (roughly 0.2%), which is a pattern worth examining across the cluster.

Within the top launches, one page is already standing out at 24 hours old: /weight-management-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ logged 189 GSC impressions on day one with zero clicks. That impression volume this early suggests Google has already crawled and is serving it in results, but the title or snippet is not compelling enough to earn a click. /virtual-emotional-eating-therapy-in-texas/ showed 31 impressions and /bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation-in-san-antonio-tx/ showed 26, both with zero clicks. The same pattern -- visible but not clicked -- is showing up across the cluster. The /bariatric-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/ page has 8 impressions on day one, also no clicks.

No new backlinks were recorded within the window specifically, but the site currently holds 4 referring domains with 12 inbound links including 5 .edu links. That .edu count is disproportionately strong for a practice this size and is likely one reason Organic Search is outperforming its baseline share right now. The 30-day rollup also shows one page in the top 10 in search rankings -- none yet in the top 3 -- so ranking momentum exists but has not yet converted to dominant positions.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit the June 24 page cluster for title and meta description quality before the crawl window closes.** Five pages already have GSC impressions with zero clicks after one day. That is the snippet problem manifesting in real time. Specifically look at /weight-management-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ (189 impressions, 0 clicks) -- if the title is generic or the description is thin, rewriting it now while Google is actively crawling the update will likely move the needle faster than waiting.

2. **Investigate the June 20--21 anomaly before it recurs.** Average session durations of 7--8 seconds across an entire day, combined with session and pageview collapse, suggests a tracking or site availability issue rather than an audience problem. Pull server logs or check GA4 debug view for those dates to confirm. If it was a plugin conflict or GTM misconfiguration, it needs to be caught before it happens again and corrupts a more consequential data window.

3. **Prioritize internal linking into the new eating disorder and weight management pages from high-traffic existing pages.** The 30-day rollup shows strong impression volume (40k+) but weak clicks (82), which suggests the pages are surfacing but not winning the click. Internal links from pages that already receive Direct or Referral traffic will give Google additional confidence signals about these pages and create a path for existing visitors to find them.

4. **Investigate the Unassigned channel (23 sessions, 8% of window traffic).** Unassigned at that share usually means there are email campaigns, social posts, or third-party referrers that are not being tagged properly. Identifying and tagging those sources will give a more accurate picture of whether Direct is truly underperforming or whether it is being diluted by attribution gaps.

5. **Capitalize on the .edu backlink position.** Five .edu inbound links at a practice of this size is above average and is likely contributing to the Organic Search overperformance. Identify which institutions those links come from and whether a second link from any of them is achievable -- through a resource page request, a guest contribution, or an existing relationship with a university counseling program in San Antonio. That model is working and is worth extending deliberately.
