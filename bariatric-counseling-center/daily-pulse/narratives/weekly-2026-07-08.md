# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-08

### The window in one sentence

The June 30 -- July 6 window was a soft week shaped almost entirely by the July 4th holiday drop, with sessions averaging 14.9 per day and organic search quietly outpacing direct for the first time in recent memory. Ten pages were modified and pushed on July 7 -- the last day of the window -- so the real question isn't what happened last week, it's whether that publishing push starts moving the needle this week.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the 7-day window (June 30 -- July 6), the site recorded 104 sessions, 97 users, and 318 pageviews. That works out to 14.9 sessions per day and 13.9 users per day -- both below BCC's 28-day median of roughly 19 sessions per day. GSC impressions and clicks show zero across all seven days, which almost certainly reflects a data pipeline or property-connection issue rather than a genuine collapse in search visibility -- the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 135,205 impressions attributed over that longer window, so something is broken in how GSC data is being pulled into the daily series. That needs to be diagnosed before any GSC trend analysis is meaningful.

Channel composition flipped the expected baseline this week: Organic Search delivered 55 sessions (53% of total), while Direct delivered 45 (43%). BCC's historical baseline is roughly 60% Direct / 25% Organic. Organic running this hot relative to Direct is actually a good sign -- it suggests the site is pulling in people who didn't already know to come back, which is the goal of the content program. Referral (2 sessions) and Email + Organic Social (1 each) were negligible, which is normal. The low Direct share is likely partly a holiday effect: people who are already patients or warm leads may have simply been offline July 3 -- 5.

Session duration was a consistent concern throughout the window. Five of the seven days flagged critically or majorly low average session duration -- the worst being July 3 at 21 seconds against a 28-day median of around 161 seconds. Some of that is holiday noise (shorter, more casual browsing), but sustained low engagement time across multiple days suggests either a lot of single-page bounces from organic visitors landing on thin pages, or a mix of bot traffic. Worth watching whether this resolves in the coming week once the holiday effect clears.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window declined slightly: first half (June 30 -- July 2) produced 54 sessions and 182 pageviews; second half (July 3 -- July 6) produced 50 sessions and 136 pageviews. That's a -7.4% sessions decline and a steeper pageviews drop. On its face that looks like softening, but the context makes it almost entirely explainable by the July 4 holiday cluster -- July 3 and July 4 were the two lowest-traffic days of the week (8 and 9 sessions respectively), and both were flagged as CRITICAL anomalies against the 28-day median.

The more encouraging signal is that July 6 rebounded to 19 sessions and 69 pageviews -- matching the strongest day of the first half (July 2) -- without any obvious trigger. That recovery suggests the underlying floor is intact and the dip was situational, not structural. The window is best read as "flat with holiday noise" rather than genuinely declining.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything that launched did so on July 7 -- technically one day after this window closed, so all performance data is 24-hour-old at most. That's too early for meaningful search signal, but the early GSC impression counts are worth noting because they suggest which pages Google has already found and is starting to serve in results.

The standout early mover is `/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/`, which already shows 660 GSC impressions in its first day with zero clicks. That impression volume at day one is unusually fast pickup and suggests Google had this URL queued or the keyword has real search demand. The 0-click rate at 660 impressions points directly at a title tag or meta description issue -- the page is appearing but not compelling people to click. That is the highest-priority fix in the batch right now. Similarly, `/binge-eating-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/` (17 impressions) and `/emotional-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/` (14 impressions) are showing early crawl pickup. The remaining seven pages in the batch -- including `/compulsive-overeating-counseling-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/binge-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and `/glp-1-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/` -- are sitting at zero impressions at the 24-hour mark, which is normal but worth flagging for indexing requests if they're still at zero in 72 hours.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows the content program has real traction in aggregate: 135,205 GSC impressions attributed, 534 clicks, and 1 page ranking in the top 3. The backlink profile (4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, 5 edu links) is lean but the edu links are high-value -- those are the kinds of citations that move domain authority in local health searches. No new backlinks appear to have landed specifically in this 7-day window.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the title/snippet on `/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/` this week.** 660 impressions with 0 clicks in 24 hours is a CTR problem, not a ranking problem. Test a more direct, service-oriented title -- something that names what the person gets and who it's for. A 2-3% CTR on that impression volume would produce 13+ clicks per day from a single page.

2. **Request manual GSC indexing for any pages in the July 7 batch still showing zero impressions by July 10.** Google indexes fast but not instantly. If `/compulsive-overeating-counseling-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and the other zero-impression pages haven't surfaced by mid-week, submit them via GSC's URL Inspection tool rather than waiting.

3. **Diagnose the GSC data pipeline.** Seven straight days of zero impressions in the daily series, against a 30-day rollup showing 135K impressions, points to a broken connection -- likely a property mismatch or token expiration in whatever script is pulling the daily GSC data. Without functioning GSC data, you're flying blind on which organic sessions are converting to clicks from which queries.

4. **Plan an internal linking audit for the GLP-1 and eating disorder clusters.** You now have multiple pages covering binge eating, emotional eating, compulsive overeating, and eating disorder treatment -- plus GLP-1 pages for Ozempic, Mounjaro/Zepbound, Foundayo, and the GLP-1 behavioral support hub. These pages need to cross-link deliberately or Google will treat them as competing rather than reinforcing. A simple internal link map for both clusters would take a few hours and likely accelerates ranking for the whole group.

5. **Investigate session duration as a content quality signal.** Five of seven days flagged critically short average session times. Once holiday noise clears, if this pattern continues into the week of July 7 it likely means organic visitors are landing on pages that don't match their intent or that load without enough substance to hold attention. Pull the top organic landing pages for that week and review them against what a first-time visitor would actually need to see.
