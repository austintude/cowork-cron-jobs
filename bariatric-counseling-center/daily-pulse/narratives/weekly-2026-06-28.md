# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-28

### The window in one sentence

The week of June 20--26 was defined almost entirely by a single-day spike on June 22 that drove roughly a third of all sessions and nearly half of all pageviews for the week. Outside that spike, daily traffic ran well below the 28-day median, and the second half of the week settled into a low-but-stable range that suggests the June 22 event was a discrete pulse rather than a sustained lift.

---

### Volume + composition

The 7-day window produced 117 sessions from 97 users, with 480 pageviews total. That works out to about 16.7 sessions per day and 68.6 pageviews per day -- numbers that look acceptable in isolation but are skewed hard by June 22. Strip that day out and the remaining six days average closer to 13 sessions and 42 pageviews, both of which sit below the 28-day median. GSC impressions and clicks show zero across every day in the window, which is a data pipeline issue to flag separately -- it does not reflect actual search visibility, but it does mean organic attribution is blind right now.

Channel composition this week diverged meaningfully from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led with 47 sessions (40% of total), followed by Direct at 31 (26%), Referral at 25 (21%), Organic Social at 13 (11%), and Unassigned at 8 (7%). The baseline expectation is roughly 60% Direct / 25% Organic Search / 10% Referral -- what actually happened is nearly the inverse for Direct vs. Organic, with Direct running about half its expected share. That gap is worth noting: it may mean a regular Direct audience (email subscribers, repeat visitors, staff) was less active this week, while Organic and Referral picked up a different kind of visitor -- likely connected to the June 22 event.

The June 22 spike itself is the single most important thing in this dataset. Sessions hit 40 (90% above the 28-day median), pageviews hit 223 (230% above median), and new users hit 26 (117% above median). Average session duration on June 22 was 197 seconds -- elevated but not extreme. The following day, June 23, session duration jumped to 507 seconds, the highest in the window, which suggests the people who came back after the spike were highly engaged. The source of the June 22 event is not cleanly attributable from this data, but the Referral and Organic Social channels together account for 38 sessions on the week, and the pattern is consistent with a post or share that drove a burst of traffic -- possibly connected to the six new pages submitted to GSC on June 25 (suggesting the content was published just before).

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions grew 20.8% from the first half (53 sessions across June 20--22) to the second half (64 sessions across June 23--26). On the surface that reads as acceleration, but the framing is misleading. The first half included two very soft days (8 and 5 sessions on June 20--21) followed by the June 22 spike. The second half had no spike but also no crash -- it held at 13--19 sessions per day, which is closer to the site's normal range.

Pageviews tell the more honest story: 247 in the first half, 233 in the second half -- a slight decline. The session duration pattern reinforces this: June 22--24 showed elevated engagement, but June 25--26 saw duration drop back to 63--63 seconds, well below the 28-day median of roughly 175--198 seconds. The window is not accelerating in any durable way. It had a spike, absorbed it, and returned to baseline. That is not necessarily bad news -- baseline is holding -- but it is not momentum. The second half of the week looked like a normal week, not a new normal.

### Launches + efficacy

The most substantive activity in this window was a batch of six page modifications pushed on June 24 and submitted to GSC on June 25. Those pages are: `/eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/semaglutide-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and `/online-weight-management-therapy-in-texas/`. All six are zero impressions and zero sessions so far, which is expected at 3--4 days post-submission -- Google typically needs 1--2 weeks to crawl, index, and surface new or refreshed pages. None of these should be flagged as underperforming yet, but they move to watch-list status at the two-week mark (around July 8--9).

The 30-day efficacy rollup gives broader context. Across 12 pages launched and 66 pages modified in the last 30 days, the attributed totals are 57,613 GSC impressions, 136 GSC clicks, and 292 GA4 sessions. Only 1 page is ranking in the top 10, and none are in the top 3. That means the content catalog is generating real impression volume -- 57K impressions is not nothing -- but it is not yet converting that visibility into clicks or rankings at scale. A 136-click result from 57,613 impressions works out to roughly a 0.24% CTR, which is low and points to a title/meta-description or snippet quality problem across the broader site, not just individual pages.

The git commit log in this window is almost entirely automated pulse reports and records entries -- no substantive new page launches beyond the six modifications noted above. The backlinks picture is stable: 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links total, 5 of which are from .edu domains. No new backlinks are confirmed as earned this specific week, but the existing .edu link profile is a meaningful asset that is currently underutilized as a referral driver.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Investigate the June 22 traffic source before next week's production decisions.** The spike produced 40 sessions and 223 pageviews with no clear GSC attribution. Pull the Referral and Organic Social channel breakdowns in GA4 to identify the specific source -- a referring URL, a social post, an external mention. If it came from an external site or share, that is a replication opportunity. If it was untagged internal email, UTM parameters need to go on outbound links immediately.

2. **Set a two-week check-in for the six June 24 page modifications around July 8--9.** These are the most strategically meaningful content moves in the window. If they still show zero impressions by then, request manual indexing inspection in GSC and audit their internal linking -- pages that are not linked from anywhere on the site often stall in the crawl queue regardless of GSC submission.

3. **Address the sitewide CTR problem as a priority project this coming week.** A 0.24% CTR on 57K impressions suggests that titles and meta descriptions across the catalog are not connecting with search intent. Run a GSC query report filtered to pages with more than 500 impressions and fewer than 5 clicks, and identify the top 5--10 pages where a title rewrite is most likely to move the number. This is higher-leverage work right now than publishing additional new pages.

4. **Develop a second .edu outreach target.** The site currently has 5 .edu backlinks from 4 referring domains. Those links are almost certainly helping anchor the site's authority in local health searches. Identify which .edu domain is sending the most referral sessions, confirm the relationship, and map one or two other San Antonio-area university health or counseling programs where a guest resource, citation, or directory listing could generate a comparable link.

5. **Do not interpret the 20.8% session trend as real growth yet.** The halves comparison is distorted by the June 22 spike and the low-traffic weekend days that preceded it. Before treating this as an upward trend, the site needs two consecutive weeks without a single anomalous day driving the average. The honest read right now is: baseline is stable, one external event produced a temporary lift, and the underlying trajectory is flat.
