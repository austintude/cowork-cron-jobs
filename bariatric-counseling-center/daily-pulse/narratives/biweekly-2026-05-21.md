# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-05-21

### The window in one sentence

This is a 3-day window (May 17--19), not a full 14-day rollup, so trend confidence is limited -- but the shape of those three days tells a clear story: the site started the window in a soft dip and ended May 19 with a genuine traffic spike, with sessions hitting 85% above the 28-day median and new users doubling it. The data infrastructure that shipped across this window (the daily pulse system, dashboard panels, and GA4/GSC data publishing) is the dominant activity, and the underlying site traffic appears to be recovering upward.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the three days in this window (May 17--19), the site recorded 73 sessions, 60 users, and 272 pageviews. The per-day average of 24.3 sessions is roughly in line with the 28-day median of ~20, but that average masks a sharp within-window swing -- May 17 was significantly below baseline (12 sessions) and May 19 was well above it (37 sessions, +85% vs. median).

GSC impressions and clicks are both zero across all three days. This is almost certainly a data lag issue rather than a true organic search collapse -- GSC data typically runs 2--4 days behind. That said, it's worth flagging: if impressions remain at zero once the lag clears, that would warrant attention.

Channel composition skewed toward Direct (43 of 73 sessions, ~59%) and Organic Search (25 sessions, ~34%), with a small Referral contribution (4 sessions) and negligible Unassigned (1). Direct at 59% is consistent with BCC's expected baseline of ~60%. Organic Search running at 34% is modestly above the ~25% baseline, which is a quiet positive signal -- it suggests content may be drawing some incremental search traffic even without GSC data to confirm rankings.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves comparison field came back empty in this window's data, likely because the window is only 3 days and the system requires a longer span to split. Working directly from the daily series: sessions went 12 -- 24 -- 37, which is a clean upward progression. Each day roughly doubled the one before it. This is accelerating, not flat or declining.

The acceleration is real, but 3 days is too short to call it a durable trend. May 19's spike (+85% vs. median, +60.9% YoY for that same day) suggests something specific drove traffic that day -- potentially a piece of content getting indexed, a social share, or an untagged email send landing in Direct. It's worth investigating whether that day's lift was a one-time event or the start of a sustained move. If May 20 and May 21 hold above the 20-session median, the acceleration is meaningful.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything that shipped in this window is internal tooling -- all 20 commits across May 18--20 are data infrastructure: the daily pulse system, dashboard panels (GMB panel, backlinks ledger, briefing slot, launch markers, daily GSC), GA4 raw data publishing alongside GSC, and markdown renderer fixes. None of these are public-facing content pages, so there are no ranking signals, GSC impressions, or attributable sessions tied to them. They won't move organic search numbers -- but they're what makes measuring organic search numbers possible.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 11 pages launched and 8 keywords ranking in the top 3 and top 10 -- but GSC impressions attributed to those launches are zero, which again is almost certainly GSC data lag rather than zero organic visibility. The 49 GA4 sessions attributed to launched content across 30 days is the more honest number here. That's a modest but real contribution from content that's been live long enough to accumulate traffic. If the 8 top-3 rankings are genuinely holding, those pages should be pulling meaningful impressions once the GSC lag clears.

No backlinks were added in this specific window. The current backlink profile shows 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 .edu links. The .edu count is a genuine asset -- those are high-authority signals, and 5 out of 12 total links being .edu is an unusually strong ratio for a local healthcare site. No new competitor links are present.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Verify what drove May 19.** Sessions nearly tripling from May 17 to May 19 without a clear content or campaign trigger is worth a 15-minute investigation. Pull the May 19 Direct sessions in GA4 and check landing pages -- if they're all landing on the homepage, it likely suggests an untagged email or social post. If they're spreading across content pages, it suggests organic discovery. Knowing the answer shapes where to put energy next week.

2. **Treat the 8 top-3 rankings as your highest-priority internal linking opportunity.** Eight pages ranking top-3 is real equity sitting on the table. If those pages aren't yet connected to your service and appointment pages with clear internal links, you're likely leaving conversion value behind. Next week's strategic task: audit the internal link structure of those 8 pages and add contextual links to consultation or contact pages where they're missing.

3. **Build a plan to grow the .edu backlink base.** Five .edu links out of 12 total is a strong foundation. The strategic play is to replicate whatever earned those links -- likely local university health programs, community partnerships, or resource citations. Identify which institutions those links came from and look for a second point of contact at each, or identify comparable institutions (UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, Trinity University) that haven't linked yet. One new .edu link per month is an achievable and high-value goal.

4. **Pressure-test the GSC zero-impression readings once lag clears.** If impressions are still zero for the 8 ranking pages after May 23 or so, something is wrong -- either Search Console property verification, a sitemap issue, or the pages are ranking in a GSC view that isn't being captured. Don't let this sit past end of next week without a clear answer.

5. **Establish a content launch (not just tooling) for the coming week.** The last 3 days were almost entirely infrastructure commits. The infrastructure is valuable -- it's what powers this briefing system -- but the 30-day window shows 11 content pages launched over a month, and organic growth depends on that pace continuing. Identify the next 1--2 content pages to publish and get them into the queue, even if they don't ship until next week.
