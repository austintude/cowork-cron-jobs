# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-11

### The window in one sentence

The 14-day window ending July 9 shows sessions accelerating 23% from the first week to the second -- a genuine momentum shift driven by Organic Search leading traffic (not Direct, which is the usual pattern) and a concentrated burst of page modifications on July 7 that likely triggered the second-half surge. GSC data is entirely absent across all 14 days, which is a structural blind spot that needs to be resolved before organic attribution can be trusted.

### Volume + composition

Total volume for the window: 248 sessions, 223 users, 1,038 pageviews, and 0 GSC impressions/clicks (zeroes across the board -- more on that below). Per-day averages landed at 17.7 sessions and 15.9 users, which is consistent with BCC's current baseline. The pageview-to-session ratio is roughly 4.2, higher than a simple informational site would see, which suggests visitors are exploring multiple pages per visit -- a healthy signal.

Channel composition this window inverted the expected baseline in a notable way. Organic Search led with 122 sessions (49% of total), well above the typical ~25% share. Direct came in at 94 sessions (38%), well below the expected ~60%. Referral was 16 sessions (6%), roughly on target. Organic Social was 8 (3%), Email 6 (2%), and AI Assistant logged 2 sessions -- a small but worth-tracking channel. The Organic Search overrepresentation relative to baseline is the most meaningful signal here: it suggests search rankings are contributing real traffic, even without GSC confirmation. The Direct underperformance likely means fewer untagged email or social hits this window, not a drop in brand awareness.

The GSC zeroes are a serious data gap. Zero impressions for 14 consecutive days is not a normal fluctuation -- it indicates either a GSC API connection failure, a property verification issue, or a data pipeline break. This is not a content or SEO problem; it is a reporting infrastructure problem. None of the organic attribution below can be cross-checked against impressions or rankings until GSC is restored.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions in the first half (June 26 -- July 2) totaled 111, averaging 15.9 per day. Sessions in the second half (July 3 -- July 9) totaled 137, averaging 19.6 per day -- a 23.4% increase. That is a clear acceleration, not noise. Pageviews tell a more complicated story: the first half generated 607 pageviews versus 431 in the second half, but the first half was heavily distorted by a single 339-pageview day on June 29 (likely a bot sweep, an internal session, or an untagged email blast that drove deep browsing). Strip that outlier and the second half pageview rate is competitive.

The second-half acceleration is credible and grounded in real events: July 3 and 4 were holiday-adjacent lows (Independence Day weekend, both flagged as CRITICAL down anomalies vs. 28-day median), and July 7 through 9 then came in with CRITICAL up anomalies -- 32, 31, and 24 sessions respectively, all well above the 28-day median of 19. The July 7-8-9 run also showed new users up 92%, 117%, and 58% respectively against median. That is a genuine new-visitor influx, not just returning traffic. The direction entering this window is positive.

If that was the case, the July 7-9 traffic lift may partly reflect Google regaining access to previously blocked content. This is worth verifying directly in Google Search Console once the API feed is restored.

The 30-day efficacy rollup attributes 142,792 GSC impressions and 550 clicks to content launches, with 1 ranking in the top 3 and 2 in the top 10 across 5 pages launched and 102 pages modified. That top-3 ranking is the most valuable asset in the current content inventory and should be identified by name -- the rollup data does not surface the specific URL, but pulling it from GSC directly should be the first move when the connection is restored. Backlinks sit at 4 referring domains, 12 total inbound links, and 5 edu links. Edu links are high-authority and difficult to earn -- that number is a meaningful asset for a local healthcare counseling practice and should be preserved and built on.

No new page launches (net-new URLs) are visible in this window -- all confirmed page-level activity was modifications, not new content. The git commits in the window are primarily pulse/reporting automation and do not represent indexable pages.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Resolve the GSC data feed before any other SEO decision.** Fourteen days of zero impressions in the pipeline means every organic attribution in this briefing is estimated, not confirmed. The login blocker clearance on July 9 and the July 7 page modifications may already be generating ranking movement that is invisible until the feed is restored. This is the highest-leverage diagnostic task of the coming week.

2. **Identify and prioritize the one top-3 ranking page from the 30-day rollup.** The efficacy data shows a single page ranking in the top 3 -- that page is your current organic anchor. It likely needs internal links from the July 7 cluster pages pointing into it, a meta description refresh, and a CTA audit to make sure the traffic it earns converts. Find it, name it, protect it.

3. **Run a crawl audit on the July 7 modified pages to confirm they are indexable and not inheriting any staging-environment restrictions.** The login blocker fix on July 9 suggests there may have been access control issues in that environment. Verify that `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/binge-eating-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and the other five cluster pages are returning 200 status, have canonical tags pointing to themselves, and are not blocked in robots.txt.

4. **Investigate the June 29 pageview spike (339 pageviews, 30 sessions) to understand what drove it.** That single day accounts for roughly 33% of all first-half pageviews. If it was an email send, a social post, or a referral from a specific source, that traffic source deserves a repeat. If it was bot traffic or internal browsing, it should be filtered. Either way, leaving it unexplained distorts every average in the window.

5. **Build a backlink outreach plan around the existing 5 edu links.** Five edu links is a real asset for a local practice. Identify which institutions they come from, and use that as a warm-approach model -- similar programs, departments, or resource pages at those same institutions are the most realistic targets for additional high-authority links. One targeted pitch to an institution already linking to BCC is worth more than five cold outreach attempts to new domains.
