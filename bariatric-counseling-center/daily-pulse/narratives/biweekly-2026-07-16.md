# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-16

### The window in one sentence

The July 1-14 window shows genuine momentum -- sessions grew 27% from the first week to the second (122 vs. 155), driven by a cluster of strong new-user days in July 8-10 that pushed well above the 28-day median. The composition of that traffic, however, raises questions worth resolving: GSC impressions are completely dark, session duration cratered mid-window, and the channel mix is inverted from baseline expectations.

---

### Volume + composition

Over 14 days, the site recorded 277 sessions across 248 users, generating 834 pageviews. That works out to roughly 19.8 sessions and 59.6 pageviews per day -- modest numbers in absolute terms, but directionally positive given where the window started. GSC impressions and clicks came in at zero across all 14 days, which almost certainly indicates a data pipeline or property connection issue rather than a true organic search blackout. That needs to be resolved before any organic conclusions can be drawn.

The channel mix is notably inverted from BCC's typical baseline. Organic Search led the window at 123 sessions (44%), followed closely by Direct at 112 sessions (40%). BCC's baseline profile is roughly 60% Direct and 25% Organic Search, so Organic punching above its usual weight is potentially good news -- it could suggest content is starting to earn search visibility that the GSC feed isn't currently capturing. Referral contributed 21 sessions (8%), which is slightly above baseline and worth watching. Organic Social brought in 11 sessions (4%), Email 8 sessions (3%), and the AI Assistant channel registered 2 sessions -- a small but worth-noting signal that BCC content is surfacing in AI-driven query environments.

One channel pattern warrants attention: the relatively low Direct share compared to baseline, combined with the high Organic figure, suggests that either the audience mix shifted meaningfully this window or some traffic that should be tagged as Direct (or Email) is being reclassified. If a newsletter or social push went out around July 7-8 without proper UTM parameters, that could partly explain the new-user spike and the Organic inflation simultaneously.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is clearly accelerating. The first half (July 1-7) totaled 122 sessions and 411 pageviews. The second half (July 8-14) totaled 155 sessions and 423 pageviews -- a 27% session lift and modest pageview growth. That is meaningful upward movement across a genuine calendar shift, not a single-day anomaly inflating the average.

The caveat is that the acceleration is lumpy rather than clean. July 8, 9, and 10 were the engine: all three days registered CRITICAL-level spikes in new users (up 116%, 58%, and 125% above the 28-day median, respectively), and session counts on those days ranged from 24 to 32. July 12 then collapsed to just 6 sessions -- a CRITICAL drop of 69% below median -- before recovering to 22 and 20 on July 13-14. The July 12 crash looks like a Saturday low-traffic pattern plus possibly a content or technical issue, but the bounce-back on July 13-14 suggests no persistent damage. More concerning is the simultaneous crash in average session duration on July 10-12, where duration dropped 73-80% below the 28-day median on three consecutive days. High new-user counts combined with extremely short sessions suggests those visitors may have arrived without clear intent or encountered a friction point -- worth investigating before calling this acceleration unambiguously healthy.

---

### Launches + efficacy

The launches captured in this window are entirely git-commits -- daily pulse reports, weekly SEO snapshots, and one LinkedIn ideas log. There are no public-facing page launches recorded in the ledger for this period. That means the session and user growth happening in the second half of the window is attributable to content or pages that shipped before July 1, not to anything that went live in this window. The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 5 actual pages launched, 217,071 GSC impressions attributed, 943 GSC clicks, and 640 GA4 sessions attributed across that broader period -- so older content is carrying the load here, and it appears to be doing real work.

The efficacy rollup also shows 2 pages ranking in the top 10 and 0 in the top 3. That top-10 presence is the most likely driver of the elevated Organic Search share this window, and getting any of those pages from position 4-10 into position 1-3 is the highest-leverage ranking move available. No new backlinks were earned in this specific window based on the ledger, but the overall backlink profile (4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, 5 of which are .edu links) is a legitimately strong foundation for a practice of this size. The .edu link count is particularly valuable -- that's not typical for a local bariatric counseling practice and suggests earned credibility worth protecting and building on.

The LinkedIn commit on July 13 (Todd psych-eval turnaround, Sara food noise) is the most strategically interesting non-technical item in the window. Social content tied to specific clinical themes could drive qualified Organic Social sessions if it's performing -- but with only 11 Organic Social sessions total for the window, the conversion from LinkedIn to site visits remains thin.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC blackout before anything else.** Fourteen consecutive days of zero impressions and zero clicks is not a content performance story -- it is a data connection problem. Verify the GSC property is linked correctly in GA4, confirm ownership isn't lapsed, and check whether the property was recently changed or migrated. Until this is resolved, organic performance is essentially unreadable.

2. **Investigate the session-duration crash on July 10-12.** New users arrived in large numbers, then sessions ran 23-34 seconds on average across three days -- well below the 28-day median of 123 seconds. This pattern suggests those visitors landed somewhere that didn't hold them. Pull a landing page breakdown for those three days and look for a page that was receiving high new-user traffic with a near-instant exit rate. It may point to a page that needs a stronger hook, better internal linking, or a clearer call to action.

3. **Prioritize moving the 2 top-10 pages toward top-3.** The efficacy rollup shows pages earning impressions and clicks but sitting outside the top-3 where the click-through-rate curve is substantially steeper. Pull those specific pages in GSC, review their title tags and meta descriptions for specificity and relevance, and check whether they have strong internal links pointing at them from related content. A targeted internal-linking pass or a title-tag refresh could move them meaningfully.

4. **Test whether the LinkedIn content is generating qualified site traffic.** The July 13 LinkedIn commit represents a content strategy worth measuring properly. Set up UTM parameters for any links posted in LinkedIn content going forward so Direct and Organic Social aren't absorbing credit that belongs to a specific social push. One week of properly tagged LinkedIn traffic would tell you whether those clinical-theme posts are actually driving visits or just engagement that stays on-platform.

5. **Build a second .edu backlink relationship.** The current profile of 5 .edu links across 4 referring domains is a real asset. Identify which institution is responsible for those links, confirm the relationship is healthy, and then map one or two other local or regional .edu contacts -- a nursing program, a health sciences department, a community college dietetics course -- who might legitimately reference BCC content. One new .edu link would materially strengthen the domain's authority profile over the next 30-60 days.
