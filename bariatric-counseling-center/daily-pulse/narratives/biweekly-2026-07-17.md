# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-17

### The window in one sentence

The July 2--15 window delivered 278 sessions at a modest +7.5% first-half-to-second-half improvement, with Organic Search slightly outpacing Direct for the first time in recent memory. The window ended on a stable note, but GSC data is fully absent and a sharp single-day collapse on July 12 masks what is otherwise a quietly improving pattern.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the 14-day window, the site logged 278 sessions, 250 users, and 840 pageviews -- averaging roughly 20 sessions and 60 pageviews per day. GSC impressions and clicks show zero across every day in the window, which almost certainly reflects a data pipeline gap rather than a true indexing collapse; the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 375,143 attributed impressions and 1,459 clicks, so the search presence is real. That zero-GSC condition needs to be confirmed and resolved before the next rollup.

Channel composition this window was notably different from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led at 119 sessions (43%), followed by Direct at 109 (39%). That's an inversion of the expected ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic split, and it's a meaningful signal -- it suggests either that content published in prior weeks is beginning to pull search traffic, or that Direct (which often captures email and untagged social) was suppressed by the holiday week at the window's start. Referral contributed 27 sessions (10%), sitting right at baseline. Organic Social added 11 sessions (4%), and a small but notable 4 sessions arrived via AI Assistant -- a channel worth watching as AI-driven discovery grows. Email was low at 8 sessions, which is worth noting if any sends went out during this period without proper UTM tagging.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

Sessions moved from 134 in the first half (July 2--8) to 144 in the second half (July 9--15), a +7.5% lift. That's not dramatic acceleration, but it is a real directional improvement -- and it came despite a near-total collapse on July 12 (only 6 sessions, down ~69% from median). Remove that outlier and the second half looks meaningfully stronger. The July 12 drop falls on a Saturday and follows a cluster of strong days July 7--10, so it reads as a weekend/holiday artifact rather than a structural issue.

Pageviews tell a more cautionary story: 453 in the first half versus 387 in the second (-14.5%). That divergence -- more sessions but fewer pageviews -- suggests that second-half visitors are landing and leaving more quickly rather than exploring. The July 10--12 session duration anomalies (down 73--80% from median) reinforce this. Something was driving shallow visits during that stretch -- possibly bot traffic, possibly direct navigation to a single page. July 15 closed with avg session duration up 124% above median, which is the kind of engaged session pattern you want to end on.

---

### Launches + efficacy

Every launch tracked in this window shipped on July 16 -- technically one day outside the July 2--15 measurement window -- so their session and impression data reflects a single day of exposure and should be treated as early signals only, not conclusions. All 19 are page-modify events, meaning these are updates to existing URLs rather than new pages, which is important: Google already has these pages indexed, so ranking changes can appear faster than they would for brand-new content.

The early standouts in that July 16 batch: `/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/` pulled 1,056 GSC impressions in one day with 0 clicks -- high impressions / zero clicks is a clear title or snippet issue, not a visibility issue. That page is being seen; it's just not being chosen. `/night-eating-syndrome-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/` showed 537 impressions and 5 clicks (0.9% CTR), and `/adhd-and-emotional-eating-in-san-antonio-tx/` came in at 211 impressions / 6 clicks (2.8% CTR) -- the ADHD page is actually outperforming on CTR for day one. `/glp-1-weight-regain-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/` hit 204 impressions / 5 clicks (2.5% CTR). The GLP-1 cluster pages (Ozempic, Mounjaro/Zepbound, behavioral support) are all at zero impressions after one day -- normal for fresh modifications, but worth monitoring next week.

On backlinks: the site holds 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links total, and 5 of those are from .edu domains. That .edu ratio is strong for a clinical practice site and worth protecting. No new backlinks appear to have been earned in this specific window.

---

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the title/meta on `/bariatric-surgery-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/` before anything else.** 1,056 impressions in one day with zero clicks means the page is ranking but the snippet is not earning trust or clearly communicating what the page does. Test a more direct title that names the service and the audience explicitly. This is the highest-leverage single edit available right now.

2. **Monitor the GLP-1 cluster pages (Ozempic, Mounjaro/Zepbound, `/glp-1-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/`) through July 22.** If impressions are still at zero by then, request indexing via GSC for each URL -- the modifications may not have been recrawled yet.

3. **Investigate the July 10--12 session duration crash.** Three consecutive days of avg session duration 73--80% below median is unusual. Pull the page-level breakdown in GA4 for those dates to check whether a single low-quality source (a specific referrer, a bot pattern, or an untagged campaign with high bounce) is skewing the number. If it's bot traffic, filter it -- it's distorting your week-over-week trend reads.

4. **Build internal links into the ADHD and GLP-1 weight regain pages from higher-traffic existing pages.** Both showed real CTR on day one, suggesting the content is resonating when people find it. Internal links from the bariatric surgery and emotional eating pages would accelerate their authority accumulation and make them easier to find for people already on the site.

5. **Pursue a second .edu backlink while the relationship model is fresh.** With 5 edu links already, BCC has demonstrated it can earn institutional trust. One targeted outreach -- to a UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or Trinity University health resource page -- could meaningfully lift domain authority and is worth a single focused pitch next week rather than waiting for it to happen organically.
