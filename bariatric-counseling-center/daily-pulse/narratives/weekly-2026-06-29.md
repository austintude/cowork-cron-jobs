# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-29

### The window in one sentence

The week of June 21-27 was defined by a single outsized day (June 22 -- 40 sessions, 223 pageviews) followed by a steady slide back to below-baseline levels, leaving the window net-negative when measured by momentum. The real story is not the traffic volume but the pattern underneath it: four meaningful page modifications shipped June 24, GSC data is returning zeros across the board, and the second half of the week trended down 15.6% from the first -- which suggests the June 22 spike was an event, not a shift.

### Volume + composition

The window produced 118 sessions, 97 users, and 485 pageviews across 7 days -- averages of 16.9 sessions/day and 13.9 users/day. Those averages are pulled upward by June 22 and do not reflect what most days actually looked like. Strip that one day out and the remaining six days averaged roughly 13 sessions/day, which is below the site's 28-day median of approximately 20-21 sessions/day. GSC impressions and clicks came in at zero for all seven days -- that is almost certainly a data pipeline or property authentication issue rather than a true organic search collapse, and it needs to be confirmed before drawing any conclusions about search visibility.

Channel composition this week flipped BCC's expected baseline almost entirely. Organic Search led with 51 sessions (43% of total), followed by Direct at 28 (24%), Referral at 25 (21%), Organic Social at 13 (11%), and a small Unassigned tail. Direct coming in at nearly half its expected share (24% vs. the 60% baseline) is notable -- it could mean fewer repeat visitors and staff clicks in this period, or it could reflect that the June 22 spike was driven largely by Organic Search and Referral traffic rather than returning audience. The relatively strong Referral number (21% vs. the 10% baseline) is worth watching -- with 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links in the backlinks data, including 5 edu links, something is likely sending periodic bursts of referral traffic.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves split is straightforward: 64 sessions and 326 pageviews in the first half (June 21-23), versus 54 sessions and 159 pageviews in the second half (June 24-27). That is a -15.6% session decline and a roughly -51% pageview decline. The pageview drop is steeper than the session drop, which suggests that the deep browsing behavior from June 22-23 -- when session duration anomalies ran 50-250% above median -- did not carry into the back half of the week. People who came in during the first half were reading multiple pages per visit; people who arrived in the second half were not staying as long or going as deep.

This is a declining window, not a growing one. It would be easy to look at the 40-session day on June 22 and read the week as a positive signal, but the trajectory after that point tells a different story. The week ends with June 27 logging 9 sessions and an average session duration of just 6 seconds -- a 97% drop from the 28-day median -- which suggests most of those late-week visits were bots, accidental landings, or very quick bounces. That session duration figure in particular is worth flagging as potentially skewing the overall engagement picture for the period.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap first.** Seven consecutive days of zero impressions and zero clicks is almost certainly a tracking or authentication issue, not an organic search collapse. Before making any content or SEO decisions based on this window, confirm that the GSC property is still verified and that the data connector is pulling correctly. This is a prerequisite for everything else -- you cannot evaluate the June 24 page modifications or the six newly submitted pages without search data.

2. **Give the four June 24 modifications two more weeks before evaluating.** /eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/ and /eating-disorder-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/ are the two pages most likely to see ranking movement if the content updates were substantive. Set a calendar reminder to pull GSC position data for those specific URLs around July 10-12 and compare to their pre-modification positions.

3. **Run a crawl or manual check on the six pages submitted to GSC on June 25.** With zero GSC data for the window, there is no confirmation these pages were indexed. Check Google Search Console's URL Inspection tool on each of the six submitted URLs directly, confirm indexing status, and re-request if any are still in the "discovered but not indexed" state.

4. **Investigate the June 22 traffic source.** A 90% session spike and 230% pageview spike on a single day, driven by Organic Search and Referral rather than Direct, suggests either a ranking jump that day, a backlink activation, or an external mention (social post from a referring site, newsletter, etc.). Identifying what caused it is worth 30 minutes in GA4's traffic source breakdown for that specific date -- if it was a referral source, that domain is worth a relationship-building outreach.

5. **Audit the Referral channel this week.** Referral running at 21% of sessions versus a 10% baseline, combined with 5 edu backlinks in the profile, suggests there may be traffic sources that are not fully documented. Pull the referral source report in GA4 for the full window, identify the top 3-4 referring domains, and confirm whether any of those edu links are actively sending traffic -- those are worth cultivating for additional link opportunities.
