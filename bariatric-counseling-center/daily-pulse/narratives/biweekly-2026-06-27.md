# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-27

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days from June 10 to June 25, sessions trended down 27% from first half to second half (169 vs. 123), with the decline driven largely by a two-day near-blackout June 20-21 and a soft close to the window -- though June 22 produced the site's strongest single-day in the data set (40 sessions, 223 pageviews), which suggests the new service pages submitted to GSC are starting to generate interest. The more significant story here is structural: GSC data is returning zero impressions across all 14 days, which means the organic search picture is effectively invisible right now, and that needs to be resolved before any content efficacy read is meaningful.

### Volume + composition

The window produced 292 sessions across 14 days (20.9 per day average), 227 users, and 1,187 pageviews. Those numbers are roughly in line with BCC's baseline volume but nothing more -- a typical two-week stretch rather than a growth period. GSC shows zero impressions and zero clicks for every day in the window, which is almost certainly a data pipeline or property configuration issue rather than a true signal. Do not interpret this as organic search drying up; it means the GSC feed is broken and needs to be checked at the source.

Channel mix this window: Organic Search led with 125 sessions (43%), Direct followed with 104 (36%), Referral contributed 44 (15%), and Unassigned added 24 (8%). The Organic Search share running above baseline (~25%) is actually a potentially positive signal -- if accurate, it suggests some of the new service pages are pulling search visitors. The Unassigned bucket at 8% is worth watching; that volume typically indicates untagged traffic from an email send or social post, and it can inflate or distort channel comparisons if it keeps growing. Referral at 15% is above baseline (~10%), which may reflect the backlink profile -- 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 of those being .edu links, which is a meaningful quality signal even at low absolute volume.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. First half (June 10-18) produced 169 sessions and 647 pageviews. Second half (June 19-25) produced 123 sessions and 540 pageviews -- a 27% drop in sessions and a 17% drop in pageviews. That directional shift is concerning, but the shape of the decline matters here. June 20 and 21 were anomalous lows (8 and 5 sessions respectively, both flagged CRITICAL vs. the 28-day median of 21), with session durations of under 9 seconds -- suggesting a tracking or site issue on those specific days rather than an organic traffic drop. June 22 then produced 40 sessions and 223 pageviews, the strongest single day in the data set (+90% above median), with session duration of nearly 3.5 minutes, which is genuinely engaged behavior.

Strip out the June 20-21 anomaly and the second half picture looks more like a plateau than a decline -- which is a more honest read. The closing days (June 24-25) running below median is the part worth monitoring going into next week. June 23 stands out for a different reason: session duration of 506 seconds (nearly 8.5 minutes), the highest in the window, suggesting a small number of highly engaged visitors were reading deeply. That kind of engagement on low-volume days sometimes precedes referral conversions or appointment inquiries.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Resolve the GSC data gap first.** All 14 days of GSC impressions and clicks are zero, which makes it impossible to evaluate whether the nine new service pages are being indexed or ranking. Verify GSC property configuration, confirm the data pipeline connection, and manually check index status for the six pages submitted June 25. Until this is fixed, organic search efficacy is unmeasurable.

2. **Identify the one top-10 ranking page from the 30-day rollup and prioritize it.** The efficacy data shows exactly one page in the top 10. That page is the most actionable asset on the site right now. Pull it from GSC (once the feed is restored), review its content for depth and internal linking, and make sure the eating disorder and bariatric therapy cluster pages link to it where topically appropriate.

3. **Build internal links into the nine June 24 service pages immediately.** These pages are newly indexed (or pending indexing) and have no session volume yet. Internal links from existing higher-traffic pages -- homepage, about, blog posts -- will help Google discover and weight them faster. Waiting for organic traffic without internal linking support suggests these pages will sit dormant for weeks longer than necessary.

4. **Investigate the June 20-21 site anomaly before writing it off.** Session durations of under 9 seconds on two consecutive days (vs. a 145-second median) may indicate a tracking script failure, a redirect loop, or a brief site outage. If it was a tracking failure, some sessions during that window are simply missing from the data. Knowing which scenario it was matters for interpreting the overall window total (292 sessions may be understated by 20-30 sessions).

5. **Develop a .edu backlink outreach strategy using the existing five .edu links as proof of concept.** BCC already has five .edu inbound links -- that is rare for a practice-scale site and suggests a credibility signal that can be leveraged. Identify which institutions those links come from and find one or two comparable Texas university health, counseling, or nutrition programs to pitch for a resource page mention. A second .edu link from a UTSA, UT Health, or Trinity source would meaningfully strengthen the domain authority profile.
