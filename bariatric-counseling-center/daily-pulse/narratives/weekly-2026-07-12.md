# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-12

### The window in one sentence

The July 4--10 window started in a holiday-driven dip and recovered sharply by mid-week, with the second half outpacing the first by 183% on sessions -- suggesting the July 7 page modifications to the eating disorder and bariatric counseling cluster triggered a measurable traffic lift. ### Volume + composition

Over the 7-day window (July 4--10), the site logged 161 sessions, 144 users, and 489 pageviews, with a daily average of 23 sessions and 20.6 users. Those numbers look modest in isolation, but the July 4th holiday strongly depressed days 1--3, so the raw totals undersell what actually happened in the back half of the week.

Channel composition this window was notably inverted from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led at 80 sessions (50% of total), while Direct came in second at 53 sessions (33%). That's a meaningful flip -- baseline expectation is ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic Search. A higher Organic share generally points to ranking movement or a content event pulling in new visitors, which aligns with the July 7 page modifications. Referral (11 sessions), Organic Social (8), and Email (7) were all in expected range. Two sessions attributed to AI Assistants is a small but worth-watching signal -- that channel is showing up more often across the industry for health-adjacent queries.

The elevated Organic share deserves scrutiny because GSC data is showing zero impressions and zero clicks for all seven days. That is almost certainly a data pipeline issue rather than a true zero -- if Organic Search drove 80 sessions, impressions were not zero. Resolving the GSC feed should be the first action this week, because without it, there is no way to confirm which queries or pages are actually driving this traffic.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves comparison tells a clear story: the first half (July 4--6) totaled 42 sessions and 120 pageviews; the second half (July 7--10) totaled 119 sessions and 369 pageviews. That is a 183% session increase from one half to the other. Even accounting for July 4th as a structural low, the rebound by July 7 was sharp enough -- and sustained through July 10 -- that it reads as a genuine acceleration, not just a holiday bounce.

What complicates the picture slightly is session duration. July 5 showed a 59% drop in average session duration versus the 28-day median, and July 10 showed a 73% drop. High new-user counts combined with short sessions can mean landing pages are attracting clicks but not holding attention -- worth watching if it continues. The July 7 session duration spiked 43% above median, which suggests the page modifications that day created more engaging content for at least one segment of visitors. The overall arc for the window is accelerating, which is good news, but the session-duration dip on July 10 is a flag to monitor in the coming week.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose and restore the GSC data feed this week.** Zero impressions across seven days while Organic Search is driving 50% of sessions is a clear pipeline break, not a content problem. Until GSC is showing real data, there is no way to know which queries are working, which pages are ranking, or whether the July 7 modifications moved positions. Everything else on this list is secondary to fixing this.

2. **Confirm the July 9 near-me meta description change is live in production, not staging.** The commit log says it was applied to staging12. If it has not been promoted to production, that is a quick win sitting on the table -- local "near me" queries are high-intent for a counseling center and the meta description directly affects CTR on those results.

3. **Plan an internal linking pass for the July 7 cluster pages.** Binge Eating Therapy, Emotional Eating Therapy, Eating Disorder IOP, and Bariatric Surgery Counseling are likely competing for some overlapping queries. A deliberate internal linking structure between them -- with clear topical hierarchy -- will help Google understand which page to surface for which intent, and will distribute whatever authority the domain has more efficiently across the cluster.

4. **Investigate the session duration drop on July 10.** New users were up 125% vs. the 28-day median that day, but average session duration crashed 73%. That pattern -- lots of new arrivals who leave quickly -- suggests a traffic source that is not well-matched to the content (possibly social referral, a syndicated link, or a mismatched search query). Identifying the source in GA4 will tell you whether this is worth addressing or just noise.

5. **Build a second edu backlink outreach plan based on the existing 5 edu links.** Five edu links is a meaningful starting point for a local counseling center. Identifying which institutions those links are coming from and whether any have related departments (nutrition science, social work, public health) that might link to additional BCC pages is a strategic play that compounds over time -- not a same-week task, but worth scoping this week so outreach can begin next.
