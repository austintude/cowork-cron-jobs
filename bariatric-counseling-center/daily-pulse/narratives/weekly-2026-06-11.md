# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-11

### The window in one sentence

Over the 7-day window (May 31 -- June 10), sessions trended up 63% from the first half to the second half, driven almost entirely by a single high-traffic day on June 3. Outside of that spike, volume returned to baseline levels and GSC impressions remain at zero across the entire window, which is the most pressing structural issue right now.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 150 total sessions, 131 users, and 608 pageviews across 7 days, averaging 21.4 sessions and 18.7 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks are both zero for every day in the window -- this is not a data gap, it is a real signal that Google has not yet served BCC pages in search results during this period, or that GSC reporting is lagging significantly. That zero line needs to be resolved before organic search can be tracked or optimized meaningfully.

Channel composition came in at roughly 46% Direct, 38% Organic Search, 12% Referral, and 3% Organic Social. That is a notable shift from BCC's expected baseline of ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic Search. Organic Search running 13 points above baseline is potentially good news -- it suggests real search-driven arrivals -- but without GSC data to confirm which queries and pages are driving it, the interpretation is incomplete. Direct running below baseline may reflect a quieter email or social push during this window, or it may be a natural fluctuation. Referral at 12% (18 sessions) is worth watching; that is above the ~10% baseline and could indicate the existing backlink profile is sending real traffic.

The anomaly count is high -- 10 critical, 8 major -- but most of them cluster around two patterns: session duration running well above the 28-day median on multiple days, and the June 3 spike inflating volume metrics. Neither pattern signals a problem on its own, but together they suggest that when people do arrive, they are spending meaningful time on the site.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

On paper, the window looks like it is accelerating: 57 sessions in the first half (May 31 -- June 2) versus 93 in the second half (June 3 -- June 10), a 63% jump. That number is accurate but misleading. June 3 alone accounted for 42 sessions -- more than the entire first half combined. The anomaly data confirms this was a real statistical outlier, with sessions up 115%, users up 117%, and pageviews up 236% versus the 28-day median for that day of the week.

Strip out June 3 and the second half averages roughly 17 sessions per day, which is actually slightly below the first half's 19 per day. The honest read here is that the window is flat to slightly declining once the spike is removed. That is not a crisis at this traffic volume, but it means the June 3 event is carrying the trend story and the underlying daily pattern has not shifted. Understanding what drove June 3 -- untagged email, a social post, a referral burst, or something else -- is more strategically important than the trend percentage itself.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC blackout first.** Seven straight days of zero impressions is the highest-priority issue in this briefing. Verify that GSC is properly connected to the GA4 property, that the correct site is verified, and that there are no manual actions or coverage issues on the GSC Search Console property. If the connection is intact, submit the June 9 and June 10 pages for indexing inspection manually via GSC's URL Inspection tool. Nothing else in the SEO workflow can be validated until impressions are reporting.

2. **Investigate June 3.** A 115% session spike with no GSC-attributed clicks and no obvious content launch on that date strongly suggests an untagged source -- likely an email send, a social post from the practice, or a referral burst. Identify the source, tag it properly going forward, and evaluate whether it can be replicated deliberately. If it was an email to a patient or referral list, that channel deserves its own UTM infrastructure.

3. **Build internal links into the GLP-1 cluster now, before indexing completes.** The Mounjaro/Zepbound, Foundayo, GLP-1 regain, and Wegovy/Ozempic pages are all modified within the same 48-hour window and target related, high-intent queries. Connecting them to each other and to the existing bariatric therapist and tirzepatide pages with descriptive anchor text while Google is still crawling them gives the crawler a cleaner topical signal and improves the odds of the cluster indexing as a coherent group rather than isolated pages.

4. **Audit the `/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx-v2/` slug.** A "-v2" URL is a flag. If this page is meant to eventually replace the original, having both live simultaneously splits link equity and creates a potential duplicate content situation. Decide now whether to redirect the original to v2, merge content, or use a canonical tag -- before either page earns enough backlinks or rankings to make the decision more costly.

5. **Pitch one .edu link-building outreach this week.** BCC already has 5 .edu links, which is above average for a single-location counseling practice and suggests an existing relationship or citation footprint in that sector. One targeted outreach to a San Antonio-area university health or counseling resource page -- UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or Trinity -- capitalizing on the eating disorder and GLP-1 content as a resource citation is a realistic play that fits the existing pattern of what is already working.
