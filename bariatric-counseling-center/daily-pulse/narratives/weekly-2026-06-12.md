# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-12

### The window in one sentence

Over the 7-day window (May 31 -- June 10), sessions trended up 63% from the first half to the second half, driven almost entirely by a single high-traffic day on June 3. Strip that day out and the underlying pattern is flat-to-soft, which makes the June 11 content push the most consequential variable to watch right now.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 150 total sessions, 131 users, and 608 pageviews across 7 days, averaging 21.4 sessions and 18.7 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks both read zero for the entire window -- this is a data pipeline issue worth flagging, not a ranking collapse, but it means organic search attribution is blind right now.

Channel composition came in at roughly 46% Direct, 38% Organic Search, 12% Referral, and 3% Organic Social. That's a notable departure from BCC's baseline (60% Direct / 25% Organic Search / 10% Referral). Organic Search running 13 points above baseline is potentially good news -- it suggests either a quiet ranking improvement or that some existing pages are pulling more search traffic than usual. The Direct share running below baseline is worth watching; it could mean email or social campaigns that were driving branded traffic have been quieter this cycle.

Referral at 12% (18 sessions) is slightly above baseline and consistent with having a small but real backlink footprint -- 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links, including 5 edu links. No new backlinks were added in this window, but the existing edu links are doing steady work.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

On paper, the window looks like it's accelerating: sessions jumped from 57 in the first half (May 31 -- June 2) to 93 in the second half (June 3 -- June 10), a 63% increase. Pageviews tell a similar story -- 179 in the first half versus 429 in the second.

The honest read is more nuanced. June 3 alone produced 42 sessions and 230 pageviews, which is more than double any other day in the window. Remove that outlier and the second half averages roughly 17 sessions per day across the remaining three days -- actually below the first half's ~19 per day average. The June 3 spike is real and worth investigating (no obvious trigger is visible in the launch ledger for that date), but it should not be read as sustained momentum. The underlying trajectory coming out of this window is essentially flat, making the June 11 content modifications the variable that will define whether the next window actually accelerates.

The Ozempic/GLP-1 medication page (`/understanding-weight-loss-medications-wegovy-ozempic-zepbound-and-mounjaro/`) also received a modification and has already pulled 4 sessions in its first day -- the most of any single launch in this batch.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 175 GA4 sessions attributed to launched content but zero GSC impressions and zero top-10 rankings. That GSC data gap is the main reliability problem right now. It's not credible that 31 pages launched over 30 days have produced no impressions whatsoever -- the most likely explanation is a GSC API connection issue or a property/filter misconfiguration. Until that's resolved, it's impossible to assess whether the content cluster is gaining search visibility or sitting unindexed.

No backlinks were earned in this specific window. The existing 5 edu links are the strongest asset in the backlink profile and there is no competitor link pressure showing.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Investigate and resolve the GSC zero-data problem before shipping more content.** The 30-day rollup showing zero impressions across 31 pages is almost certainly a data issue, not a ranking reality. Confirm the GSC property is connected correctly in whatever integration feeds this system, check that the property matches the site's canonical domain, and manually verify 2-3 of the older pages directly in GSC. Until this is fixed, efficacy measurement is essentially broken.

2. **Audit the June 3 spike to understand its source.** 42 sessions and 230 pageviews on a single day with no corresponding launch is unusual. Check GA4 for the specific pages and traffic sources that drove it -- if it was a referral or social share, that's a replication model worth understanding. If it was branded search, that's a different signal.

3. **Build internal links into the binge eating cluster before the next content push.** The June 11 modifications created a dense cluster of topically related pages (binge eating IOP, binge eating in men, binge eating vs. emotional eating, pre-surgery screening). If these pages are not already cross-linking to each other and pointing to the service page at `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, that's a structural gap that will limit how much authority consolidates on the IOP page once search visibility kicks in.

4. **Use the GLP-1 medication page as a test case for editorial-to-service linking.** It pulled 4 sessions in one day, suggesting it has an existing audience. Check whether it currently links to the therapy and bariatric counseling service pages -- if it doesn't, adding those links is a low-effort, high-leverage move that could convert informational visitors into potential clients.

5. **Pursue a second edu backlink, likely through UTSA Health or a comparable San Antonio institution.** With 5 edu links already in the profile, there's proof the outreach model works. One additional high-authority edu link pointing to the eating disorder or binge eating cluster would meaningfully strengthen the pages most likely to compete in search over the next 60-90 days.
