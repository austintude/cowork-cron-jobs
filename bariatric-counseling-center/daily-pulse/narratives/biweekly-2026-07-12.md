# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-12

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14-day window (June 27 -- July 10), sessions accelerated sharply in the second half, climbing 53% from the first week to the second, driven by Organic Search overtaking Direct as the leading channel and a cluster of page modifications to core service pages on July 7. GSC data is returning zeros across the entire window, which is a data pipeline issue that needs to be resolved before search performance can be properly assessed.

---

### Volume + composition

The site logged 266 sessions and 241 users across 14 days, averaging 19 sessions and 17.2 users per day. Total pageviews came in at 1,068, averaging about 76 per day -- though that number is skewed heavily by June 29 (339 pageviews in a single day, likely a crawl or a referral burst worth investigating). GSC impressions and clicks are reporting as zero for every day in the window, which is almost certainly a data pipeline or authentication issue rather than a real signal. Do not treat those zeros as meaningful until the connection is confirmed working.

Channel composition this window diverged meaningfully from BCC's baseline. Organic Search led with 125 sessions (47% of total), well above the expected ~25% share. Direct came in at 104 sessions (39%), below the typical ~60% baseline. Referral was modest at 15 sessions (6%), Organic Social at 13 (5%), and Email at 7 (3%). The Organic Search dominance is the headline here -- it suggests content that was published or modified in prior weeks is starting to rank and pull clicks. The lower Direct share likely means fewer people who already know BCC are visiting, while more people who are discovering BCC through search are coming in. That is a healthy sign for audience growth, assuming the page experience holds up once they land.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

This window is accelerating, and clearly so. The first half (June 27 -- July 3) produced 105 sessions total, averaging 15 sessions per day. The second half (July 4 -- July 10) produced 161 sessions averaging 23 per day -- a 53% jump in session volume between the two periods. July 4 and July 5 dipped below baseline, which is expected given the holiday weekend. The meaningful signal is what happened starting July 7: sessions hit 32, then 31, then 24, then 32 again across the final four days of the window. That is a sustained run above the 28-day median of 19, not a single-day spike.

Pageviews tell a slightly different story. The first half totaled 579 pageviews, which includes the June 29 outlier (339 pageviews alone). Strip that out and first-half pageview depth was actually thin. The second half's 489 pageviews across seven days reflects more consistent engagement -- roughly 70 pages per day without a single distorting spike. The anomaly data confirms July 7 through July 10 logged CRITICAL upside alerts on new users (up 92-125% vs. the 28-day median across those days), which suggests the site is reaching genuinely new people, not just cycling through existing visitors. The one concern: average session duration crashed to 33 seconds on July 10, a 73% drop vs. median. That warrants attention -- it may indicate a high-bounce landing page drawing in those new users.

---

### Launches + efficacy

The most significant production activity in this window was a batch of five page modifications on July 7: Binge Eating Therapy, Emotional Eating Therapy, Eating Disorder IOP, Bariatric Surgery Counseling, and the Foundayo (Orforglipron) behavioral support page. All five carry medium confidence as meaningful updates. The timing lines up precisely with the session surge that began the same day -- July 7 was the window's strongest non-outlier day (32 sessions, 130 pageviews, average session duration up 43% vs. median). That correlation is worth noting, though it likely reflects a combination of the updates themselves, possible social sharing, and any internal or email promotion that may have accompanied the publish.

The Foundayo page is worth watching specifically. Orforglipron is a new oral GLP-1 medication with very low search competition and growing public interest. If that page is indexed and ranking, even at position 20-40, it represents a first-mover opportunity in a query space that will grow substantially over the next 6-12 months. Unfortunately, the GSC data gap means we cannot confirm its current SERP position from this data. The 30-day efficacy rollup does show 161,478 GSC impressions and 640 clicks attributed to launches over that broader period -- with 1 page ranking in the top 3 and 2 in the top 10. Those numbers suggest older content is performing, but we cannot trace which pages without the GSC connection working.

The rest of the in-window commits are operational (daily pulse and marketing pulse logs) rather than publishable content, so there is no meaningful SEO surface area to evaluate there. No new backlinks were recorded in the window. The site currently holds 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links, including 5 from .edu sources, which is a meaningful asset at this domain's likely authority level.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the GSC data connection first.** Every strategic decision about content performance, ranking trajectory, and which pages to prioritize is currently blocked by zero-reporting GSC data. This is not a content task -- it is a technical/infrastructure task that should be escalated this week. Without it, the 30-day rollup numbers (161K impressions, 640 clicks) cannot be connected to specific pages, and the Foundayo page and July 7 modifications cannot be evaluated for search traction.

2. **Audit the July 10 session-duration crash before publishing anything new to that likely landing page.** New users were up 125% on July 10 but average session duration was 33 seconds -- a sharp mismatch that suggests a high-volume entry point with a poor landing experience, a mismatched audience expectation, or an untagged bot spike. Identify which URL was the primary entry point that day and review the page before driving more traffic to it.

3. **Build internal links from existing high-traffic pages into the five July 7 modified pages.** The binge eating, emotional eating, and IOP pages are likely competing in a local search cluster. Connecting them to each other and to the Bariatric Surgery Counseling page via contextual internal links will help Google understand the topical relationship and distribute any ranking authority already accrued. This is a multi-day strategy task, not a same-day fix.

4. **Develop a backlink outreach plan for the Foundayo / GLP-1 content.** Five .edu links is a strong foundation. The Foundayo page is topically novel -- behavioral health support for a new oral GLP-1 -- which means health journalism outlets, patient advocacy organizations, and university health programs may find it linkable if it is framed as an explainer rather than a service pitch. Identify two or three target publications this week and draft a short outreach approach.

5. **Investigate the June 29 pageview spike (339 in one day).** That single day produced nearly a third of the first half's total pageviews and the source is unclear from this data. If it was a referral, identify the domain -- that referrer may be worth nurturing. If it was a crawl, it should be filtered. Either way, understanding what caused it will inform whether it is repeatable or ignorable.
