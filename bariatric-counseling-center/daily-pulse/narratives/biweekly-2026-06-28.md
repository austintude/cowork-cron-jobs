# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-28

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending June 26, the site logged 286 sessions at a 20.4 daily average -- a number that looks acceptable on the surface but masks a real decline, with second-half sessions running 31% lower than the first half. The window's defining story is a two-day collapse on June 20--21, a single-day spike on June 22 that inflated the averages, and a cluster of six page modifications in the eating-disorder and weight-management space that landed on June 24 and need time to prove themselves.

---

### Volume + composition

Total volume for the window: 286 sessions, 221 users, 1,151 pageviews, and -- a continuing problem -- zero GSC impressions or clicks recorded. The per-day average of 20.4 sessions is close to the 28-day median, so the window doesn't read as a crisis, but averages are doing a lot of flattering work here. June 11 (30 sessions, 178 pageviews) and June 22 (40 sessions, 223 pageviews) are both pulling the mean upward. Strip those two days out and the remaining 12 days average closer to 17 sessions, which is softer than baseline.

Channel composition is notably different from BCC's expected mix. Organic Search led the window at 126 sessions -- 44% of total traffic -- well above the expected 25% share. Direct came in at 99 sessions (35%), running under the expected 60%. Referral contributed 43 sessions (15%), nearly double its expected share. Organic Social added 16 sessions and Unassigned accounted for 23, which is worth flagging -- Unassigned at 8% often indicates traffic arriving without UTM parameters, likely from a social post or email that didn't get tagged.

The Organic Search overperformance relative to baseline is a meaningful signal. It suggests something is ranking -- even without GSC data to confirm which queries -- and that the content investment is generating search-driven traffic. The suppressed Direct share is harder to interpret cleanly; it could mean the practice's patient-facing and staff communications were quieter this window, or that some Direct traffic is being absorbed into Organic or Unassigned due to attribution gaps.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. First-half sessions totaled 169 (average: 24.1/day across seven days). Second-half sessions totaled 117 (average: 16.7/day). That's a 30.8% drop half-over-half, and it's not explained by the June 22 spike -- that spike is already included in the second half and it's still pulling the second-half average down relative to the first.

The June 20--21 crash is significant. Sessions hit 8 and then 5 on those two days, both 60--76% below the 28-day median. Average session duration on June 21 was 8.7 seconds -- essentially nobody was engaging with anything. This looks less like a content problem and more like an external event: a site error, a hosting hiccup, or a Monday-into-Tuesday lull in direct traffic that wasn't compensated by organic. The June 22 recovery was real (40 sessions, 223 pageviews, session duration back above 197 seconds), but the days following June 22 settled at 13--19 sessions, below the first-half baseline. The second half of this window is soft, and that pattern is what carries into the coming week.

### Launches + efficacy

The most substantive activity this window happened on June 24: six pages were modified and pushed to production. Those pages -- `/eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/semaglutide-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and `/online-weight-management-therapy-in-texas/` -- represent a coherent cluster of service-level pages covering eating disorders and weight management behavioral support. They were also submitted to GSC on June 25, per the commit record. At four days old as of June 28, it's too early to expect ranking movement, but these are exactly the pages that should be generating impressions within the next 7--14 days if Googlebot is crawling efficiently. The fact that GSC is returning zero impressions across the entire window is a data gap that needs to be resolved -- either the integration is broken or these pages aren't indexed yet.

The 30-day efficacy rollup offers a broader view: 57,613 GSC impressions and 136 clicks attributed across launches in that period, with one page holding a top-10 ranking. That's a thin but real foothold. Zero top-3 positions means there's no page yet generating consistent referral-quality organic sessions. The Organic Search channel's 44% share of window traffic suggests something is working at positions 4--20 -- pages getting impressions and occasional clicks -- but nothing has broken into the range where it becomes a traffic driver on its own.

The remainder of the launches in the window are tooling and reporting commits (daily pulse reports, SEO pulse records), not content production. Useful for tracking but not pulling search weight. No backlinks were earned in this specific window based on available data, though the existing profile shows 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and notably 5 EDU links, which is a stronger-than-average foundation for a local health practice.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap before producing more content.** Zero impressions for the entire 14-day window is not a minor gap -- it's either a broken GSC integration or a crawling/indexing problem. Until this is resolved, you're producing content without the ability to measure whether it's reaching search results at all. Confirm that the six pages submitted June 25 are appearing in GSC's URL Inspection tool. If they're not indexed, request indexing manually for each one before shipping anything new.

2. **Build internal links into the June 24 cluster immediately.** The six eating-disorder and weight-management pages are live and indexed requests are submitted, but they're likely sitting as isolated pages with little internal authority pointing at them. Spend time next week identifying 3--5 existing pages on the site that could contextually link to each of the six new pages. Internal linking is often what separates a page that surfaces in search from one that doesn't, especially in the early weeks.

3. **Investigate the June 20--21 drop for a root cause.** Two consecutive days at 5--8 sessions with near-zero session duration is not normal weekend behavior -- it's anomalous even for low-traffic days. Pull the server logs or check GA4's real-time data to determine whether this was a tracking issue, a site availability issue, or genuinely no traffic. If it was a tracking gap, the window totals are understated. If it was a real drop, understanding why protects against it recurring.

4. **Pursue a second EDU backlink using the existing relationship as a model.** Five EDU links in a four-domain referring profile is genuinely strong for a local counseling practice. Identify which institution produced those links and look for adjacent opportunities -- a UTSA Health, UT Health San Antonio, or similar program that covers eating disorders, bariatric medicine, or behavioral health, where a resource page or faculty reference could generate a link. One more EDU link in this profile would measurably strengthen domain authority.

5. **Audit the Unassigned channel before the next content push.** Twenty-three sessions arriving as Unassigned (8% of window traffic) suggests at least one referral source -- likely an email send or social post -- that wasn't tagged with UTM parameters. Identify what went out during the window without tagging and fix the template going forward. Unassigned traffic is traffic you can't act on strategically.
