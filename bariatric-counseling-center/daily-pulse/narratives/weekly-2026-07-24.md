### Volume + composition

The 7-day window produced 111 sessions from 105 users, 377 pageviews, and a per-day average of 15.9 sessions and 15.0 users. GSC impressions and clicks both read zero for every day in the window -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline or property connection issue, not a real signal, given that the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 395,704 attributed impressions. That GSC gap should be investigated before drawing any conclusions about search visibility this week.

Channel composition leaned heavier on Direct than BCC's baseline would predict. Direct accounted for 56 of 111 sessions (50%), Organic Search came in at 24 (22%), and Referral delivered 21 (19%). Direct is running roughly in line with the 60% baseline after accounting for the 6 Unassigned sessions (which likely belong there), but Referral is running notably above the 10% baseline at nearly double. That Referral overperformance is worth tracking -- with 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links (including 5 from .edu domains), BCC's link profile may be delivering real navigation-intent traffic. Email accounted for 6 sessions, suggesting some list activity during the week, though the volume is small enough that a single send could explain it.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The first half (July 16-18) generated 61 sessions and 219 pageviews. The second half (July 19-22) generated 50 sessions and 158 pageviews. That is a -18% decline in sessions and roughly -28% in pageviews. On the surface this looks like a weakening week, but the shape of the data tells a different story: July 18-19 look like a near-total traffic interruption (8 sessions Saturday, 2 sessions Sunday) that pulled the second-half total down. Once that weekend floor is accounted for, Monday through Wednesday (14, 17, 17) show a reasonable recovery back toward baseline. The week is not accelerating, but it is not structurally declining either -- it is noisy, shaped by a weekend collapse that is consistent with a primarily referral- and direct-driven audience that is not browsing on weekends. The concerning signal is July 22's average session duration dropping 76% below the 28-day median. If that persists into next week, it suggests people are landing and leaving quickly, which would be worth diagnosing at the page level.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit the GSC data connection.** Seven straight days of zero impressions in the daily data while the 30-day rollup shows nearly 400K impressions is a clear pipeline discrepancy, not a rankings collapse. Resolve this before the July 23 launch cluster gets its first full week of data -- you will need clean GSC numbers to evaluate whether those modifications moved rankings.

2. **Prioritize title and meta description rewrites for `/nutritional-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/` and `/holistic-weight-loss-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/`.** These pages are already appearing in search results (3,854 and 318 impressions respectively) but generating zero clicks. That is the clearest optimization opportunity in the current portfolio -- the visibility is there, the click-through is not. Test more specific, benefit-oriented titles that reflect what someone searching those terms actually wants to know.

3. **Build an internal linking pass across the eating disorder and food relationship cluster.** Twenty pages touching related topics were modified on July 23 without a coordinated internal linking structure likely producing isolated pages rather than a topical cluster. Next week, map which pages link to which, identify the gaps, and connect them systematically -- this is how clusters generate compounding authority rather than competing against each other.

4. **Investigate the July 22 session duration drop.** Average session duration fell 76% below the 28-day median on the last day of the window. Before attributing it to content quality, check whether it coincides with a specific traffic source, a specific landing page, or a technical event (caching change, redirect, page speed regression). One low-duration day is not a pattern, but it is worth ruling out a technical cause early.

5. **Track the Foundayo page separately as a standalone experiment.** `/foundayo-behavioral-support-san-antonio/` is targeting a brand-new medication category (orforglipron is not yet widely prescribed) and already has one session at one day old. This page likely has low search volume now but high growth potential as the drug enters wider clinical use. Set a reminder to pull its GSC data at 30 and 60 days -- if it starts ranking for branded queries, it could become one of the higher-converting pages in the portfolio.
