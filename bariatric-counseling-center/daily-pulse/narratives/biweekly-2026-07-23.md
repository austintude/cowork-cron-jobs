### Volume + composition

The 14-day window produced 269 sessions from 245 users, generating 808 pageviews. Per-day averages land at 19.2 sessions and 17.5 users -- within the site's normal operating range but not growing. GSC impressions and clicks are both zero across every day in the window, which is a data availability issue rather than a true performance collapse (GSC typically lags 3-4 days and the integration may need a recheck).

Channel composition came in at: Direct 117 (43%), Organic Search 87 (32%), Referral 35 (13%), Email 13 (5%), Organic Social 12 (4%), AI Assistant 4 (1.5%), Unassigned 6 (2%). Compared to BCC's baseline of roughly 60% Direct / 25% Organic Search / 10% Referral, Direct is running low and Organic Search is running high. That Organic Search share at 32% -- seven points above baseline -- is the most interesting number in this table. It suggests search-driven traffic is punching above its usual weight, likely a lagging benefit from earlier content launches in the eating-disorders and mental-health clusters.

Referral at 13% (35 sessions) is also above baseline. With 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links total -- including 5 edu links -- some of that referral traffic is probably coming from those edu sources, which is high-quality audience. No new backlinks were recorded as earned within the window itself, but the existing link profile appears to be delivering.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

This window is declining. First half (July 8-14) totaled 155 sessions and 423 pageviews. Second half (July 15-21) totaled 114 sessions and 385 pageviews -- a drop of 26.5% in sessions and 9% in pageviews. That gap is large enough to be meaningful, not just noise.

The shape is important, though. The second half started well: July 15-17 produced the window's strongest three-day stretch, with July 16 alone hitting 29 sessions and 109 pageviews (both CRITICAL anomalies above the 28-day median). Average session duration on July 15-16 spiked dramatically -- 246 seconds and 491 seconds respectively, against a median of roughly 110-112 seconds -- suggesting people who did arrive were engaging deeply, possibly with the binge eating disorder content that published July 17. Then July 18 and especially July 19 collapsed (8 and 2 sessions respectively), pulling the second-half average down hard. July 18-19 pattern likely reflects a weekend, but the depth of the drop (-89.7% on July 19 vs median) is steeper than a typical weekend would explain. Worth confirming whether there was a technical issue or a lapse in any scheduled distribution that weekend.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Investigate the July 18-19 floor.** Two sessions on July 19 is an extreme outlier, not just a slow Sunday. Before attributing it to weekend behavior, check whether any distribution channel (email newsletter, social posts, SMS) went dark that weekend, or whether there was a site availability issue. If it was a gap in distribution, that's a process fix, not a content fix.

2. **Set a GSC indexing checkpoint for the two new pages by July 28.** Both the binge eating disorder page and the ADHD page should start showing impressions within 1-2 weeks of publish. If either shows zero impressions by July 28, submit it for manual indexing in GSC and verify it's not accidentally noindexed. This is low-effort and high-stakes.

3. **Audit what's driving Organic Search at 32% this window.** That channel is running 7 points above baseline without a clear single cause. Pull the top landing pages from Organic Search in GA4 for this window and identify which pages are pulling sessions. If it's older eating-disorder or bariatric content, that's the cluster to prioritize for internal linking to the two new July pages.

4. **Build an internal link from the binge eating disorder page to the ADHD page and vice versa.** Both sit in adjacent topical territory (eating behaviors, mental health, body image). Cross-linking them now, while both are in early indexing, helps Google understand topical relationship and helps users who land on one find the other. This is a 20-minute task with a compounding return.

5. **Plan the next pitch to a referring edu domain.** BCC has 5 edu backlinks already -- a stronger profile than most bariatric counseling practices. That's a repeatable model. Identify which edu institution those links came from, find a second relevant department or resource page on a different edu domain, and draft an outreach note this week. Edu links are rare to earn but BCC has proven it can get them.
