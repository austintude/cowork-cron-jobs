# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-24

### The window in one sentence

The July 9--22 window delivered 255 sessions at a modest 18.2 per day average, with a mid-window spike on July 15--17 followed by a notable drop on July 18--19 that pulled the second half down 23% compared to the first. All of the significant page modifications shipped on July 23 -- the last day before this briefing -- so the real test of this content push is just beginning.

### Volume + composition

Over the 14-day window (July 9--22), the site logged 255 sessions, 234 users, and 764 pageviews, averaging 18.2 sessions and 16.7 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks both read zero for this window, which is almost certainly a data pipeline or property configuration issue rather than a true signal -- the 30-day efficacy rollup attributes 395,704 impressions and 1,696 clicks to tracked launches, so the underlying organic presence is real. Resolving the GSC data gap should be a near-term priority.

Channel composition diverged noticeably from BCC's baseline. Direct traffic led with 111 sessions (43.5%), but that is meaningfully below the expected ~60% -- suggesting Direct-driven audiences (email lists, repeat visitors, untagged social) were quieter than usual this period. Organic Search came in at 78 sessions (30.6%), running above the ~25% baseline, which is a modest positive signal. Referral at 38 sessions (14.9%) ran well above the ~10% baseline and is worth noting -- 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links (including 5 .edu links) in the backlink totals suggest that referral traffic has real institutional backing behind it. The small AI Assistant channel (4 sessions) is worth watching as a longer-term pattern. The above-baseline Organic Search share combined with above-baseline Referral share suggests the site is leaning on search and institutional links more than its own audience right now -- that is not a problem, but it does point to an opportunity to re-engage the Direct audience.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining on the surface: first-half sessions totaled 144, second-half totaled 111, a drop of 22.9%. Pageviews tell a similar story -- 387 in the first half versus 377 in the second, essentially flat on a per-session basis. The decline in sessions is largely explained by two anomalous low days: July 19 registered only 2 sessions (-89.7% vs the 28-day median) and July 18 hit 8 sessions (-59.0% vs median). Both days also showed sharp drops in new users and pageviews. These look like isolated disruptions -- possibly a tracking issue, a server hiccup, or a day with genuinely low traffic around a weekend -- rather than a structural trend downward. The days before and after (July 17: 24 sessions, July 20: 14, July 21: 17) recovered to near-normal levels.

The more meaningful pattern inside the halves is the July 15--17 cluster: session duration spiked to 245--490 seconds (versus a 28-day median around 110--123 seconds), pageviews hit 109 on July 16, and new users ran 28--60% above median. Something drove unusually engaged visitors to the site mid-window. That engagement quality is worth identifying -- if it was a specific referral source or content piece, replicating it matters more than the raw session count recovery.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit the July 15--17 engagement spike before the signal fades.** Average session duration hit 490 seconds on July 16 -- more than 8 minutes -- alongside a 75% user spike above median. Pull GA4 source/medium and landing page data for those three days specifically. If a referral source, social post, or specific page drove that engagement, you want to know what it was and do more of it.

2. **Prioritize CTR optimization on the nutritional counseling page this week.** With 3,854 impressions and zero clicks, /nutritional-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ is appearing in search results but not getting chosen. Rewrite the title tag and meta description with a clearer value proposition -- something that signals the practice's specific approach to people seeking support rather than a generic location-tagged service name.

3. **Set a GSC indexing check cadence for the July 23 cluster.** By July 30, the newly modified pages should show at least initial impression data. Flag any page that still reads zero impressions by that date for manual GSC inspection and indexing requests -- particularly therapy-for-eating-disorders, binge-eating-therapy, eating-disorder-iop, and food-addiction-treatment, which all started at zero.

4. **Investigate the GSC zero-data issue for this window's date range.** The 14-day window shows 0 impressions and 0 clicks across all days while the 30-day efficacy rollup shows nearly 400,000 impressions. That discrepancy suggests a property connection or date-range filtering problem in the reporting pipeline. Fixing this is a prerequisite for accurate trend analysis going forward.

5. **Consider a targeted outreach to one of the .edu referring domains.** Five .edu links is a stronger foundation than most comparable practices have. Identifying which institutions are already linking and pitching them a second, more specific link -- tied to the new eating disorder or bariatric support content -- is a low-effort, high-authority play that fits the referral over-performance already showing in this window.
