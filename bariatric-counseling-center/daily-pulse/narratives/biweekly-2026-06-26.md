# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-26

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending June 24, the site delivered 293 sessions at a 20.9 daily average -- a workable baseline -- but the window is trending down 21% (first half vs. second half), driven by a near-complete traffic collapse on June 20--21 that the June 22 spike only partially offset. The good news is that Organic Search is running above its expected share and a large batch of service pages shipped on June 24, giving the coming weeks real upside potential.

---

### Volume + composition

Total volume for the window: 293 sessions, 228 users, 1,215 pageviews, and -- critically -- zero GSC impressions or clicks recorded. That last figure is a data problem, not a ranking problem; the 30-day efficacy rollup shows 46,520 attributed GSC impressions and 96 clicks, which means the per-day GSC feed into this window is broken or not yet reconciled. Do not interpret the zero as a rankings collapse.

Channel composition is the most interesting story here. Organic Search drove 124 sessions (42% of total), well above its ~25% baseline. Direct came in at 108 sessions (37%), below its usual ~60% share. Referral was 43 sessions (15%), also above its ~10% norm. Organic Social contributed 16 sessions (5%) and Unassigned added 24 (8%). The Organic Search overrepresentation is a real signal -- it suggests ranking content is pulling in new visitors at a higher rate than usual, while the Direct underperformance likely means email or direct-navigation traffic was lighter (possibly fewer internal staff visits, a quieter week on any email sends, or both).

The Unassigned 8% is worth watching. Unassigned traffic in GA4 typically signals untagged sources -- links from PDF documents, some social referrals without UTM parameters, or direct app traffic. If you sent any untagged email campaigns or social posts during this window, that bucket is where they landed.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves comparison is declining: 164 sessions in the first half (June 9--17) versus 129 in the second half (June 18--24), a drop of 21.3%. That is a meaningful slide, not noise. However, the shape of the decline matters. Sessions were running strong in the first half -- peaking at 30 on June 11 and holding above 22 most days. The second half was distorted by two genuinely anomalous days: June 20 (8 sessions, -62% vs. the 28-day median) and June 21 (5 sessions, -76% vs. median). Those two days together likely represent a technical or access issue rather than an organic audience drop, because June 22 immediately rebounded to 40 sessions -- the single highest day of the window -- with pageviews of 223 (+230% vs. median) and average session duration of nearly 3.5 minutes.

If you strip the June 20--21 trough, the second half is roughly flat to the first half, not declining. The more honest read is: the window is steady with an unexplained two-day disruption, followed by a strong recovery. The June 22--23 session quality data (avg. session duration of 506 seconds on June 23, nearly 9 minutes) suggests highly engaged visitors, possibly people reading the new service pages in depth.

The headline launch event of this window is the June 24 batch push: 10 service pages were modified and pushed to production in a single day, covering the eating disorder cluster (/eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/, /eating-disorder-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/, /eating-disorder-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/), the GLP-1 support cluster (/semaglutide-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/, /ozempic-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/), and several core therapy and counseling pages (/bariatric-therapy-in-san-antonio-tx/, /bariatric-surgery-psychological-evaluation-in-san-antonio-tx/, /weight-management-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/, /online-weight-management-therapy-in-texas/, /virtual-emotional-eating-therapy-in-texas/, /holistic-weight-loss-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/). The June 25 commit confirms that 6 new pages from the June 18--25 SEO work were submitted to GSC. All of these pages are 1--2 days old relative to this briefing, so zero GSC impressions and minimal GA4 sessions is expected and appropriate. The /eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/ page already has 1 GA4 session at 2 days old, which is a fine early signal.

The 30-day efficacy rollup provides useful context: across the last 30 days, 12 pages launched and 71 were modified, generating 46,520 GSC impressions, 96 clicks, and 282 attributed GA4 sessions. Only 1 page is ranking in the top 10 and none in the top 3. That means the content is getting indexed and seen (46K impressions is not nothing), but it is not yet converting impressions to clicks at a meaningful rate. This is a CTR and SERP snippet problem more than an indexing problem. The 30-day click-through rate on attributed impressions is roughly 0.2%, which is very low even for informational pages in a competitive health vertical.

Backlink position: 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links total, 5 of which are .edu links. Five .edu backlinks is a legitimately strong signal for a local counseling practice -- those carry real authority weight. No new backlinks are documented as earned specifically within this window, but the .edu count is a baseline worth building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit GSC titles and meta descriptions for the June 24 batch before they fully index.** The 30-day data shows ~46K impressions but only 96 clicks, pointing to weak snippet performance across the site. The new pages are not yet indexed, which means you have a narrow window to optimize their title tags and meta descriptions for click appeal before Google caches what it finds. Prioritize the GLP-1 pages (/semaglutide-behavioral-support/, /ozempic-behavioral-support/) -- that search demand is high and competition is active.

2. **Investigate the June 20--21 traffic collapse before attributing it to anything strategic.** Two consecutive days at 5--8 sessions with near-zero engagement time (avg. session duration under 10 seconds) suggests a possible crawl or bot filtering event, a server access issue, or a GA4 tracking disruption. Ruling out a technical cause is worth 30 minutes in GA4's real-time debug view and Search Console's coverage report. If it was a tracking gap, the true second-half numbers are healthier than they appear.

3. **Build internal links between the new service pages before end of June.** The June 24 batch covers three distinct clusters (eating disorders, GLP-1 support, bariatric therapy). Right now those pages likely exist in relative isolation. Internal links from existing high-traffic pages (the homepage, the about page, any existing blog posts) to the new cluster pages will accelerate Googlebot discovery and pass relevance signals. This is the highest-leverage SEO task in the week ahead.

4. **Identify which of the 5 .edu backlinks is most authoritative and pursue a second link from that same institution.** A local counseling practice with .edu links has something most competitors do not. One follow-up -- a resource mention, a guest post, a counselor profile on a university wellness page -- could meaningfully move domain authority. This is a relationship play, not a cold outreach play, so start by identifying the contact you already have at that institution.

5. **Set a 14-day GSC monitoring checkpoint for the June 24 pages.** None of the new pages will show meaningful ranking data for at least 7--10 days. Mark your calendar for July 8 to pull first-impression data on all 10 pages. If any of the eating disorder or GLP-1 pages show impressions but low CTR by that date, that is your signal to revise the meta copy immediately rather than waiting for a full 30-day cycle.
