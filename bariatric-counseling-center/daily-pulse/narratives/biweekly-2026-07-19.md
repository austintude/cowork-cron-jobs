# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-07-19

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending July 17, the site delivered 302 sessions and 917 pageviews at a modest but functional pace -- then accelerated sharply in the final two days (July 16--17), driven by a batch of page modifications across the GLP-1 and bariatric service cluster and the fresh publication of the binge eating disorder post. GSC data is absent for the entire window, which is the single most pressing structural issue to resolve.

---

### Volume + composition

Over the 14-day window (July 4--17), the site recorded 302 sessions, 272 users, and 917 pageviews. That works out to roughly 21.6 sessions and 65.5 pageviews per day. GSC impressions and clicks both show zero for every day in the window -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline or property connection problem, not a collapse in organic visibility, because GA4 is showing healthy Organic Search traffic in parallel.

Channel composition tells an interesting story. Organic Search led at 119 sessions (39%), followed by Direct at 123 sessions (41%). That's nearly a reversal of BCC's typical baseline (~60% Direct / ~25% Organic Search). Organic Search pulling almost as much traffic as Direct suggests either a genuine ranking gain on one or more pages, or that Direct is underperforming -- possibly because an email or social campaign that usually pads Direct numbers didn't go out this period. Referral came in at 33 sessions (11%), slightly above the 10% baseline, which is worth watching. Email was 12 sessions (4%), Organic Social 10 (3%), and AI Assistant contributed 4 sessions -- a small but notable signal that BCC content is surfacing in conversational search tools.

The July 4 holiday weekend predictably suppressed the opening two days (9 and 14 sessions). Excluding that soft opening, the site's operating range for the window was roughly 19--32 sessions on weekdays, which is consistent with recent history.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window shows a modest overall decline: the first half (July 4--10) produced 161 sessions vs. the second half's 141, a 12.4% dip. However, that number is being pulled down by the July 12 collapse (just 6 sessions, a 69% drop vs. the 28-day median) -- a Sunday anomaly that likely reflects normal weekend behavior compounded by post-holiday quietness, not a structural problem. If you remove July 12 as an outlier, the second half looks considerably healthier.

More importantly, the final two days of the window (July 16: 29 sessions, 109 pageviews; July 17: 24 sessions, 82 pageviews) are the two strongest engagement days of the entire period. Session duration on July 16 was 491 seconds -- a 340% spike above the 28-day median -- which strongly suggests visitors who landed via the modified GLP-1 and bariatric pages were actually reading, not bouncing. The trend going into the current week is accelerating, not declining, and the July 12 dip should not be mistaken for a pattern.

### Launches + efficacy

The headline activity in this window was a coordinated batch of page modifications published July 16, covering at least 14 service pages across the GLP-1 cluster (Ozempic, Mounjaro/Zepbound, GLP-1 behavioral support, GLP-1 weight regain counseling), the bariatric cluster (bariatric therapy, bariatric therapist, bariatric surgery counseling, psychological evaluation), and the eating-disorder-adjacent cluster (ADHD and emotional eating, night eating syndrome, anxiety and weight). The same day also saw sitemap touches on `/category/eating-disorders/` and `/author/shamilton/`. The July 16 traffic and engagement spike is directly correlated with this push -- 29 sessions, 109 pageviews, and that 491-second average session duration all landed the same day the modifications went live. That's meaningful signal that the updated content is resonating.

The `/adhd-and-emotional-eating-in-san-antonio-tx/` page is the early standout from the modified group: 211 GSC impressions and a 2.8% CTR (6 clicks) in just three days is a solid opening. The `/night-eating-syndrome-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/` page has 537 impressions and a 0.9% CTR (5 clicks) -- good impression volume but a CTR that suggests the title or meta description may not be converting searchers. The `/author/shamilton/` page is an unexpected performer: 2,356 GSC impressions and 7 clicks in three days for an author archive page is noteworthy and suggests an author-entity or E-E-A-T signal may be building around that profile.

The freshly published binge eating disorder post (`/eating-disorders/binge-eating-disorder-the-symptoms-causes-and-effective-treatment-options/`) went live July 17 and has already pulled 2 GA4 sessions with zero GSC impressions -- entirely normal at two days old. It's in indexing warmup and needs 10--14 more days before drawing conclusions. No new backlinks were earned in this specific window, but the existing profile (4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, 5 .edu links) is a meaningful foundation for a site at this stage.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Investigate and fix the GSC data gap first.** Zero impressions and zero clicks across all 14 days is not organic performance -- it's a broken connection between the GSC property and whatever pipeline feeds this briefing. Until that's resolved, you cannot assess keyword rankings, CTR performance, or indexing status with any confidence. Verify the GSC property is verified, the data connector is authenticated, and the correct property (domain vs. URL prefix) is selected.

2. **Prioritize the night eating syndrome page's snippet.** `/night-eating-syndrome-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/` is generating 537 impressions in three days but only a 0.9% CTR. That impression volume at low CTR suggests it's ranking in a visible position but the title tag or meta description isn't compelling enough to earn the click. Rewrite the meta description this week to lead with the person's felt experience -- not the clinical term -- and test whether CTR improves over the following 7--10 days.

3. **Build internal links from the binge eating disorder post into the eating-disorder cluster.** The new post at `/eating-disorders/binge-eating-disorder-the-symptoms-causes-and-effective-treatment-options/` is sitting in a category that already has sitemap visibility. Add contextual internal links from that post to the night eating syndrome page and the ADHD and emotional eating page -- and vice versa -- to pass authority within the cluster before Google fully indexes the new post.

4. **Investigate what drove the Organic Search share spike.** Organic Search at 39% of sessions is well above BCC's 25% baseline. Pull GSC (once reconnected) or GA4 landing page data to identify which specific pages or queries are generating that lift. If it's coming from one or two pages, those pages deserve immediate attention -- internal links, schema review, and possible content expansion.

5. **Develop the `/author/shamilton/` entity signal intentionally.** An author archive page generating 2,356 impressions in three days is an unusual signal for a local counseling practice. This likely reflects Google evaluating author authority as part of E-E-A-T for the eating disorder and bariatric content. Add a substantive bio, credentials, and links to published posts on that author page -- and ensure every post in the eating-disorder cluster is attributed to that author -- to strengthen the signal Google is already responding to.
