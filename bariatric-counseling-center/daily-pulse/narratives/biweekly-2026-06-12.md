# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-12

### The window in one sentence

Over this 13-day window (May 17 -- June 11), sessions trended upward 19.7% from the first half to the second half, with 301 total sessions driven primarily by Direct and Organic Search traffic at roughly expected ratios. A concentrated batch of 10 content updates published June 11 is too fresh to evaluate but represents the most significant publishing day in the window.

---

### Volume + composition

The site logged 301 sessions, 253 users, and 1,208 pageviews across 13 tracked days, averaging 23.2 sessions and 19.5 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks are reporting at zero for the entire window -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline issue rather than a real signal, and it makes it impossible to assess organic search performance from a ranking or click-through angle during this period. That gap needs to be investigated before any SEO efficacy conclusions can be drawn.

Channel composition held close to BCC's historical baseline: Direct at 150 sessions (roughly 50%) ran slightly below the expected 60%, while Organic Search at 104 sessions (roughly 35%) ran above the expected 25%. Referral contributed 36 sessions (12%), Organic Social was minimal at 8, and Unassigned was negligible at 5. The Organic Search overperformance relative to baseline is a positive signal -- it suggests existing content is pulling people in from search, even without GSC data to confirm which pages. The slightly lower Direct share is worth monitoring; it could reflect a shift in audience mix or a quieter period for internal promotions and referrals.

No unusual channel spikes suggest untagged email or social campaigns during this window. The traffic is reasonably organic and mixed, which is a healthy pattern.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is accelerating. The first half (May 17-21 and May 31) generated 137 sessions; the second half (June 1-11) generated 164 sessions -- a 19.7% increase. Pageviews show an even steeper shift: 472 in the first half versus 736 in the second half, a 56% jump. That pageviews-per-session ratio climbing in the second half suggests visitors arriving more recently are exploring more pages per visit, which is a meaningful quality signal, not just a volume one.

The most prominent single-day anomaly was June 3, where sessions hit 42 (115% above the 28-day median) and pageviews reached 230 (235% above median) -- with session duration also elevated at 248 seconds. No launch is logged for June 3, so that spike likely reflects a referral event, a social share, or a direct send that isn't tagged. It is worth investigating what drove that day specifically, because if it's replicable, it's a growth lever. June 8-9 showed a brief dip in new users and sessions, but June 10-11 recovered with strong pageview and session duration numbers. The pattern going into June 11 looks solid.

Everything that shipped in this window landed on June 11 -- one day ago as of this briefing. That means no efficacy data is available yet, and none should be expected. What matters right now is that this was a high-output day: five service pages were updated (including `/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/food-addiction-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/therapy-for-depression-and-weight-loss/`, and the GLP-1 medications page at `/understanding-weight-loss-medications-wegovy-ozempic-zepbound-and-mounjaro/`), along with five blog posts spanning binge eating disorder, the BCC IOP program, Ozempic and eating disorders, binge eating in men, and the binge-eating-vs-emotional-eating distinction. That cluster covers clinically adjacent topics in a way that could support both ranking and internal-linking depth if the pages are wired together well.

The GLP-1 medications page pulled 4 sessions on its first day, and the bariatric therapist page pulled 2 -- small numbers but immediate engagement on day one is a reasonable early signal, likely from Direct traffic. The binge eating cluster posts each pulled 1-2 sessions. None of these have GSC data yet, and given the pipeline issue reporting zero impressions sitewide, it will take additional days before ranking trajectory becomes visible.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 0 rankings in top 3 or top 10, and 0 GSC impressions attributed to any launch -- which again points to the GSC data gap rather than a true absence of search visibility. With 31 pages launched and 97 modified in the last 30 days, some of that work should be surfacing in GSC. Resolving the data connection is a prerequisite for evaluating whether this volume of production is translating into rankings.

On backlinks: BCC currently holds 4 referring domains and 12 inbound links total, with 5 edu links -- no new links are recorded in this window. The edu link count is a meaningful asset worth building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data gap first.** Zero impressions and zero clicks across 13 days sitewide is not a ranking problem -- it's a reporting problem. Before evaluating whether any content is working from an organic search perspective, confirm the GSC property is connected, the correct view is selected, and the data is flowing. This is a prerequisite for almost every other strategic decision.

2. **Build internal links across the June 11 binge eating cluster now, not later.** The five new/updated posts (`/binge-eating-in-men-why-40-percent-of-cases-missed/`, `/binge-eating-disorder-ozempic-what-nobody-tells-you/`, `/day-inside-bcc-binge-eating-iop/`, `/binge-eating-vs-emotional-eating-clinical-differences/`, and the pre-surgery screening post) should be linking to each other and to the `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/` service page. Clusters rank and convert as systems, not as isolated pages. The window to get this right is the first two weeks after publication.

3. **Investigate the June 3 traffic spike.** Sessions more than doubled the median and pageviews nearly tripled, with no launch logged. Something sent people to the site that day -- a referral, a social share, a newsletter, or a partner. Identifying the source could reveal a repeatable distribution channel.

4. **Prioritize a second edu backlink in the next two weeks.** With 5 edu links already in place, BCC has demonstrated it can earn links from academic or institutional sources. That's the hardest part. Now it's worth identifying which edu domain(s) those links came from and pitching a second link from the same institution or a comparable one -- a resource mention, a citation on a student health or counseling page, or a partner listing.

5. **Pause new page production for one week and let the June 11 batch breathe.** With 10 pieces published in a single day and GSC data currently dark, shipping more content before the pipeline is confirmed and the existing cluster is internally linked is likely to dilute focus without adding measurable value. A week spent on infrastructure -- linking, GSC verification, and indexing requests -- will compound the June 11 work rather than compete with it.
