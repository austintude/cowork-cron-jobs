### Volume + composition

Over the 7-day window, the site logged 141 sessions, 128 users, and 428 pageviews -- averaging 20.1 sessions and 18.3 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks show as zero in the window-level aggregate, but individual page records in the launch data show real impression counts (more on that below), which suggests a data-attribution lag rather than a true search blackout.

Channel composition came in close to baseline on Direct (70 sessions, roughly 50%) but ran heavier than expected on Organic Search (39 sessions, ~28%) and lighter on Referral (22 sessions, ~16%). Email contributed 5 sessions and AI Assistant contributed 2 -- a small but worth-noting signal that BCC content is surfacing in at least some AI-driven discovery flows. The elevated Referral share relative to the ~10% baseline likely reflects the 4 referring domains in the backlink data; 5 of those 12 inbound links are from .edu sources, which tend to produce engaged, low-bounce sessions.

The 6 "Unassigned" sessions are a low-grade ongoing concern -- untagged links from social posts or email campaigns are the most common cause, and at small site volumes, even a handful of misattributed sessions distorts channel reads.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

This window is clearly accelerating. The first three days (July 11-13) totaled 48 sessions and 133 pageviews; the final four days (July 14-17) totaled 93 sessions and 295 pageviews -- a 93.8% jump in session volume and a 121.8% jump in pageviews. That's not a flat trend with a single spike day inflating the number; July 15, 16, and 17 all ran at or above median, with July 16 (29 sessions, 109 pageviews) being the standout. Average session duration also recovered sharply after two unusually low days on July 11-12 -- hitting 490 seconds on July 16 and 245 seconds on July 15, both well above the 28-day median.

The July 12 dip (6 sessions, 22 pageviews, flagged CRITICAL across every metric) is the one blemish in an otherwise strong close to the week. A Sunday dip of that magnitude usually points to a data collection gap or a genuinely slow day with no traffic driver -- not a structural problem. The recovery the following day was immediate and full, which supports the "low-traffic Sunday" read over anything more concerning.

### Launches + efficacy

July 16 was the productive center of this window. A batch of 13+ page modifications went live covering the full GLP-1 cluster (Ozempic, Mounjaro/Zepbound, GLP-1 behavioral support, GLP-1 regain counseling), the bariatric services cluster (therapy, therapist, surgery counseling, psych eval), and two eating-disorder-adjacent pages (ADHD and eating, night eating syndrome). That volume of updates in a single day is significant -- it tells Google there's active editorial activity across a topically coherent set of pages, which can accelerate re-crawl cycles.

Of those July 16 pages, two are already showing meaningful GSC impressions at the individual level despite the aggregate showing zeros: the `/adhd-and-emotional-eating-in-san-antonio-tx/` page has 211 impressions and a 2.8% CTR (6 clicks in 3 days), and `/night-eating-syndrome-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/` has 537 impressions and 5 clicks (0.9% CTR). The ADHD/eating page's 2.8% CTR is genuinely strong for a page this new -- that's a title and snippet that's resonating. The `/author/shamilton/` sitemap touch is pulling 2,356 impressions with a 0.3% CTR, which is a lot of visibility for an author archive page; the low CTR there is expected and not a concern.

The binge eating disorder post (`/eating-disorders/binge-eating-disorder-the-symptoms-causes-and-effective-treatment-options/`) published July 17 with 2 sessions already and zero GSC impressions -- that's textbook indexing warmup behavior at 2 days live. It's too early to evaluate. The `/category/eating-disorders/` sitemap touch has 69 impressions and zero clicks, which is worth flagging: category pages almost never produce strong CTR on their own, but 69 impressions in 3 days suggests Google is serving it and nobody is clicking. That's not a crisis, but the category page's title and meta description are worth reviewing if it keeps accumulating impressions without clicks.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 383,998 GSC impressions and 1,511 clicks attributed across all launched content -- with zero pages currently ranking in the top 10. That last number is the one to keep an eye on. It suggests BCC has real search visibility (impressions are not trivial) but hasn't yet broken into positions where click-through becomes reliable. Closing that gap is the medium-term content objective.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Internal-link the July 16 GLP-1 and bariatric page updates to each other and to the binge eating disorder post.** These pages were modified in the same session but may not yet reference each other. Tight internal linking across topically related pages reinforces cluster authority and gives Google a cleaner crawl path -- especially important when none of these pages are yet ranking in the top 10.

2. **Audit the `/category/eating-disorders/` title tag and meta description.** It has 69 impressions in 3 days with zero clicks. If this page accumulates more impressions without clicks, that signals a snippet problem -- either the title is too generic or it's competing with the individual posts it contains. A distinct, value-forward meta description could move the needle on CTR before a ranking position is even established.

3. **Investigate the zero-GSC aggregate against individual page impression data.** The window-level GSC totals show zero, but individual launch records show real impressions on at least three pages. This is likely a property-level filtering or attribution window issue, but it's worth confirming GSC is properly connected to the right property so rollup data is trustworthy going forward.

4. **Identify the source of the 6 Unassigned sessions and the 5 Email sessions.** At this site's traffic volume, a handful of mislabeled sessions meaningfully distorts the channel picture. Check whether any email sends or social posts went out without UTM parameters in this window, and tag them retroactively where possible.

5. **Let the binge eating disorder post sit for 10-14 days before any revisions.** It has 2 sessions already, which is a healthy start at 2 days live. The temptation to modify it early is understandable, but repeated edits in the indexing-warmup phase can delay GSC pickup. Plan its first structured internal-linking pass and any copy refinements for the week of July 28 instead.
