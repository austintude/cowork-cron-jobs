# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-15

### The window in one sentence

Over the 13 tracked days between May 17 and June 11, sessions grew 19.7% from the first half to the second half of the window, with June 3 and June 11 producing the two highest-traffic days in the dataset. The site is showing clear upward momentum in both volume and engagement depth, but GSC data is entirely absent -- meaning organic search performance is a blind spot right now.

---

### Volume + composition

The window produced 301 sessions from 253 users across 1,208 pageviews, averaging 23.2 sessions and 19.5 users per day. Those numbers are modest in absolute terms, but the daily shape matters more than the total: the site had multiple days well above baseline (37 sessions on May 19, 42 on June 3, 30 on June 11), and the average pageviews-per-session across the window comes out to roughly 4.0 -- which suggests people who land are actually moving through the site.

Channel composition came in at roughly 50% Direct (150 sessions), 35% Organic Search (104), 12% Referral (36), and a small slice of Organic Social (8) and Unassigned (5). Compared to BCC's expected baseline of 60% Direct / 25% Organic / 10% Referral, Organic Search is punching above its weight and Direct is running lower than usual. The elevated Organic share is worth watching -- it suggests content may be earning traffic, even though GSC impressions are showing zero across the entire window (more on that below). Referral at 12% is also slightly elevated, which could indicate a linking partner drove some traffic during this period.

The GSC zero-across-the-board reading is almost certainly a data pipeline issue rather than a real-world signal. The site is clearly receiving Organic Search sessions (104 of them), so impressions and clicks are happening -- they're just not being recorded. Resolving the GSC feed should be a near-term priority before drawing any conclusions about organic visibility.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is accelerating. The first half (May 17-21 + May 31, six days) produced 137 sessions and 472 pageviews. The second half (June 1-11, seven days) produced 164 sessions and 736 pageviews -- that's a 19.7% session lift and a 55.9% pageview lift. The pageview growth outpacing session growth is meaningful: people arriving in the second half were reading more per visit, not just landing and leaving.

The anomaly pattern reinforces this. June 3 triggered five simultaneous CRITICAL anomalies -- sessions at +115%, users at +117%, pageviews at +236%, all against 28-day medians. June 11 followed with another CRITICAL pageview spike (+154%) and a strong session day (+39.5%). The back half of the window also shows a consistent pattern of elevated session duration (June 8: +134%, June 9: +143%, June 10: +327%, June 11: +101%), which suggests the content refreshes from June 11 may have had an immediate same-day impact on how long people stayed. The mid-window dip around June 8-9 (sessions dropping to 14-17) is normal weekend/low-traffic variation and does not interrupt the broader upward trajectory.

### Launches + efficacy

The heaviest publish day in this window was June 11, when BCC pushed out four page modifications and seven post modifications in a single batch. The pages touched were high-intent service pages -- `/bariatric-therapist-in-san-antonio-tx/`, `/therapy-for-depression-and-weight-loss/`, `/food-addiction-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/`, and `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/` -- alongside a mix of posts covering binge eating, Ozempic, eating disorder IOP, and a hydration piece. That same day, June 11, was also the second-highest session and pageview day in the window (30 sessions, 178 pageviews), which strongly suggests the published content drove same-day Direct or social traffic before search engines had time to index anything.

At four days post-launch, the early leaders in the June 11 batch are the GLP-1 medication page (`/understanding-weight-loss-medications-wegovy-ozempic-zepbound-and-mounjaro/`, 4 sessions), the bariatric therapist page (2 sessions), and the binge eating in men post (2 sessions). Everything else in the batch is at zero or one session. That's expected at four days -- none of these have had meaningful time to rank -- but the medication page pulling the most early traffic is consistent with how high consumer interest in Ozempic/Wegovy content is right now. That page is worth tracking closely over the next two weeks.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 31 pages launched and 97 modifications across 200 tracked events, with 218 GA4 sessions attributed to launch activity. Zero GSC impressions attributed and zero top-3 or top-10 rankings recorded -- again, almost certainly a GSC feed problem rather than a real ranking picture. The backlink profile stands at 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, and 5 .edu links with zero competitor links. The .edu count is a genuine asset worth building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Fix the GSC data feed before anything else.** Every strategic decision about organic search -- which pages are ranking, which need refreshing, whether the June 11 batch is getting indexed -- is blocked until GSC impressions are flowing correctly. This is the single highest-leverage infrastructure task in the system right now. Identify whether the issue is a property verification problem, a reporting API key issue, or a data ingestion error, and resolve it this week.

2. **Build internal links into the June 11 binge eating cluster.** The posts covering binge eating vs. emotional eating, binge eating in men, binge eating and Ozempic, and the IOP day-in-the-life piece are all live but almost certainly not yet interconnected. A deliberate internal linking pass -- service pages pointing to relevant posts and posts pointing to each other and to `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/` -- will help Google understand the cluster and likely improve CTR once rankings surface.

3. **Watch the GLP-1 medication page as a leading indicator.** `/understanding-weight-loss-medications-wegovy-ozempic-zepbound-and-mounjaro/` is already the top early-traffic getter in the June 11 batch, and search demand for this topic remains very high. In the next 7-14 days, check whether it earns any GSC impressions once the feed is fixed, and consider whether it needs a stronger internal link from the homepage or a primary service page to accelerate indexing.

4. **Investigate the June 3 and June 11 traffic spikes for source.** Both days produced anomalous sessions and pageviews well above the 28-day median. If these spikes came from an untagged email send, a social post, or a referring partner, that source is worth identifying and repeating. If they correlate with the publish activity itself (content going live and triggering platform notifications), that's also useful to know and document as a repeatable pattern.

5. **Develop a plan to grow the referring domain count beyond four.** BCC has 5 .edu backlinks, which is a meaningful signal of authority, but the total domain count is very low. Given that Referral traffic is already running slightly above baseline in this window, there is likely an active referring source worth identifying. A short outreach plan targeting San Antonio-area health organizations, bariatric surgery practices, or university health programs -- modeled on whatever .edu relationship already exists -- could meaningfully expand both domain authority and referral sessions over the next quarter.
