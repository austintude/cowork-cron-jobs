# Biweekly briefing -- 2026-06-15

### The window in one sentence

Over the 14 days ending June 14, the site logged 323 sessions at a daily average of 23.1 -- volume that is essentially flat but with a meaningful engagement signal buried inside it: average session duration spiked repeatedly and significantly in the second half, suggesting the binge-eating content cluster launched June 11 is attracting people who actually read. GSC data is fully absent for this window, which is the most important problem to solve before any organic-search conclusions can be drawn.

---

### Volume + composition

The 14-day window produced 323 sessions, 269 users, and 1,268 pageviews. Per-day averages came in at 23.1 sessions and 19.2 users. Those numbers are modest but consistent with a site still building organic authority. Two days -- June 3 (42 sessions, 230 pageviews) and June 11 (30 sessions, 178 pageviews) -- drove a disproportionate share of the window's pageview total and account for why the pageview-to-session ratio of roughly 3.9 looks healthy: people are multi-page browsing on those spike days, not bouncing after one view.

Channel composition came in at 50% Direct (161 sessions), 36% Organic Search (115 sessions), 11% Referral (36 sessions), and the remainder split between Organic Social and Unassigned. That is a meaningful departure from BCC's 60/25/10 baseline. Organic Search at 36% is notably above baseline, which would ordinarily be a ranking win worth celebrating -- but with GSC impressions showing zero across all 14 days, that attribution is suspect. The most likely explanation is a GA4/GSC property mismatch or a GSC data pipeline failure. Direct at 50% (below the 60% baseline) combined with a higher-than-expected Organic share could also reflect untagged social or email traffic being misclassified. Both warrant a quick property audit before drawing conclusions about search performance.

Referral at 11% is slightly above baseline and worth watching. The backlinks data shows 12 inbound links from 4 referring domains, including 5 .edu links, which is a meaningful signal for a specialty healthcare site. If referral sessions are coming from those .edu sources, that is the highest-value traffic acquisition in the window.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is technically accelerating, but barely. First half (May 17 -- June 1) generated 158 sessions across 7 days; second half (June 2 -- June 14) generated 165 sessions across 7 days. That is a 4.4% session gain -- too small to call a genuine momentum shift but at least not a decline. Sessions alone undersell what the second half actually delivered: 727 pageviews vs 541 in the first half, a 34% jump. People arriving in the second half are viewing significantly more pages per visit.

The more interesting signal is what the anomaly data shows about engagement quality. Average session duration hit CRITICAL-level spikes on June 8 (+134%), June 9 (+143%), June 10 (+327%), and June 11 (+101%) -- all in the second half, and all landing well above the 28-day median. That pattern lines up directly with the binge-eating cluster pages going live June 11 and suggests people are finding content they want to spend time with. Flat session counts with rising depth and duration is a reasonable early-stage organic content profile: Google is not sending volume yet, but the people arriving are engaged. The concern is that June 9 also showed sessions and users both down 33-37% from median on the same day duration spiked -- a pattern that could indicate a small number of highly engaged people rather than broad audience growth.

### Launches + efficacy

The most consequential launch activity in the window was a cluster of binge-eating content published or touched on June 11. That single date accounts for 10 separate sitemap touches and 3 page modifications. The content titles are specific and well-targeted: `/binge-eating-disorder-ozempic-what-nobody-tells-you/`, `/binge-eating-in-men-why-40-percent-of-cases-missed/`, `/day-inside-bcc-binge-eating-iop/`, `/pre-bariatric-surgery-binge-eating-screening/`, and `/binge-eating-vs-emotional-eating-clinical-differences/`. The three service pages modified the same day -- Bariatric Therapist in San Antonio, Therapy for Depression and Weight Loss, and Food Addiction Treatment in San Antonio -- suggest a coordinated content-plus-services push, which is the right structural move.

Early session counts on the June 11 pages are thin but not zero: the IOP day-in-the-life page and the Ozempic/binge-eating page each have 3 GA4 sessions at 4 days old; the men's binge-eating page has 2. For content launched 4 days ago with no GSC impressions yet visible (likely a data gap rather than indexing failure), those numbers are neither alarming nor impressive. The real test is whether impressions appear in GSC over the next 10-14 days. The June 3 session spike (42 sessions, 230 pageviews, +235% above median) predates the June 11 launch cluster and lacks an obvious attribution in the launch ledger -- worth investigating whether a social share or email mention drove that day.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 24 pages launched and 77 pages modified in the broader period, with 256 GA4 sessions attributed across all launched content. Zero top-3 or top-10 GSC rankings are recorded, though again the GSC data gap makes this unreliable. The 5 .edu backlinks in the backlinks total are the most durable asset created in this window: .edu referring domains are difficult to earn and have lasting authority value for a health and wellness site in a competitive local market.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Audit the GSC data connection before next week's content decisions.** Fourteen consecutive days of zero impressions is a pipeline failure, not a performance failure. Until GSC is flowing correctly, ranking decisions, keyword targeting, and efficacy reporting are all running blind. Verify the property matches the GA4 domain exactly and manually pull a GSC report for the June 11 URLs to confirm they are being crawled.

2. **Build internal links from the modified service pages into the June 11 cluster.** The Bariatric Therapist, Food Addiction Treatment, and Therapy for Depression pages were all touched June 11 -- that timing suggests they were meant to connect with the binge-eating content. Confirm those service pages have contextual links pointing to `/binge-eating-disorder-ozempic-what-nobody-tells-you/`, `/binge-eating-in-men-why-40-percent-of-cases-missed/`, and `/eating-disorder-iop-in-san-antonio-tx/`. Internal linking is the lever available right now since external authority takes time.

3. **Investigate the June 3 spike.** Forty-two sessions and 230 pageviews on a single day with no corresponding launch in the ledger suggests an untagged traffic source -- likely a social post, an email, or a referral that isn't tagged. Identify what drove that day, because whatever it was worked and is worth repeating intentionally.

4. **Prioritize outreach to the .edu referrers already in the backlinks portfolio.** Five .edu links from 4 referring domains is a strong start. The pattern that earns a second link from an existing .edu partner is worth understanding: find which institution(s) are linking, identify the context, and draft a specific pitch for a second mention or resource link. This is a higher-leverage play than prospecting cold .edu targets.

5. **Hold new page production for one week and request indexing for the June 11 cluster.** Fourteen new URLs in a single sitemap push is a lot for Google to process at once. Rather than adding more pages, use next week to manually request indexing via Google Search Console for the 10 June 11 sitemap-touch URLs and monitor crawl status. Indexing confirmation is the prerequisite for any ranking data -- and ranking data is what will tell you whether the content quality matches the engagement quality you're already seeing on-site.
