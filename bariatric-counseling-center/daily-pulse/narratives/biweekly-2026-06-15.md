### Volume + composition

The 14-day window produced 323 sessions, 269 users, and 1,268 pageviews against a per-day average of 23.1 sessions and 19.2 users. Pageviews per session averaged about 3.9, which is healthy -- people landing and exploring, not bouncing immediately. GSC impressions and clicks are zeroed out across the entire window, which is a data problem worth confirming (a misconfigured GSC property or a reporting lag) rather than a signal that organic search stopped working.

Channel composition came in at approximately 50% Direct, 36% Organic Search, and 11% Referral -- with Organic Social and Unassigned making up the remaining 3%. That Organic Search share (36%) is meaningfully above BCC's expected ~25% baseline, which is a positive sign even without GSC confirmation. Direct at 50% is below the expected 60%, suggesting either that known-audience traffic (email lists, social regulars) was quieter than usual this stretch, or that some of what would normally be tagged Direct is now attributable to Organic. Referral at 11% is tracking close to the expected 10% baseline and holding steady.

The elevated Organic Search share during a window with zero GSC data is the tension to watch. If GSC data comes back online and shows impressions climbing, that 36% Organic share will make more sense. If GSC comes back flat, the source of that traffic is worth investigating -- it's possible some Referral traffic is being bucketed into Organic due to missing UTM parameters on external links.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is gently accelerating. The first half (May 17 through June 1) produced 158 sessions and 541 pageviews. The second half (June 2 through June 14) produced 165 sessions and 727 pageviews -- a 4.4% sessions lift and a 34% pageviews lift. The pageviews gap is the more meaningful number here. It suggests that second-half visitors were reading more pages per visit, not just arriving in slightly higher numbers. That pattern often follows new content landing: people find one page and click through to related content.

The session duration anomalies reinforce this. From June 8 through June 11, average session duration was running 100-327% above the 28-day median on multiple days. That's not a volume story -- it's an engagement story. People who came during the second half of this window stayed significantly longer. The flip side is that June 8 and June 9 also showed new-user counts down 35-40% from median, so the longer sessions were likely return visitors or highly motivated first-timers -- not a broad new audience arriving. The window is trending in the right direction, but the growth is narrow rather than broad-based.
