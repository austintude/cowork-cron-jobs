### Trend (first half vs second half)

On the surface, the halves split looks stable: 62 sessions in the first three days (July 13--15) versus 63 in the final four (July 16--19), a 1.6% change that is statistically flat. But that number hides a significant shape problem. The second half opened with the two strongest days of the window -- 29 sessions on July 16 and 24 on July 17 -- and then fell off sharply: 8 sessions on July 18 and just 2 on July 19. Pageviews tell the same story more vividly: 109 and 82 on those peak days, then 28 and 4.

The July 18--19 drop is steep enough (sessions down 59% and 90% respectively against the 28-day median) that it warrants a tracking check before treating it as a real audience behavior signal. A Saturday/Sunday pattern could explain some of this if BCC's audience skews weekday, but a drop to 2 sessions on a Sunday is more extreme than typical weekend softness. Confirm that GA4 tracking was firing correctly on those two days. If tracking was intact, the pattern suggests the mid-week surge was event-driven -- possibly tied to the binge eating disorder post going live -- and then dissipated quickly without a sustained source pulling traffic forward.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Verify July 18--19 tracking before treating the drop as real.** Pull the GA4 real-time or DebugView logs for those two days and confirm the tracking tag was firing. A 90% drop to 2 sessions on a Sunday is possible, but it is also consistent with a tag outage or a caching/CDN issue that blocked the analytics script. If tracking was intact, then the audience is not sustaining engagement beyond the initial content push -- which is a different problem requiring a different solution.

2. **Request GSC indexing for the July 16--17 launches.** The five modified service pages and the binge eating disorder post should all be submitted through Google Search Console's URL Inspection tool if that has not happened already. The sitemap touch on July 16 helps, but manual submission speeds up the crawl queue for pages you want indexed quickly.

3. **Pull individual GSC data for the five service pages modified on July 16.** The GLP-1 regain and Mounjaro/Zepbound pages in particular are targeting high-intent queries in a competitive space right now. Knowing their pre- and post-modification impression and ranking data will tell you whether those edits moved the needle or need another pass.

4. **Investigate the Referral channel spike.** Referral running at 19% -- nearly double the expected baseline -- suggests either a new linking source came online or an existing one had an unusually active week. Identifying which domains sent those 24 referral sessions is worth 15 minutes in GA4. If it is a high-authority site linking to BCC organically, that relationship deserves attention.

5. **Build an internal linking plan for the eating disorder cluster before the binge eating disorder post has been live two weeks.** The post is new and will need time to rank, but internal links from existing indexed pages (especially the `/category/eating-disorders/` hub and any related service pages) will help Google understand its place in the site architecture. This is the kind of structural support that separates posts that plateau at position 15 from posts that climb to position 5.
