Referral traffic (4 sessions) is low but in the expected range. With 4 referring domains and 12 total inbound links including 5 from .edu properties, there is authority sitting in the backlink profile that should continue to pay dividends as the new pages mature.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Submit the May 12 pages for indexing via Google Search Console's URL Inspection tool.** All six pages are old enough (7--8 days) that manual indexing requests are appropriate. Do not wait for passive crawling -- requesting indexing directly accelerates the timeline by several days and gives you a clean timestamp to measure from.

2. **Audit internal linking across the May 12 cluster.** The pages are live but there is no evidence yet that they are being linked to from high-traffic pages on the site. If the homepage, the primary services page, and any high-session blog content do not link to these six pages, that is likely suppressing both crawl frequency and ranking speed. This is a one-time structural task that compounds over time.

3. **Investigate the May 18 session-duration drop.** The 44-second average on May 18 versus 373 seconds on May 17 is a swing so wide it suggests two different types of visitors on those days (or a tracking anomaly). Pull the May 18 GA4 session breakdown by landing page to see whether a specific page or traffic source is inflating the bounce rate. If it is a content or UX issue on one of the new pages, that is fixable now before rankings solidify.

4. **Develop a second outreach target for .edu backlinks.** The current profile has 5 .edu links, which is strong for this niche. Identify which institution(s) those links come from and map out one additional target -- a UTSA department, a community college counseling resource page, or a Texas health initiative -- and draft a low-ask outreach angle. Edu links in this space are rare and durable, and one more would measurably lift domain authority for the new pages.

5. **Set a review checkpoint for the May 12 pages at 21 days (around June 2).** Pages in competitive-but-local queries like "bariatric therapist san antonio" and "therapy for eating disorders san antonio" typically need 3--6 weeks to settle into stable SERP positions. Mark June 2 as the first meaningful evaluation date. If GSC impressions are still zero by then, that is a content or indexing issue worth diagnosing -- but acting on zero data before that date risks optimizing prematurely.
