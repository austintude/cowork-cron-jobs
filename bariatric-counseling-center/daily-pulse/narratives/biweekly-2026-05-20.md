The data picture is thin (only 3 days, no GSC signal yet), so the story here is directional rather than conclusive.

### Volume + composition

Across May 17--19, the site logged 73 sessions, 60 users, and 272 pageviews, averaging 24.3 sessions and 20 users per day. GSC impressions and clicks are both zero for this window, which almost certainly reflects the standard 3--5 day lag in Search Console data rather than an actual indexing problem -- expect those numbers to populate within the next few days.

Channel composition skewed toward Direct at 59% (43 sessions), with Organic Search pulling 34% (25 sessions) and Referral at 5% (4 sessions). That Direct number is right in line with BCC's ~60% baseline, so no flag there. What is worth noting is Organic Search running slightly above its expected 25% share. That could mean a ranking improvement is quietly building, but without GSC data to confirm it, that's a hypothesis to revisit once impressions populate.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves data wasn't populated in this window, which makes sense -- 3 days doesn't split cleanly into two meaningful halves. What the daily series does show is a clear upward line: 12 sessions on May 17, 24 on May 18, 37 on May 19. That's a 3x climb across three consecutive days, with May 19 landing 85% above the 28-day median and 61% above the same day last year.

May 17 was the outlier in the other direction -- sessions down 40%, new users down 43% vs. the 28-day median, though session duration on that day was a striking 373 seconds (225% above median), suggesting a small number of highly engaged visitors rather than a true traffic problem. The window overall is accelerating, not declining, but three days is too short to call this a sustained trend. It needs another 4--5 days of data before that trajectory is meaningful.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything in this window is internal tooling -- all 10+ commits logged in the window are dashboard and daily-pulse infrastructure work (markdown renderer fixes, briefing tabs, rollup logic, valence-aware colors, GMB panel, backlinks ledger). None of these are public-facing content pages, so there's no direct SEO or traffic attribution to assign to them. These are the instruments you're using to measure the site, not the site itself -- they matter, but their value shows up in decision quality over time, not in session counts this week.

The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 11 pages launched in the last 30 days, with 8 URLs ranking in the top 3 and 8 in the top 10. Those are strong ranking numbers. However, GSC impressions attributed to launches are showing zero in this window, which is likely a data-lag issue rather than a real indexing gap -- the same lag affecting the broader GSC window above. The 49 GA4 sessions attributed to launched pages over 30 days is a real number worth watching; as the page count grows and GSC data catches up, that figure should climb.

The backlinks picture is small but meaningful: 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links total, and 5 of those are .edu links. For a local medical practice, 5 edu backlinks is a disproportionately strong signal -- those carry real authority weight. No new backlinks appear to have been earned specifically in this 3-day window, but the existing .edu count is an asset worth actively building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Wait for GSC data to populate before drawing conclusions about content performance.** The zero-impression readings across this entire window are almost certainly lag, not failure. Check back in 3--4 days and pull a fresh GSC window for May 13--19 specifically to see whether the 8 top-ranking pages are generating impression volume.

2. **Investigate the May 19 spike.** Sessions nearly doubled from May 18 to May 19 and landed 85% above the 28-day median. That doesn't happen randomly. Check GA4 for the traffic source breakdown on May 19 specifically -- if a social post, email, or external mention drove it, you want to know what worked so you can repeat it.

3. **Build a second .edu backlink.** You already have 5 edu links from what appears to be a small referral base. Identify which institution(s) are linking to you, find a counterpart at UTSA, UT Health San Antonio, or Trinity University, and pitch a resource link or collaborative mention. The model is already working -- this is the right time to replicate it.

4. **Audit whether the 11 recently launched pages have adequate internal linking.** Eight pages ranking in the top 3 is a strong content result, but 49 attributed sessions over 30 days from 11 pages is a modest conversion of that ranking strength. That gap suggests people may be finding the pages in search results but not being pulled deeper into the site -- a systematic internal-linking pass across those pages is likely to close that gap.

5. **Confirm the sitemap touches for `/author/tmccord845/` and `/author/austintude/` are intentional.** Those author archive pages showed up as sitemap-touched on May 13 and have no apparent content or SEO value. If they're placeholder author pages, they should either be built out with real bios or excluded from the sitemap to avoid diluting crawl budget.
