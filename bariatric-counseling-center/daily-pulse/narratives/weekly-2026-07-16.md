# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-16

### The window in one sentence

The week of July 8-14 opened strong -- sessions ran 60-90% above the 28-day median on the first three days -- then dropped sharply in the second half, leaving the window down 21.8% overall from first half to second half. GSC data is returning zero impressions across all seven days, which is the most urgent unresolved issue in the data right now.

---

### Volume + composition

Over seven days (July 8-14), the site recorded 155 sessions, 137 users, and 423 pageviews. That works out to about 22.1 sessions per day and 19.6 users per day. For context, the 28-day median sits around 19 sessions per day, so the week was above baseline in aggregate -- but just barely, and only because the first three days carried the whole load.

Channel mix was noticeably different from BCC's expected baseline. Direct came in at 41% of sessions (vs. a ~60% baseline), while Organic Search ran at 37% (vs. a ~25% baseline). Referral landed at roughly 10%, Email at 4.5%, and Organic Social at 6.5%. The elevated Organic Search share is worth paying attention to -- it suggests either a ranking improvement or a content piece drawing new search traffic. The below-baseline Direct share could mean less branded or repeat traffic this week, or it could reflect untagged traffic being captured differently by GA4.

There were no GSC impressions or clicks recorded for any day in this window. That is not a normal data state -- it reads as a GSC connection or reporting gap, not as the site actually disappearing from search. The Organic Search sessions in GA4 confirm search traffic was arriving, which makes the flat GSC line a data integrity issue, not a performance issue. This needs to be confirmed and resolved before any search-performance conclusions can be drawn with confidence.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is declining. The first half (July 8-10) totaled 87 sessions and 239 pageviews. The second half (July 11-14) totaled 68 sessions and 184 pageviews -- a drop of 21.8% in sessions across a four-day stretch. That's a meaningful pullback, not statistical noise.

The July 12 Saturday dip (6 sessions, 22 pageviews -- both down roughly 65-70% vs. the 28-day median) is the sharpest single-day drop and typical of weekend behavior for a clinical services site. The days around it (July 11, 13, 14) were also soft by comparison to the early-week highs, which suggests the opening burst was driven by something specific -- a social post, an email going out, or a news mention -- rather than a sustained structural improvement. Average session duration was critically low on July 10, 11, and 12 (around 24-34 seconds vs. a median of 123 seconds), which points to a meaningful portion of those sessions being very low-intent or possibly bot traffic. That's worth investigating before concluding the new-user spike was all real audience.

### Launches + efficacy

Everything that shipped inside this window is a git commit -- daily pulse logs, SEO snapshots, a marketing pulse, and a LinkedIn content log (the week-of-July-13 ideas featuring Todd's psych-eval turnaround and Sara's food noise angle). None of these are public-facing pages, so there's nothing in this window's launches that would directly drive sessions or rankings. The commit activity reflects consistent operational logging, not net-new content production.

The 30-day efficacy rollup tells a more useful story: over the past 30 days, five pages launched and 100 pages were modified across 82 commits. Those pages have collectively generated 217,071 GSC impressions and 943 clicks (attributed). GA4 attributes 640 sessions to those launches. Two pages are currently ranking in the top 10 in search, with zero in the top 3. That top-10 presence without top-3 representation is a specific and actionable gap -- those two pages are close enough to matter but not yet positioned to capture the highest-intent clicks.

Backlinks stand at 4 referring domains, 12 inbound links total, and 5 of those are from .edu domains. That's a meaningful edu-link share for a site this size and suggests some institutional trust signals are in place. No new backlinks appear to have been earned inside this specific window, and no competitor links are present. The referring domain count of 4 is low enough that each new domain added has outsized impact on domain authority -- which means backlink acquisition should be a sustained priority, not a one-off.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose and fix the GSC data gap first.** Seven consecutive days of zero impressions in a week where GA4 is showing real Organic Search traffic is a reporting disconnect. Check the GSC property connection in GA4, confirm the property is verified, and check whether a date-range filter or API quota issue is causing the blank. Until GSC data is reliable, you cannot make informed decisions about rankings or content performance.

2. **Investigate the session-quality drop.** Average session duration of 24-34 seconds on three consecutive days -- against a median of 123 seconds -- suggests a chunk of the July 10-12 traffic was low-quality. Pull a landing-page report for those days to see where that traffic entered. If a single page or referral source is feeding short sessions, that's either a bot pattern or a traffic source that isn't converting to engagement. Knowing which changes the response.

3. **Push the two top-10 ranking pages toward top-3.** The 30-day rollup shows two pages in positions 4-10. Identify those specific pages, review their title tags and meta descriptions for click-through optimization, and check whether internal linking is pointing enough authority their way. A position 4 page with a better snippet could move meaningfully with targeted effort.

4. **Treat the LinkedIn content log as a test worth tracking.** The July 13 commit logged Todd's psych-eval turnaround and Sara's food noise content as sent. If LinkedIn is generating any referral or direct sessions in the coming week, that's a signal the content-to-traffic loop is working. Set a reminder to pull channel data specifically for Organic Social and Referral from LinkedIn for the week of July 13-20 and compare it to this window's 10 Organic Social sessions.

5. **Plan one substantive public-facing page for the coming week.** The window's launches were entirely internal logs. The 30-day rollup shows only 5 pages launched in 30 days, against 100 modifications. That ratio suggests a lot of maintenance activity and less net-new content entering the index. One new page targeting a mid-funnel search intent -- something around bariatric surgery preparation, insurance coverage questions, or post-surgery emotional support -- would give the next rollup something to evaluate against the two existing top-10 pages.
