# Weekly briefing -- 2026-07-20

### The window in one sentence

The week of July 12--18 ended stronger than it started, with sessions accelerating 69% from the first half to the second half of the window, driven by a burst of page modifications across the GLP-1 and eating disorder clusters on July 16. ### Volume + composition

The window produced 129 sessions, 115 users, and 403 pageviews across seven days, with daily averages of 18.4 sessions and 16.4 users. Those averages sit slightly below the 28-day median (roughly 19--20 sessions/day implied by the anomaly baselines), meaning the week was not a breakout in absolute terms. However, the distribution matters: sessions ranged from a low of 6 on July 12 to a high of 29 on July 16, and the week ended with strong mid-week numbers before falling back on Saturday (July 18). GSC impressions and clicks show zero for the entire window -- this is almost certainly a data pipeline or property configuration issue rather than a genuine absence of search visibility, since the 30-day efficacy rollup attributes 385,200 GSC impressions to launches. That discrepancy needs to be resolved before GSC trend analysis is possible here.

Channel composition for the week came in at roughly 43% Direct (56 sessions), 31% Organic Search (40), 18% Referral (23), 4% Email (5), 5% Unassigned/AI/Social (10). Compared to BCC's baseline of ~60% Direct / ~25% Organic / ~10% Referral, the Direct share is notably lower than normal and Referral is running almost double baseline. That elevated Referral number is worth tracking -- it could reflect a specific source sending consistent traffic, and identifying it would tell you whether there is a partnership or listing working quietly in the background. The lower Direct share is not alarming by itself, but if it persists it may suggest that the audience most familiar with BCC (direct navigators) is less active while newer-discovery traffic is picking up. That would actually be a healthy diversification, not a problem.

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The halves comparison is the clearest good-news signal in this window. The first half (July 12--14) generated 48 sessions and 131 pageviews across three days. The second half (July 15--18) generated 81 sessions and 272 pageviews across four days -- but even adjusting for the extra day, the per-day averages moved from 16 to 20.3 sessions and from 43.7 to 68 pageviews. That is a 68.8% session lift and a roughly 56% pageview lift. July 16 was the standout day (29 sessions, 109 pageviews, average session duration of nearly 8 minutes -- more than triple the 28-day median), and July 17 followed with 24 sessions and 82 pageviews, both well above baseline.

The sharp drops on July 12 and July 18 (both weekend-adjacent days, both flagging CRITICAL anomalies at roughly -60% below median) are consistent with BCC's existing weekend pattern and should not be read as trend reversals. The underlying mid-week momentum is accelerating. The session duration spikes on July 15 and July 16 -- 246 seconds and 491 seconds respectively, both well above the 110--112 second median -- suggest that visitors landing on the newly modified pages are spending real time with the content. That is a meaningful engagement signal, even before search rankings materialize.

This is almost certainly a property connection or date-range configuration issue, not a real traffic absence. Until this is resolved, trend analysis for this briefing is partially blind. That ratio suggests Google is surfacing it but the snippet is not earning the click. A tighter, more specific title -- one that names the conditions covered and speaks to the person searching, not just the category label -- is likely to improve CTR without any additional content production.

3. **Build an internal linking pass for the July 16 GLP-1 cluster updates.** Ten pages were modified in a single batch, but unless they link to each other and to the new binge eating disorder post, the cluster cannot accumulate the page-to-page authority signals that support ranking. A structured internal linking audit across /glp-1-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/, /ozempic-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/, /mounjaro-zepbound-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/, and /glp-1-weight-regain-counseling-in-san-antonio-tx/ -- connecting them to each other and to relevant eating disorder content -- would strengthen the cluster's cohesion before Google finishes re-evaluating the updates.

Identifying which domain(s) are sending that traffic -- and whether it is consistent or a single event -- will tell you whether there is a partnership opportunity to formalize, a listing to optimize, or simply a one-time spike to note and move on from.
