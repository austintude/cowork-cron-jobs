# Weekly briefing -- 2026-06-26

### The window in one sentence

The week of June 18--24 was defined by a sharp valley mid-week (June 20--21 were the lowest-traffic days on record vs. the 28-day median) followed by a strong recovery on June 22 that pushed the second half of the window well above baseline. The overall shape is encouraging -- second-half sessions were 74.5% higher than the first half -- but the valley and the GSC data blackout mean caution is warranted before calling this a clean win.

---

### Volume + composition

The 7-day window produced 129 sessions, 104 users, and 569 pageviews across June 18--24, with daily averages of 18.4 sessions and 14.9 users. Those averages look modest, but they are skewed downward by the two-day trough on June 20--21 (just 13 sessions and 24 pageviews combined). Strip those two days out and the remaining five days average roughly 23 sessions -- closer to a healthy baseline. GSC impressions and clicks are reporting at zero for the entire window, which almost certainly reflects a data pipeline issue rather than a genuine collapse in search visibility; that needs to be confirmed before drawing any conclusions about organic search health.

Channel composition this week does not match BCC's typical 60/25/10 baseline. Organic Search led at 52 sessions (40%), Direct came in at 43 (33%), Referral at 21 (16%), and Unassigned added another 23. The Unassigned bucket is worth watching -- 18% of traffic without a source attribution suggests either untagged email sends, QR codes, or improperly configured UTM links from a campaign that went out around June 22 (the same day the session spike occurred). Organic Search leading the channel mix over Direct is unusual for BCC and is a positive signal if it holds, but the GSC blackout makes it impossible to confirm which queries or pages are driving it right now.

---

### Trend (first half vs second half)

The window is accelerating on paper -- 74.5% more sessions in the second half (82) versus the first half (47). But the shape of that acceleration matters. The first half includes the June 20--21 floor, which artificially compresses the baseline. June 22 alone accounted for 40 sessions and 223 pageviews, both well above the 28-day median, and session duration on June 22--24 was elevated (ranging from roughly 2x to 3.5x the median). That pattern -- high session count plus long dwell time -- suggests the people arriving in the second half of the week were genuinely engaged, not just bouncing.

The concern is that the recovery may be event-driven (a single send, a share, one referral source) rather than structural organic growth. Until GSC data is restored and the Unassigned channel is identified, it is worth treating the second-half lift as a promising but unverified signal rather than confirmed momentum.

### Launches + efficacy

The biggest production event in this window was a batch of 10 page modifications pushed on June 24, all submitted to GSC the following day. These cover a range of service clusters: eating disorder treatment (/eating-disorder-treatment-in-san-antonio-tx/), eating disorder therapist and counseling pages, semaglutide and Ozempic behavioral support, bariatric therapy and surgery psychological evaluation, online and virtual therapy options for Texas, weight management counseling, and holistic counseling. This is a meaningful cluster push -- it touches multiple high-intent query types and two emerging GLP-1 medication support terms (semaglutide, Ozempic) that are seeing sustained search demand nationally.

The problem is timing. All 10 pages were submitted to GSC on June 25, meaning they have had one day to be crawled. None show impressions or rankings yet, and only the eating disorder treatment page shows any GA4 activity (1 session, likely a direct visit). That is completely normal for pages this new. The 30-day efficacy rollup shows 46,520 GSC impressions and 96 clicks attributed across 12 launched pages, with 1 page ranking in the top 10 -- which tells us older launches are beginning to find footing in search, even if no single page is yet dominating. The batch from June 24 needs 2--4 more weeks before any efficacy judgment is possible.

There are no new backlinks recorded in the window itself, but the overall profile (4 referring domains, 12 inbound links, 5 of those from .edu domains) is a meaningful baseline for a practice this size. The .edu link concentration is worth protecting and building on.

### What this means + what to do next

1. **Diagnose the GSC data blackout before anything else.** Seven consecutive days of zero impressions in GSC is almost certainly a reporting or authentication issue, not a ranking collapse. Confirm the GSC property is still verified, check that the service account credentials are active, and manually spot-check two or three page URLs directly in GSC's URL Inspection tool. Restoring this data feed is the highest-priority infrastructure task next week -- every other SEO decision depends on it.

2. **Identify the June 22 traffic source.** The spike that day (40 sessions, 223 pageviews, 90.5% above the 28-day median, plus a +344% YoY comp) almost certainly had a specific cause -- an email send, a social post, a referral link someone clicked. The Unassigned channel volume (23 sessions for the week) is a strong clue. Trace the source, add proper UTM parameters to that channel going forward, and if it was an email, note the send time and content type as a repeatable model.

3. **Set a 14-day review checkpoint for the June 24 cluster.** The 10 modified pages -- especially the eating disorder counseling cluster and the GLP-1 support pages -- are the strategic bets of the month. Schedule a structured GSC impression and ranking check for the week of July 7--11, when Google will have had enough time to index and evaluate them. At that point you will know which pages picked up impressions quickly and which need title tag or meta description refinement.

4. **Build internal links to the GLP-1 pages now.** The semaglutide and Ozempic behavioral support pages (/semaglutide-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/ and /ozempic-behavioral-support-in-san-antonio-tx/) are targeting query types that are actively growing. They will rank faster if other established pages on the site link to them. Identify 3--5 existing pages with topical overlap (bariatric therapy, weight management counseling) and add contextual links pointing to the GLP-1 pages this week.

5. **Protect and expand the .edu backlink position.** Five .edu links out of 12 total is a strong ratio and likely a meaningful trust signal for a health services site. Identify which institutions those links come from and look for a second contact or a related resource page at each domain where a second link might be appropriate. One new .edu link this quarter would meaningfully strengthen the domain's authority baseline.
